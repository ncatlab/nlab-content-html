What this page is for

This Sandbox is specifically for mucking about with SVGs. The point is that these pictures can get rather large (particularly if automatically generated - does anyone write their own?). A Sandbox, such as this one is for trying things out. Usually those are little things, such as getting the correct syntax for something, so if the Sandbox gets really big and takes ages to load and resubmit, it gets difficult to try out lots of little changes. One SVG, such as my original one, can easily swamp the ‚Äòbox. However, as I‚Äôve found out, getting SVGs to work in Instiki can take a little tweaking so this ‚Äòbox is for that.

One shouldn‚Äôt leave an SVG here for long. I don‚Äôt think that there‚Äôs much to be learnt from looking through someone else‚Äôs SVG code (unlike in the original Sandbox where it can be useful to scan through and see how something is done). Rather, you should muck about with your SVG, get it right, and then transfer it across to the requisite page.

A reasonable rule would be that the author should delete it when they are finished with it, and that the maximum lifespan of a picture here is, say, 1 week. To make this easier to police, please timestamp your SVGs.

Obviously as we play with these then we‚Äôll learn various strategies. These should be added to the FAQ or HowTo pages as appropriate. Maybe we should have a list of SVGs in the n-Lab so that people can find examples of what can be done and how to do it, this could be commented with what has had to be done to get it to work in Instiki. If someone has a particularly nice example, or one they‚Äôre particularly proud of, but doesn‚Äôt really fit into an ‚Äúhonest‚Äù page on the n-Lab then may I suggest they put it on their userpage (I‚Äôll probably do that with the ‚Äúmap of manifolds‚Äù one since it doesn‚Äôt really fit anywhere else).

Been a while since I thought about getting a good graphics engine here in inStiki, but these pictures have got me excited again. Right now I am thinking that the following procedure could work well:

  • You copy and paste this svg text into the nLab page you are editing.

  • Now you click ‚ÄúSubmit‚Äù as usual.

  • Here‚Äôs the trick: the next time someone clicks ‚ÄúEdit page‚Äù in the nLab, the Instiki software is intelligent and displays the SVG text as a javascript ‚Äúexpand box‚Äù.

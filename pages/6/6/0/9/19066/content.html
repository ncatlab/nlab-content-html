Idea

The Grothendieck construction may be lifted from categories to model categories, where it serves as a presentation for the ∞ \infty -Grothendieck construction.

Definition

Let ℳ\mathcal{M} be a model category and

(1)ℳ ⟶ ModCat X ↦ F(X) ↓ f f *↑↓ f ! Y ↦ F(Y) \array{ \mathcal{M} &\longrightarrow& Mod Cat \\ X &\mapsto& F(X) \\ \Big\downarrow\mathrlap{{}^{f}} && \mathllap{^{f^\ast}}\Big\uparrow \Big\downarrow\mathrlap{{}^{f_!}} \\ Y &\mapsto& F(Y) }

a pseudofunctor, where ModelCatModelCat is the 2-category of model categories, Quillen adjunctions (pointing in the direction of their left adjoints f !f_!), and mate-pairs of natural isomorphisms.

We say that:

Definition

FF is relative functor if for any weak equivalence ff in MM, the associated Quillen adjunction f !⊣f *f_! \dashv f^* is a Quillen equivalence.

Definition

FF is “proper”, if when f:A→Bf \colon A\to B is an acyclic cofibration (resp. an acyclic fibration) in ℳ\mathcal{M} then f !f_! (resp. f *f^*) preserves all weak equivalences.

Definition

(integral model structure)
Given a pseudofunctor as in (1), we say that a morphism

(f,ϕ):(X,A)⟶(Y,B) (f,\phi) \;\colon\; (X,A) \longrightarrow (Y,B)

in its Grothendieck construction ∫ X∈ℳF(X)\int_{X \in \mathcal{M}} F(X) (where f:X→Yf \colon X\to Y in ℳ\mathcal{M} and ϕ:f !(A)→B\phi \colon f_!(A) \to B in F(Y)F(Y)) is:

  • an integral equivalence iff

    1. f:X→Yf \colon X \to Y is a weak equivalence in ℳ\mathcal{M}

    2. f !(QA)→f !(A)→ϕBf_!(Q A) \to f_!(A) \xrightarrow{\phi} B is a weak equivalence in F(Y)F(Y), where QQ is a cofibrant replacement.

      (Since f !⊣f *f_!\dashv f^* is a Quillen equivalence by relativeness, this is equivalent to the adjunct condition that A→ϕ˜f *(B)→f *(RB)A \xrightarrow{\widetilde{\phi}} f^*(B) \to f^*(R B) is a weak equivalence in F(X)F(X).)

  • an integral cofibration iff

    1. ff is a cofibration in ℳ\mathcal{M}

    2. ϕ:f !(A)→B\phi \colon f_!(A) \to B is a cofibration in F(Y)F(Y).

  • an integral fibration iff

    1. ff is a fibration in ℳ\mathcal{M}

    2. the adjunct ϕ˜:A→f *(B)\widetilde{\phi} \colon A \to f^*(B) is a fibration in F(X)F(X).

Proposition

If the pseudofunctor (1) is relative (Def. ) and proper (Def. ) then the classes of maps in Def. make ∫ X∈ℳF(X)\int_{X \in \mathcal{M}} F(X) a model category.

This is Harpaz & Prasma (2015), Theorem 3.0.12.

Properties

Proposition

Given a proper relative F:ℳ→ModelCatF \colon \mathcal{M} \to ModelCat, we can compose with the underlying ( ∞ , 1 ) (\infty,1) -functor Ho:ModelCat→QCatHo \colon ModelCat \to QCat with values in (say) quasicategories.
Since FF is relative, this map takes weak equivalences in ℳ\mathcal{M} to equivalences of quasicategories, so it induces a functor of quasicategories Ho(M)→Ho(QCat)=(∞,1)CatHo(M) \to Ho(QCat) = (\infty,1)Cat. The (∞,1)-Grothendieck construction of this functor is then equivalent, over Ho(M)Ho(M), to the underlying (∞,1)(\infty,1)-category of the Grothendieck-construction model structure on ∫F\int F

(Harpaz & Prasma (2015), Proposition 3.1.2)

First to prove that the pseudofunctor is relative, here is a lazy argument (to be improved): By general results in Higher Topos Theory, the given pseudofunctor presents the ∞ \infty -functor

References

The first model category version of the Grothendieck construction was given in

  • Agustí Roig, Model category structures in bifibred categories, Journal of Pure and Applied Algebra 95 2 (1994) 203–223 [doi:10.1016/0022-4049(94)90074-4]

This article (Roig 94) had a mistake, which was fixed in

  • Alexandru E. Stanculescu, Bifibrations and weak factorisation systems, Applied Categorical Structures 20 1 (2012) 19–30 [doi:10.1007/s10485-009-9214-3]

The construction was then generalized in

  • Yonatan Harpaz, Matan Prasma, The Grothendieck construction for model categories, Advances in Mathematics 281 (2015) 1306-1363 [arXiv:1404.1852, 10.1016/j.aim.2015.03.031]

Another approach is found in

  • Pierre Cagne, Paul-André Melliès, On bifibrations of model categories, (arXiv:1709.10484)

For the special case of pseudofunctors with values in groupoids, a model category version of the Grothendieck construction was discussed in

  • Sharon Hollander, A homotopy theory for stacks (arXiv:math.AT/0110247).

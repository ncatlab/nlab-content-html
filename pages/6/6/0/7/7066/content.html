This entry is about polarization of phase spaces (or of any symplectic manifold) into canonical ‚Äúposition‚Äù coordinates and canonical momenta. Different concepts of a similar name include the polarization identity (such as in an inner product space or a Jordan algebra) or wave polarization (such as polarized light). On the other hand, the concept of polarized algebraic variety is closely related.

Idea

For a symplectic manifold (X,œâ)(X, \omega) regarded as the phase space of a physical system, a choice of polarization is, locally, a choice of decomposition of the coordinates on XX into ‚Äúcanonical coordinates‚Äù and ‚Äúcanonical momenta‚Äù.

The archetypical example is that where X=T *Œ£X = T^* \Sigma is the cotangent bundle of a manifold Œ£\Sigma. In this case the canonical canonical coordinates are those parameterizing Œ£\Sigma itself, while the canonical canonical momenta are coordinates on each fiber of the cotangent bundle.

But for general symplectic manifolds there is no such canonical choice of coordinates and momenta. Moreover, in general there is not even a global notion of canonical momenta. Instead, a choice of (real) polarization is a foliation of phase space by Lagrangian submanifolds and then

  • the ‚Äúcanonical coordinates‚Äù are coordinates on the corresponding leaf space (parameterizing the leaves);

  • the ‚Äúcanonical momenta‚Äù are coordinates along each leaf. If there is no typical leaf then this is not a globally defined notion, only the polarization itself is.

Related concepts

Quantum states and wave functions

Upon (geometric) quantization of the physical system described by the symplectic manifold (X,œâ)(X, \omega) a quantum state is supposed to be a function on XX ‚Äì or rather a section of a prequantum line bundle which is a ‚Äúwave-function that only depends on the canonical coordinates‚Äù, not on the canonical momenta. In terms of polarizations this is formalized by saying that a quantum state is a section which is covariant constant along the leaves of the polarization (along the ‚Äúmomentum direction‚Äù).

Bohr-Sommerfeld leaves

After a choice of prequantum line bundle ‚àá\nabla lifting œâ\omega, a Bohr-Sommerfeld leaf of a (real) polarization is a leaf on which the prequantum line bundle is not just flat, but also trivializable as a circle bundle with connection.

Liouville integrability

If a polarization on an 2n2n-dimensional symplectic manifold is generated from nn Hamiltonian vector fields whose Hamiltonians commute with each other under the Poisson bracket (and one of them is regarded as that generating time evolution of a mechanical system) then one speaks of a Liouville integrable system.

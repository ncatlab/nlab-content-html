Idea

The notion of monomorphism in an (âˆ,1)(\infty,1)-category is the generalization of the notion of monomorphism from category theory to (âˆ,1)-category theory. It is the special case of the notion of n-monomorphisms for n=1n = 1. In an (âˆ,1)-topos every morphism factors by an effective epimorphism (1-epimorphism) followed by a monomorphism through its 1-image.

The dual concept is that of an epimorphism in an (âˆ,1)-category.

There is also the concept regular monomorphism in an (âˆ,1)-category, but beware that this need not be a special case of the definition given here.

There are also a notions of (homotopy) monomorphism in model categories and derivators.

Theorem and Definition

There is a unique ring-structure on the ℤ\mathbb{Z}-group WW such that either of the two following conditions is satisfied:

  1. Each Φ n:W→O ℤ\Phi_n:W\to O_\mathbb{Z} is a ring homomorphism.

  2. ι(ab)=ι(a)ι(b)\iota(ab)=\iota(a)\iota(b), a,b∈R∈M ℤa,b\in R\in M_\mathbb{Z}.

The ℤ\mathbb{Z}-ring WW is called Witt-Ring, each W nW_n is a quotient ring of WW. The canonical morphisms R n:W→W nR_n:W\to W_n and R:W n+1→W nR:W_{n+1}\to W_n are ring homomorphisms (but not TT).

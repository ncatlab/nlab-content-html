Idea

A moduli stack of elliptic curves is, as the name suggests, a moduli stack which classifies elliptic curves. Hence it is a stack ‚Ñ≥ ell\mathcal{M}_{ell} such that for XX any other suitable space, the groupoid of maps X‚Üí‚Ñ≥ ellX \to \mathcal{M}_{ell} and homotopies between them is equivalent to that of XX-parameterized elliptic curves with equivalences between these. (There are some variants of ‚Ñ≥ ell\mathcal{M}_{ell} corresponding to the choice of which singularities and degeneracies of elliptic curves are taken into account.)

This is formalized in algebraic geometry, hence XX here is a scheme over the integers in general. The moduli stack always has a tautological construction as a ‚Äúsheaf of groupoids‚Äù (a stack, whence the name) over the site of affine schemes, given by sending any Spec(R)Spec(R) to the groupoid of suitable elliptic curves over Spec(R)Spec(R). For concrete computations it typically helps to know that the moduli stack of elliptic curbes is represented by a geometric stack, dually given by a Hopf algebroid.

The moduli stack ‚Ñ≥ ell\mathcal{M}_{ell} has a compactification ‚Ñ≥ ell¬Ø\mathcal{M}_{\overline{ell}} obtained by adding the nodal cubic curve, and often (but not always) this compactified version is the default meaning of ‚Äúmoduli stack of elliptic curves‚Äù. Adding also the cuspidal cubic curve and hence all cubic curves produces the full moduli stack ‚Ñ≥ cub\mathcal{M}_{cub} of cubic curves, inside which ‚Ñ≥ ell\mathcal{M}_{ell} sits as the locus of non-singular curves.

Since an elliptic curve is a genus-1 algebraic curve with a marked point (the neutral element of the group structure), ‚Ñ≥ ell\mathcal{M}_{ell} is equivalently the moduli stack of algebraic curves for genus g=1g = 1 with n=1n = 1 punctures, and as such is often equivalently written

‚Ñ≥ 1,1=‚Ñ≥ ell. \mathcal{M}_{1,1} = \mathcal{M}_{ell} \,.

Description over the complex numbers

An elliptic curve E‚ÜíSpec(‚ÑÇ)E \to Spec(\mathbb{C}) over the complex numbers is determined, up to non-canonical isomorphism, by its j-invariant

j(E)‚àà‚ÑÇ. j(E) \in \mathbb{C} \,.

Here every complex number appears as a value, and therefore the moduli space of elliptic cuves a priori is not compact.

A compactification of the moduli space is obtained by including also elliptic curves with nodal singularity.

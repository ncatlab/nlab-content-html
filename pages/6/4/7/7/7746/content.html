Idea

Factorization homology is a notion of homology theory for framed nn-dimensional manifolds with coefficients in En-algebras, due to (Francis). It is similar in spirit to factorization algebras, blob homology and topological chiral homology. In fact the definition of factorization homology turns out to be equivalent to that of topological chiral topology (Francis b).

Definition

Write Mfd n ‚àêMfd_n^{\coprod} for the category of manifolds with embeddings as morphisms. This is naturally a topological category, hence regard it as an (infinity,1)-category. Regard it furthermore as a symmetric monoidal (‚àû,1)-category with tensor product given by disjoint union.

For kk a field, write Mod kMod_k for the symmetric monoidal (‚àû,1)-category of kk-chain complexes.

Let H(Mfd n ‚àê,Mod k)H(Mfd_n^{\coprod}, Mod_k) be the sub-(‚àû,1)-category of those monoidal (‚àû,1)-functors F:Mfd n op‚ÜíMod kF : Mfd_n^{op} \to Mod_k which are ‚Äúcosheaves‚Äù in that for any decomposition of a manifold XX into submanifolds X‚Ä≤X' and X‚Ä≥X'' with overlap OO, we have an equivalence

F(X)‚âÉF(X‚Ä≤)‚äó F(O)F(X‚Ä≥). F(X) \simeq F(X') \otimes_{F(O)}F(X'') \,.

Next, let Disk n‚äÇMfd nDisk_n \subset Mfd_n be the full sub-(‚àû,1)-category on those manifolds which are finite disjoint unions of the Cartesian space ‚Ñù n\mathbb{R}^n.

Restriction along this inclusion gives an (‚àû,1)-functor

‚à´ XA=ùîº X‚äó Disk nA \int^X A = \mathbb{E}_X \otimes_{Disk_n} A

This is equivalent to topological chiral homology, to be thought of as a topological version of chiral algebras. A version with values in homotopy types instead of chain complexes was given by Salvatore and Graeme Segal.

Properties

Relation to cobordism hypothsis

From a functor F‚ààH(Mfd n,Mod k)F \in H(Mfd_n, Mod_k) we get an extended TQFT with values in kk-linear (‚àû,n)(\infty,n)-categories

From a Disk nDisk_n-algebra AA we obtain the corresponding delooping BA‚àà(Cat n(k) dualizable) O(n)\mathbf{B}A \in (Cat_n(k)_{dualizable})^{O(n)} which is a kk-linear (infinity,n)-category that is a fully dualizable object. The cobordism hypothesis identifies this with cobordism representations, and the claim is that this identification is compatible factorization homology.

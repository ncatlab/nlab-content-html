Idea

In equivariant stable homotopy theory over a compact Lie group GG, a GG-universe is a GG-representation that contains ‚Äúall‚Äù representations of GG of sorts.

This is used in one definition of G-spectra via looping and delooping by representation spheres.

Definition

A G-universe in this context is (e.g. Greenlees-May, p. 10) an infinite dimensional real inner product space equipped with a linear GG-action that is the direct sum of countably many copies of a given set of (finite dimensional) representations of GG, at least containing the trivial representation on ‚Ñù\mathbb{R} (so that UU contains at least a copy of ‚Ñù ‚àû\mathbb{R}^\infty).

Idea

What is called global equivariant homotopy theory is a variant of equivariant cohomology in homotopy theory where pointed topological spaces/homotopy types are equipped with GG-infinity-actions ‚Äúfor all compact Lie groups GG at once‚Äù.

Sometimes this is referred to just as ‚Äúglobal homotopy theory‚Äù, leaving the equivariance implicit. There is also a stable version involving spectra equipped with infinity-actions, see at global equivariant stable homotopy theory.

More precisely, the global equivariant homotopy category is the (‚àû,1)-category (or else its homotopy category) of (‚àû,1)-presheaves PSh ‚àû(Orb)PSh_\infty(Orb) on the global orbit category OrbOrb (Henriques-Gepner 07, section 1.3), regarded as an (‚àû,1)-category.

Here OrbOrb has as objects compact Lie groups and the (‚àû,1)-categorical hom-spaces Orb(G,H)‚âîŒ†[BG,BH]Orb(G,H) \coloneqq \Pi [\mathbf{B}G, \mathbf{B}H] , where on the right we have the fundamental (‚àû,1)-groupoid of the topological groupoid of group homomorphisms and conjugations.

Relation to topological stacks and orbispaces

under construction

By the main theorem of (Henriques-Gepner 07) the (‚àû,1)-presheaves on the global orbit category are equivalently ‚Äúcellular‚Äù topological stacks/topological groupoids (‚Äúorbispaces‚Äù), we might write this as

(As such the global equivariant homotopy theory should be similar to ETop‚àûGrpd. Observe that this is a cohesive (‚àû,1)-topos with Œ†\Pi such that it sends a topological action groupoid of a topological group GG acting on a topological space XX to the homotopy quotient Œ†(X)//Œ†(G)\Pi(X)//\Pi(G).)

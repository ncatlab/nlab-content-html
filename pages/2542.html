Definition

Definition

A lined topos (ùíØ,R)(\mathcal{T}, R) is

  • a ringed topos (ùíØ,k)(\mathcal{T}, k)

    (usually with the internal ring object (k,+,‚ãÖ)(k,+,\cdot) assumed to be commutative)

  • and equipped with a choice (R,+,‚ãÖ)(R,+,\cdot) of internal commutative algebra object (R,+,‚ãÖ)(R,+,\cdot) over kk ‚Äì the line object.

Variations

  • A smooth topos is a lined topos where the line is required to be a smooth differentiable space with infinitesimal subspaces in a certain way. This is the basic type of object studied in synthetic differential geometry.

  • A super smooth topos is a lined topos that is a smooth topos and in which the kk-algebra structure on RR is refined to that of a kk-superalgebra.

Idea

There are several notions of homomorphisms between pairs of adjoint functors, notably the notion of pairs of conjugate natural transformations (Def. below).

Relation to bifibrations

Proposition

Under the Grothendieck construction, the Grothendieck fibrations which arise from pseudofunctors ‚Ñ¨‚ü∂Cat\mathcal{B} \longrightarrow Cat that factor through Cat adj Cat_{adj} (5) are equivalently the bifibrations.

Proof

A Grothendieck construction on a pseudofunctor yielding a bifibration is fairly immediately equivalent to all base change functors having an adjoint on the respective side (e.g. Jacobs (1998), Lem. 9.1.2). By the fact that Cat adj‚ÜíCatCat_{adj} \to Cat is a locally full sub-2-category (Prop. ) this already means that the given pseudofunctor factors through Cat adjCat_adj, and essentially uniquely so.

See also Harpaz & Prasma (2015), Prop. 2.2.1.

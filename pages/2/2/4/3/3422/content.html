Idea

The (‚àû,1)(\infty,1)-Grothendieck construction is the generalization of the Grothendieck construction from category theory to (‚àû,1)-category theory.

Recall that the 1-category-theoretic Grothendieck construction establishes equivalences of categories,

Fib(C)‚âÉ2Func(C op,Cat)AAAAandAAAAFib Grpd(C)‚âÉ2Func(C op,Grpd) Fib(C) \simeq 2Func(C^{op}, Cat) {\phantom{AAAA}} and {\phantom{AAAA}} Fib_{Grpd}(C) \simeq 2Func(C^{op}, Grpd)

between (a) fibered categories (or just categories fibered in groupoids) and (b) pseudofunctors to Cat (or just to Grpd).

Analogously, the full Grothendieck construction for (‚àû,1)-categories constitutes an equivalence of (‚àû,1)-categories

CartFib(C)‚âÉ‚àûFunc(C op,(‚àû,1)Cat) CartFib(C) \,\simeq\, \infty Func\big( C^{op}, (\infty,1)Cat \big)

between (a) Cartesian fibrations of quasi-categories and (b) indexed (‚àû,1)-categories, that is, (‚àû,1)-functors to (‚àû,1)Cat;

while its restriction to the Grothendieck construction for ‚àû-groupoids constitutes an equivalence of (‚àû,1)-categories

RFib(C)‚âÉ‚àûFunc(C op,‚àûGrpd) RFib(C) \,\simeq\, \infty Func\big(C^{op}, \infty Grpd\big)

between right fibrations of quasi-categories and (‚àû,1)-functors to ‚àûGrpd.

This correspondence may be modeled:

  • in the case of ‚àû\infty-groupoids by a Quillen equivalence between the model structure for right fibrations and the projective global model structure on simplicial presheaves,

  • in the case of (‚àû,1)(\infty,1)-categories by a Quillen equivalence between the model structure for Cartesian fibrations and the global model structure on functors with values in the model structure on marked simplicial sets.

For fibrations in ‚àû\infty-groupoids

The generalization of a category fibered in groupoids to quasi-category theory is a right fibration of quasi-categories.

By the fact that there is the classical model structure on simplicial sets we have that every morphism of ‚àû\infty-groupoids X‚ÜíCX \to C factors as

For general fibered (‚àû,1)(\infty,1)-categories

The generalization of a fibered category to quasi-category theory is a Cartesian fibration of quasi-categories.

Theorem

((‚àû,1)(\infty,1)-Grothendieck construction)

Let CC be a small (‚àû,1)-category. There is an equivalence

Cart(C)‚âÉFunc(C op,(‚àû,1)Cat) Cart(C) \simeq Func(C^{op}, (\infty,1) Cat)

  • and on the right the (‚àû,1)-category of (‚àû,1)-functors from C opC^{op} to the (‚àû,1)-category of (‚àû,1)-categories.

Furthermore, this is equivalence is natural for CC, where Cart(‚àí)Cart(-) acts by taking pullbacks and Func(‚àí op,(‚àû,1)Cat)Func(-^{op}, (\infty,1) Cat) acts by composition.

A reference for the naturality in small CC is corollary A.32 of Gepner-Haugseng-Nikolaus 15. The dual statement is made in remark 1.13 of Mazel-Gee.

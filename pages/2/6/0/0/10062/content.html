Any pretriangulated dg-category ùíû\mathcal{C} presents a stable (infinity,1)-category. A plain dg-category only presents a spectrally enriched (infinity,1)-category. One way to construct this is to apply the Dold-Kan correspondence on each hom-object to produce a fibrant sSet-enriched category and then, if desired, form the homotopy coherent nerve of that to obtain a quasi-category.

On the other hand, the dg-nerve of ùíû\mathcal{C} is a more direct construction that directly sends the dg-category to a simplicial set which is the quasi-category incarnation of the corresponding stable (‚àû,1)-category.

To the extent that one may think of ùíû\mathcal{C} as analogous to a category of chain complexes, the dg-nerve may be thought of producing the simplicial set whose kk-simplices are the local systems on Œî k\Delta^k with coefficients in ùíû\mathcal{C} (flat ‚àû-connections with coefficients in ùíû\mathcal{C}). The formula is just as for Lie integration of L-infinity algebroids.

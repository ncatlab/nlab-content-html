Idea

Let LL be a 01-bounded semilattice, that is, a semilattice with an absorbing element. Phoa’s principle states that every endofunction f:L→Lf:L \to L is monotonic and the precomposition function h↦h∘i:L L→L 𝟚h \mapsto h \circ i:L^L \to L^\mathbb{2} by the embedding i:𝟚↪Li:\mathbb{2} \hookrightarrow L of the booleans into LL is an embedding.

If LL is a distributive lattice, then Phoa’s principle is equivalent to the linear interpolation condition that for all endofunctions f:L→Lf:L \to L and elements x∈Lx \in L:

f(x)=f(⊤)∧(x∨f(⊥))f(x) = f(\top) \wedge (x \vee f(\bot))

In Pos the category of posets and monotonic functions, Phoa’s principle holds for the boolean domain 𝟚\mathbb{2}. In synthetic Stone duality, Phoa’s principle holds for the type of open propositions Open\mathrm{Open}. In synthetic domain theory, Phoa’s principle holds for a dominance Ω\Omega.

Phoa’s principle is named after Wesley Phoa.

Idea

A dualizable object in a symmetric monoidal (‚àû,n)-category ùíû\mathcal{C} is called fully dualizable if the structure maps of the duality unit and counit each themselves have adjoints, which have adjoints, and so on, up to level (n‚àí1)(n-1).

Properties

By the cobordism hypothesis-theorem, symmetric monoidal (‚àû,n)-functors out of the (‚àû,n)-category of cobordisms are characterized by their value on the point, which is a fully dualizable object.

Examples

  • In the symmetric monoidal category Vect of vector spaces (over some field), the fully dualizable objects are the finite-dimensional vector spaces.

  • In the 2-category of associative algebras with bimodules between them as morphisms, over a commutative ring, fully dualizable objects are separable algebras which are projective modules over the base ring (these conditions are given in SchommerPries 11, Definition 3.70 and attributed to Lurie‚Äôs paper cited below).

  • In the symmetric monoidal 3-category of monoidal categories and bimodule categories between them, the fully dualizable objects are (or at least contain) the fusion categories. (DSPS 13).

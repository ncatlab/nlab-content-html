Idea

A generalized graph in the sense of HRY is a generalization of the notion of a pseudograph given at graph (see in particular Definition in terms action on a set of half edges). By adding structure like directions for the edges, we can define a wheeled graph which is a generalization of a directed pseudograph. What differentiates a generalized graph from a pseudograph and a wheeled graph from a directed pseudograph is the notion of an ‚Äúexceptional cell,‚Äù which should be thought of as a set of half edges which have no vertices.

A generalized graph can be visualized as a set of vertices with half edges attached to them, a rule for attaching half edges to glue the vertices together, and potentially some half edges (flags) that are attached to only one or zero vertices. For instance, a particularly simple generalized graph is one with no vertices and one edge.

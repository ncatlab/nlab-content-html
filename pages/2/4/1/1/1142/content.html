Idea

As every topos, a category of presheaves is cartesian closed monoidal.

The first statement is a special case of the general fact that limits of presheaves are computed objectwise.

For the second statement, first assume that [X,Y][\mathbf{X}, \mathbf{Y}] does exist. Then by the hom-adjunction isomorphism we have for any other presheaf Z\mathbf{Z} a natural isomorphism of the form

(1)Hom [ùíû op,Set](Z,[X,Y])‚âÉHom [ùíû op,Set](Z√óX,Y). Hom_{[\mathcal{C}^{op}, Set]}(\mathbf{Z}, [\mathbf{X},\mathbf{Y}]) \;\simeq\; Hom_{[\mathcal{C}^{op}, Set]}(\mathbf{Z} \times \mathbf{X}, \mathbf{Y}) \,.

This holds in particular for Z=y(c)\mathbf{Z} = y(c) a representable functor (i.e. in the essential image of the Yoneda embedding) and so the Yoneda lemma implies that if it exists, then [X,Y][\mathbf{X}, \mathbf{Y}] must have the claimed form:

Hence it remains to show that this formula does make (1) hold generally.

Definition in terms of homs of direct images

Often another, equivalent, expression is used to express the internal hom of presheaves:

Let XX be a pre-site with underlying category S XS_X. Recall from the discussion at site that just means that we have a category S XS_X on which we consider presheaves F‚ààPSh(S X):=[S X op,Set]F \in PSh(S_X) := [S_X^{op}, Set], but that it suggests that

  • to each object U‚ààPSh(X)U \in PSh(X) and in particular to each U‚ààS X‚Ü™PSh(X)U \in S_X \hookrightarrow PSh(X) there is naturally associated the pre-site UU with underlying category the comma category S U=(Y/Y(U))S_U = (Y/Y(U));

  • that the canonical forgetful functor j U‚ÜíX t:S U‚ÜíS Xj^t_{U \to X} : S_U \to S_X, which can be thought of as a morphism of pre-sites j U‚ÜíX:X‚ÜíUj_{U \to X} : X \to U induces the direct image functor (j U‚ÜíX) *:PSh(X)‚ÜíPSh(U)(j_{U \to X})_* : PSh(X) \to PSh(U) which we write F‚Ü¶F| UF \mapsto F|_U.

Then in these terms the above internal hom for presheaves

hom:PSh(X) op√óPSh(X)‚ÜíPSh(X) hom : PSh(X)^{op} \times PSh(X) \to PSh(X)

is expressed for all F,G‚ààPSh(X)F,G \in PSh(X) by

hom(F,G)=U‚Ü¶Hom PSh(U)(F| U,G| U). hom(F,G) = U \mapsto Hom_{PSh(U)}(F|_U, G|_U) \,.

Relation of the two definitions

To see the equivalence of the two definitions, notice

  • that by the Yoneda lemma we have that S US_U is simply the over category S U=S X/US_U = S_X/U;
  • by the general properties of functors and comma categories there is an equivalence PSh(S X/U)‚âÉPSh(S X)/y(U)PSh(S_X/U) \simeq PSh(S_X)/y(U);
  • which identifies the functor (‚àí)| U:PSh(S X)‚ÜíPSh(S U)(-)|_U : PSh(S_X) \to PSh(S_U) with the functor ((‚àí)√óy(U)‚Üíp 2y(U)):PSh(S X)‚ÜíPSh(S X)/y(U)((-)\times y(U) \stackrel{p_2}{\to} y(U)) : PSh(S_X) \to PSh(S_X)/y(U);
  • and that Hom PSh(S X)/y(U)(y(U)√óF,y(U)√óG)‚âÉHom PSh(S X)(y(U)√óF,G)Hom_{PSh(S_X)/y(U)}(y(U) \times F, y(U) \times G) \simeq Hom_{PSh(S_X)}(y(U) \times F, G).

Presheaves over a monoidal category

It is worth noting that in the case where XX is itself a monoidal category (X,‚äó,I)(X, \otimes, I), Psh(X)Psh(X) is equipped with another (bi)closed monoidal structure given by the Day convolution product and its componentwise right adjoints. by the following end formulas:

F\H=V‚Ü¶‚à´ U‚ààXF(U)‚ÜíH(U‚äóV)F \backslash H = V \mapsto \int_{U\in X} F(U) \to H(U\otimes V)

In the case where the monoidal structure on XX is cartesian, the induced closed monoidal structure on Psh(X)Psh(X) coincides with the cartesian closed structure described in the previous sections.

Write Conf(‚Ñù n)Conf(\mathbb{R}^n) for the ‚Äúplain‚Äù configuration space of unlabeled and unordered points in the Cartesian space (Euclidean space) ‚Ñù n\mathbb{R}^n, hence for the evident topological space of finite subsets of ‚Ñù n \mathbb{R}^n .

This space naturally carries the structure of a partial abelian topological monoid, where the addition of a pair of configurations is defined if they are disjoint, in which case it is given by their (necessarily disjoint) union [Segal 1973, p. 215].

This motivates asking for more explicit descriptions of the group completed configuration space of points, itself as a configuration space, now of objects somewhat richer than plain points.

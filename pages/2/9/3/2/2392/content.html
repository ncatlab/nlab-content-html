Idea

The notion of (âˆ,1)(\infty,1)-operad is to that of (âˆ,1)-category as operad is to category.

So, roughly, an (âˆ,1)(\infty,1)-operad is an algebraic structure that has for each given type of input and one type of output an âˆ-groupoid of operations that take these inputs to that output.

There is a fairly evident notion of an âˆ-algebra over an (âˆ,1)-operad. Examples include

  • E-âˆ algebras

  • L-âˆ algebras;

  • A-âˆ algebras.

(âˆ,1)(\infty,1)-Operads form an (âˆ,2)-category (âˆ,1)Operad.

The second models the (âˆ,1)-category version of a category of operators of an operad.

In terms of dendroidal sets

Here simplicial sets are generalized to dendroidal sets. The theory of (âˆ,1)(\infty,1)-operads is then formulated in terms of dendroidal sets in close analogy to how the theory of (âˆ,1)-categories is formulated in terms of simplicial sets.

There is a model structure on dendroidal sets whose fibrant objects are the quasi-operads in direct analogy to the notion of quasi-category.

So the model structure on dendroidal sets is a presention of the (âˆ,1)-category of (âˆ,1)(\infty,1)-operads. It is Quillen equivalent to the standard model structure on operads enriched over Top or sSet. Therefore, conversely, the traditional homotopy-theoretic constructions on topological and chain operads (such as cofibrant resolutions in order to present homtopy algebras such as A-âˆ algebras, L-âˆ algebras, homotopy BV-algebras and the like) are also indeed presentations of (âˆ,1)(\infty,1)-operads.

In terms of relative operads

Relative operads are the operadic analogue of relative categories. In generalization of how the latter model ( âˆ , 1 ) (\infty,1) -categories, so relative operads model (âˆ,1)(\infty,1)-operads:

A relative operad is a pair (O,W)(O,W), where OO is a colored operad and WW is a replete subcategory of the category of unary operations of OO. Given a relative operad OO, one can formally invert the maps in WW to obtain an (âˆ,1)(\infty,1)-operad O[W âˆ’1]O[W^{-1}], called its localization. This process defines a functor

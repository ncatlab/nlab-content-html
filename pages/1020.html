Idea

The left part of a pair of adjoint functors is one of two best approximations to a weak inverse of the other functor of the pair. (The other best approximation is the functorâ€™s right adjoint, if it exists.) Note that a weak inverse itself, if it exists, must be a left adjoint, forming an adjoint equivalence.

A left adjoint to a forgetful functor is called a free functor. Many left adjoints can be constructed as quotients of free functors.

The concept generalises immediately to enriched categories and in 2-categories.

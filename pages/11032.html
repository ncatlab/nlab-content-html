Idea

The concept of traced monoidal category axiomatizes the structure on a monoidal category for it to have a sensible notion of trace the way it exists canonically in compact closed categories.

Definition

The original definition due to (Joyal-Street-Verity 96) is stated in the general setting of balanced monoidal categories?. Here we give just the slightly simpler formulation for the case of symmetric monoidal categories (Hasegawa 1997).

A symmetric monoidal category (C,‚äó,1,b)(C,\otimes,1,b) (where bb is the symmetry) is said to be traced if it is equipped with a natural family of functions

  • Superposing: Tr C‚äóA,C‚äóB X(id C‚äóf)=id C‚äóTr A,B X(f)Tr_{C\otimes A,C\otimes B}^X(id_C \otimes f) = id_C \otimes Tr_{A,B}^X(f) (for all f:A‚äóX‚ÜíB‚äóXf : A \otimes X \to B \otimes X)

The three axioms above (as well as the naturality conditions) then all have natural graphical interpretations (see Joyal-Street-Verity 96 or Hasegawa 1997).

In cartesian monoidal categories

For a cartesian monoidal category, the existence of a trace operator is equivalent to the existence of a ‚Äúparameterized‚Äù fixed point operator satisfying certain properties (Hasegawa 1997).

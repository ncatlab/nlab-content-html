Overview

The usual notion of Lambda-ring is directly related to the Banach ring (‚Ñ§,|‚ãÖ| 0)(\mathbb{Z},|\cdot|_0) of integers equipped with their trivial norm in the following way: a Lambda-ring is a usual ring equipped with an action of the monoid ‚Ñï=‚Ñ§\mathbb{N}=\mathbb{Z}-{0}/{¬±1}\{0\}/\{\pm 1\}. From the perspective of global analytic geometry, one thinks of Lambda-structures as related to the Banach ring of integers with their trivial norm, so that one may seek for various generalizations, that will be called ‚Äúgeneralized Lambda-structure‚Äù, associated to more general Banach or ind-Banach rings.

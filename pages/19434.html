Idea

The following is a quote from Ross Street's â€œAustralian conspectus of higher categoriesâ€ (Street 2010, p. 241):

In 1971 Bob Walters and I began work on Yoneda structures on 2-categories [[KS1]], [[StW]]. The idea was to axiomatize the deeper aspects of categories beyond their merely being algebraic structures. This work centred on the Yoneda embedding Aâ†’ğ’«AA\to \mathcal{P}A of a category AA into its presheaf category ğ’«A=[A op,Set]\mathcal{P}A =[ A^{op} ,Set] . Size was just an extra part of the structure. With the advent of elementary topos theory and the stimulation of the work of Anders Kock and Christian Mikkelsen, we showed that the preordered objects in a topos provided a good example. We were happy to realize [[KS1]] that an elementary topos was precisely a finitely complete category with a power object (that is, a relations classifier). This meant that my work with Walters could be viewed as a higher-dimensional version of topos theory. As usual when raising dimension, what we might mean by a 2-dimensional topos could be many things, several of which could be useful.

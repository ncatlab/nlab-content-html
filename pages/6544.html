Idea

The traditional notion of recursion over the natural numbers ‚Ñï\mathbb{N} is a way of defining a function out of ‚Ñï\mathbb{N} by specifying the image of 00 (the ‚Äúinitial value‚Äù) along with a way to obtain each successive value from the previous one(s). There is a rich theory regarding the functions which can or cannot be defined using only recursion: this is computability theory and has deep connections with the logic of Peano arithmetic.

More generally, recursion is a way of defining a function on any mathematical object which is ‚Äúdefined inductively‚Äù (in a way analogous to how the natural numbers are characterized by zero and successor). In place of the ‚Äúinitial value‚Äù and ‚Äúsuccessor step‚Äù, a general definition by recursion consists of giving one ‚Äúclause‚Äù for each ‚Äúconstructor‚Äù of the inductively defined object.

Recursion is formalized in type theory by the notion of inductive type (and the corresponding elimination rule) and, equivalently, in category theory by the notion of initial algebra of an endofunctor. For FF an endofunctor, a morphism of the form F(X)‚ÜíXF(X) \to X determines a collection of constructors and the recursion principle is the statement that there is a (unique) morphism f:A‚ÜíXf : A \to X from the initial such structure F(A)‚ÜíAF(A) \to A. This ff is the corresponding recursively defined function.

Viewed from just a slightly different angle, this state of affairs is the induction principle.

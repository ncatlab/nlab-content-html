Idea

In perturbative quantum field theory the St√ºckelberg-Petermann renormalization group (St√ºckelberg-Petermann 53) is the (original) incarnation of the renormalization group in the perspective of causal perturbation theory: It is the group (def. 2 below) of perturbative interaction vertex redefinitions (def. 1) which is such that any two choices of S-matrix renormalization schemes ùíÆ,ùíÆ‚Ä≤\mathcal{S}, \mathcal{S}' are related by a unique vertex redefinition ùíµ\mathcal{Z} via precomposition

ùíÆ‚Ä≤=ùíÆ‚àòùíµ. \mathcal{S}' \;=\; \mathcal{S} \circ \mathcal{Z} \,.

This statement (prop. 3 below) is also called the main theorem of perturbative renormalization.

If scaling transformations on spacetime happen to transform renormalization schemes into each other, then this main theorem of perturbative renormalization (prop. 3) directly implies that every scaling transformation uniquely corresponds to a interaction vertex redefinition, or conversely that the vertex redefinitions are given as funcitons of scale. As such they are also referred to as running coupling constants (def. 3 below). Beware that these do not in general form a group but a group cocycle, the Gell-Mann-Low renormalization cocycle (prop. 4 below.

Definition

(St√ºckelberg-Petermann renormalization group of vertex redefinitions)

Let (E BV-BRST,L‚Ä≤,Œî H)(E_{\text{BV-BRST}}, \mathbf{L}', \Delta_H) be a gauge fixed free field vacuum (this def.).

Then prop. 2 and prop 3 together says that (‚Äúmain theorem of perturbative renormalization‚Äù):

  1. the vertex redefinitions ùíµ\mathcal{Z} (def. 1) form a group under composition;

  2. the set of S-matrix renormalization schemes (this def., this remark) is a torsor over this group, in that any two are related by a unique vertex redefinition.

This group is called the (large) St√ºckelberg-Petermann renormalization group.

Typically one imposes a set of renormalization conditions (this def.) and then the corresponding subgroup of vertex redefinitions preserving these.

Properties

Scaling transformations and ‚Äúrunning coupling constants‚Äù

A priori the St√ºckelberg-Petermann renormalization group is not about scaling transformations. But if scaling transformations happen to produce new S-matrices/renormalization schemes from given ones, then the main theorem of perturbative renormalization induces for each such scaling transformation a re-definition of interaction Lagrangian densities, this is the Gell-Mann-Low renormalization cocycle (Gell-Mann & Low 54, Brunetti-D√ºtsch-Fredenhagen 09) for review see (D√ºtsch 18, section 3.5.3).

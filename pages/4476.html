Definition

A division algebra is a possibly non-associative algebra AA, typically over a field kk, which is also a division ring, hence with the property that for any a,b‚ààAa,b \in A then ab=0a b = 0 implies either a=0a = 0 or b=0b = 0 (or ab‚â†0a b \ne 0 whenever a,b‚â†0a, b \ne 0).

Related concepts

For applications to physics, the most interesting division algebras are probably the normed division algebras: the real numbers, complex numbers, quaternions and octonions. These have important relations to supersymmetry.

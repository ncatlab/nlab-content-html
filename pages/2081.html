Definition

A sheaf FF of sets on (the category of open subsets of) a topological space XX is flabby (flasque) if for any open subset U‚äÇXU\subset X, the restriction morphism F(X)‚ÜíF(U)F(X)\to F(U) is onto. Equivalently, for any open U‚äÇV‚äÇXU\subset V\subset X the restriction F(V)‚ÜíF(U)F(V)\to F(U) is surjective. In mathematical literature in English, the original French word flasque is still often used instead of flabby here.

The concept generalizes in a straightforward manner to flabby sheaves on locales.

Idea

Numbers like 3\sqrt{3} and 53{}^5\sqrt{3} look tied to the usual integers: indeed they are obtained from an integer number by doing operation of 2nd or 5th root. More generally, we can start with the usual integer numbers and do similar algebraic operations, namely form monic polynomials with such integer coefficients and search for roots in a larger ring, obtaining generalized integers as solutions of a monic polynomial equation, which makes sense in an arbitrary commutative ring. If we look for solutions of monic equations within â„š\mathbb{Q}, the field of rationals, we get nothing new, just the usual integers. Thus the integral elements in a ring generalize integers in wide context and form a â€œring of integersâ€ within a larger ring. This generalizes the standard integers inside the field of rational numbers to various situations like number fields and local non-archimedean fields.

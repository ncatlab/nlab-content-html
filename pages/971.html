Idea

An accessible category is a possibly large category which is however essentially determined by a small category, in a certain way.

Definition

Definition

A locally small category CC is Œ∫\kappa-accessible for a regular cardinal Œ∫\kappa if:

  1. the category has Œ∫\kappa-directed colimits (or, equivalently, Œ∫\kappa-filtered colimits), and

  2. there is a set of Œ∫\kappa-compact objects that generate the category under Œ∫\kappa-directed colimits.

Then CC is an accessible category if there exists a Œ∫\kappa so that it is Œ∫\kappa-accessible.

Remark

Unlike for locally presentable categories, it does not follow that if CC is Œ∫\kappa-accessible and Œ∫<Œª\kappa\lt \lambda then CC is also Œª\lambda-accessible. It is true, however, that for any accessible category, there are arbitrarily large cardinals Œª\lambda such that CC is Œª\lambda-accessible.

Proposition

Equivalent characterizations include that CC is accessible iff:

  • it is the category of models (in Set) of some small sketch.

  • it is of the form Ind Œ∫(S)Ind_\kappa(S) for SS small, i.e. the Œ∫\kappa-ind-completion of a small category, for some Œ∫\kappa.

  • it is of the form Œ∫Flat(S)\kappa\,Flat(S) for SS small and some Œ∫\kappa, i.e. the category of Œ∫\kappa-flat functors from some small category to SetSet.

  • it is the category of models (in SetSet) of a suitable type of logical theory.

Adjoint functor theorem

Proposition

(adjoint functors)

Every accessible functor satisfies the solution set condition, and every left or right adjoint between accessible categories is accessible. Therefore, the adjoint functor theorem takes an especially pleasing form for accessible categories: a functor is a left (resp. right) adjoint iff it is accessible and preserves all small colimits (resp. limits).

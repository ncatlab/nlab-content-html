Idea

A diffeomorphism œï:X‚ü∂X\phi \colon X \longrightarrow X of a (pseudo-) Riemannian manifold XX is volume preserving (VPD) if its pullback of differential forms fixes the volume form: œï *vol=vol\phi^\ast vol = vol.

If the dimension is dim(X)=2dim(X) = 2, then one also speaks of area-preserving diffeomorphisms (APD).

Volume preserving diffeomorphisms form a subgroup

SDiff(X)‚äÇDiff(X) SDiff(X) \subset Diff(X)

of the general diffeomorphism group of the underlying smooth manifold of XX. For XX compact, this is a Fr√©chet Lie group. Its Lie algebra is the algebra of incompressible (i.e. zero-divergence) vector fields on XX. For the case dim(X)=2dim(X) = 2, these Lie algebras (or their central extensions) are known as w ‚àû w_\infty -algebras and certain deformation quantizations of these as W ‚àû W_\infty -algebras.

In physics (field theory), volume-preserving diffeomorphisms appear:

  • as the gauge symmetries of unimodular gravity,

  • as residual gauge symmetries of relativistic brane sigma models after light cone gauge-fixing (cf. below),

  • as (gauge) symmetries of effective descriptions of fractional quantum Hall systems (cf. below).

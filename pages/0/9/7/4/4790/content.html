Definition

For n∈ℕn \in \mathbb{N}, the symplectic group Sp(2n,ℝ)Sp(2n, \mathbb{R}) is one of the classical Lie groups.

It is the subgroup of the general linear group GL(2n,ℝ)GL(2n, \mathbb{R}) of elements preserving the canonical symplectic form Ω\Omega on the Cartesian space ℝ 2n\mathbb{R}^{2n}, that is: the group consisting of those matrices AA such that

A TΩA=Ω. A^T \Omega A = \Omega \,.

The real symplectic group Sp(2n,ℝ)Sp(2n,\mathbb{R}) should not be confused with the compact symplectic group Sp(n)Sp(n), which is the maximal compact subgroup of the complex symplectic group Sp(2n,ℂ)Sp(2n,\mathbb{C}).

Properties

Maximal compact subgroup

The maximal compact subgroup of the symplectic group Sp(2n,ℝ)Sp(2n, \mathbb{R}) is the unitary group U(n)U(n).

Homotopy groups

By the above the homotopy groups of the symplectic group are those of the corresponding unitary group.

In particular the first homotopy group of the symplectic group is the integers

π 1(Sp(2n,ℝ))≃ℤ. \pi_1(Sp(2n,\mathbb{R})) \simeq \mathbb{Z} \,.

The unique connected double cover obtained from this is the metaplectic group extension Mp(2n)→Sp(2n,ℝ)Mp(2n) \to Sp(2n, \mathbb{R}).

The name “complex group” formerly advocated by me in allusion to line complexes, as these are defined by the vanishing of antisymmetric bilinear forms, has become more and more embarrassing through collision with the word “complex” in the connotation of complex number. I therefore propose to replace it by the corresponding Greek adjective “symplectic.” Dickson calls the group the “Abelian linear group” in homage to Abel who first studied it.

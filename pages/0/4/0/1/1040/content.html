Idea

An idempotent monad is a monad that ‚Äúsquares to itself‚Äù in the evident category-theoretic sense. Idempotent monads hence serve as categorified projection operators, in that they encode reflective subcategories and the reflection/localization onto these.

In terms of type theory idempotent monads interpret (co-)modal operators; see modal type theory.

In the language of stuff, structure, property, an idempotent monad may be said to equip its algebras with properties only (since C T‚ÜíCC^T\to C is fully faithful), unlike an arbitrary monad, which equips its algebras with at most structure (since C T‚ÜíCC^T\to C is, in general, faithful but not full).

If TT is idempotent, then it follows in particular that an object of CC admits at most one structure of TT-algebra, that this happens precisely when the unit Œ∑ X:X‚ÜíTX\eta_X\colon X\to T X is an isomorphism, and in this case the TT-algebra structure map is Œ∑ X ‚àí1:TX‚ÜíX\eta_X^{-1}\colon T X \to X. However, it is possible to have a non-idempotent monad for which any object of CC admits at most one structure of TT-algebra, in which case TT can be said to equip objects of CC with property-like structure; an easy example is the monad on semigroups whose algebras are monoids.

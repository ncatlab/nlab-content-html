Idea

The notion of final (‚àû,1)(\infty,1)-functor (also called a cofinal (‚àû,1)(\infty,1)-functor, but see Rem ) is the vertical categorification of the notion of final functor from category theory to (‚àû,1)-category-theory:

An (‚àû,1)-functor p:K‚Ä≤‚ÜíKp \colon K' \to K is final precisely if precomposition with pp preserves (‚àû,1)-colimits: if pp is final then for F:K‚ÜíCF \colon K \to C any (‚àû,1)-functor we have

lim ‚Üí(K‚ÜíFC)‚âÉlim ‚Üí(K‚Ä≤‚ÜípK‚ÜíFC), \lim_\to (K \stackrel{F}{\to} C) \simeq \lim_{\to} ( K' \stackrel{p}{\to} K \stackrel{F}{\to} C),

when either of these (‚àû,1)-colimits exist.

Terminology

The terminology conventions surrounding the topic of final functors are a bit of a mess. Often

  1. ‚Äúfinal map‚Äù is used to refer to ‚àû\infty-functors such that restriction along them preserves ‚àû \infty -colimiting cocones.

  2. ‚Äúinitial map‚Äù is used for functors for which restriction preserves ‚àû \infty -limiting cones.

A mnemonic rule for this terminology is that final objects are picked out by final functors and likewise for initial objects.

But beware that this convention is not universal, for instance Lurie‚Äòs work is a notable exception to this rule. Also beware of the following:

Remark

Beware that the term ‚Äúcofinal (‚àû,1)-functor‚Äù can mean either a functor for which the precomposition functor preserves colimits (as in Lurie‚Äôs Higher Topos Theory) or limits (as in Cisinski‚Äôs Higher Categories and Homotopical Algebra).

Given that the two main sources for quasicategories assign opposite meanings to this term, it is best to avoid its usage altogether.

Further adding to the confusion is that some sources, like Borceux‚Äòs Handbook of Categorical Algebra use the term ‚Äúfinal functor‚Äù for a functor for which the precomposition functor preserves limits, in contrast to the majority of the literature. Such usage, fortunately, is marginal.

In more detail:

  • Joyal‚Äòs The Theory of Quasi-Categories and its Applications:

    colimit-preserving: final;

    limit-preserving: initial (page 171);

  • Cisinski‚Äòs Higher Categories and Homotopical Algebra:

    colimit-preserving: final (Definition .4.1.8);

    limit-preserving: cofinal (Definition 4.4.13);

  • Riehl‚ÄìVerity‚Äòs Elements of ‚àû-Category Theory:

    colimit-preserving: final;

    limit-preserving: initial (Definition 2.4.5).

  • Lurie‚Äòs Higher Topos Theory:

    colimit-preserving: cofinal (Definition 4.1.1.1);

    limit-preserving: (no terminology introduced);

  • Lurie‚Äòs Higher Algebra:

    colimit-preserving: left cofinal;

    limit-preserving: right cofinal (page 13);

  • Lurie‚Äòs Kerodon:

    colimit-preserving: right cofinal (Tag 02N1);

    limit-preserving (initial (‚àû,1)-functors): left cofinal.

See also the warning in the article final functor (here).

The following says that up to equivalence, the cofinal maps of simplicial sets are the right anodyne morphisms

Proposition

A map of simplicial sets is cofinal precisely if it factors as a right anodyne map followed by a trivial fibration.

This is (Lurie, cor. 4.1.1.12).

On categories of simplices

Definition

For K‚ààK \in sSet a simplicial set, write Œî /K\Delta_{/K} for its category of elements, called here the category of simplices of the simplicial set:

an object of Œî /K\Delta_{/K} is a morphism of simplicial sets of the form Œî n‚ÜíK\Delta^n \to K for some n‚àà‚Ñïn \in \mathbb{N} (hence an nn-simplex of KK) and a morphism is a commuting diagram

Œî n 1 ‚Üí Œî n 2 ‚Üò ‚Üô K. \array{ \Delta^{n_1}&&\to&& \Delta^{n_2} \\ & \searrow && \swarrow \\ && K } \,.

Moreover, write

for the non-full subcategory on the non-degenerate simplices.

Remark

When the simplicial set KK is non-singular, i.e. every face of a non-degenerate simplex is still non-degenerate, then the category Œî /K nd\Delta_{/K}^{nd} is a poset and it coincides with the barycentric subdivision of KK.

Suppose KK is a non-singular simplicial set. Then the inclusion

N(Œî /K nd)‚Ü™N(Œî /K) N(\Delta_{/K}^{nd}) \hookrightarrow N(\Delta_{/K})

is a cofinal morphism of quasi-categories.

This appears as (Lurie, variant 4.2.3.15).

Proposition

For every simplicial set KK there exists a cofinal map

N(Œî /K)‚ÜíK. N(\Delta_{/K}) \to K \,.

This is (Lurie, prop. 4.2.3.14).

Remark

If the simplicial set KK is singular, it is not in general true that the inclusion N(Œî /K nd)‚Ü™N(Œî /K)N(\Delta_{/K}^{nd}) \hookrightarrow N(\Delta_{/K}) is final. For a counter-example, see (Hovey, 9).

Proposition

For every simplicial set KK, evaluation at the initial vertex

N(Œî /K) op‚ÜíK. N(\Delta_{/K})^{op} \to K \,.

is both initial and final.

This appears as (Shah, 12.2) and also follows from the fact that this map is a weak localization (Cisinski, 7.3.15).

Remark

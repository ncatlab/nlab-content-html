Idea

The notion of nn-fold category is what is obtained by iterating the process of forming internal categories nn-times, starting with sets: an 00-fold category is just an object of the ambient category (say a set) and then inductively an n+1n+1-fold category is a internal category in the category of nn-fold categories.

If the ambient category is instead an (∞,1)-category such as ∞Grpd, then an nn-fold category is an “n-fold Segal space”. If a “completeness” condition is added (analogous to “globularity” of an n-fold category), one obtaines an n-fold complete Segal space, which is a model for (∞,n)(\infty,n)-categories.

Definition

nn-fold categories

A 00-fold category is a set. A (strict) nn-fold category for n>0n \gt 0 is an internal category in the category of (n−1)(n-1)-fold categories. An nn-fold category is also known as an nn-tuple category.

In particular:

  • A 11-fold category is precisely a category.
  • A 22-fold category is precisely a double category (introduced by Charles Ehresmann in 1963).
  • A 33-fold category is precisely a triple category.

Advantages

A key advantage of nn-fold categories is the ease of expressing multiple compositions, and so the idea of “algebraic inverse to subdivision”. This is important because subdivision is a key tool in many local-to-global problems in mathematics and science, and these themselves are an important class of problems.

Thus subdividing an nn-cube amounts to dividing the cube into small cubes by hyperplanes parallel to the faces. For a 22-fold category, we can define a composable array (a ij)(a_{ij}) of 22-dimensional elements (called squares) to be such that any a ija_{ij} is composable with its immediate neighbours. In such case, the associative and interchange laws imply that the composition [a ij][a_{ij}] is well defined. This process is easily extended to nn-fold categories, using elements say a (r)a_{(r)} where (r)(r) is multi-index, and is applied widely in the JPAA papers by Brown and Higgins listed below. It seems much more difficult to express these ideas in the globular or simplicial contexts.

nn-fold groupoids

Analogously, a 00-fold groupoid is again a set, and an nn-fold groupoid is an internal groupoid in (n−1)(n-1)-fold groupoids; in particular, a 11-fold groupoid is a groupoid.

Analogous to how a group is a groupoid with a single object, one can consider (n+1)(n+1)-fold groupoids for which all morphisms in one of the (n+1)(n+1) directions are endomorphisms. These are the cat-n-groups.

More generally, an (n,r)(n,r)-fold groupoid is an rr-fold category in (n−r)(n-r)-fold groupoids; compare (n,r)(n,r)-category.

Note also that a category object in the category of groups is actually a groupoid object.

Properties

Cartesian closure

The category of nn-fold categories is a cartesian closed category. By induction from the statement at Internal category - Cartesian closure.

Homotopy types

Even though an nn-fold category is a strict version of an n-category in that all nn composition operations are strictly unital and associative and strictly commute with each other, still nn-fold groupoids model all homotopy n-types. See homotopy hypothesis.

Relation to strict globular ω\omega-categories

By a theorem by Al-Agl, Brown and Steiner, strict omega-categories are equivalent to those ∞\infty-fold categories that satisfy a couple of restrictive properties (something like that all 1-categories of nn-cells for all nn are the same and that all the “thin” identity elements exist, called “connections”): these are the “cubical ω\omega-categories with connections”. Because it is relatively straightforward to define a monoidal closed category in the cubical theory, using the formula I m⊗I n=I m+nI^m \otimes I^n = I^{m+n}, this leads to a monoidal closed structure for strict globular ω\omega categories.

This is a category version of a corresponding groupoid theorem of Brown and Higgins which follows from the two papers listed below.

Jean-Louis Loday introduced in the paper listed below the category of what he called nn-cat groups, but are now called catn^n-groups as they are exactly nn-fold groupoids internal to the category of groups. He showed that these objects model weak, pointed homotopy nn-types, see homotopy hypothesis. The paper by Brown and Loday shows that these structures can be used, via a van Kampen type theorem, for explicit computations in homotopy theory, and this is further developed in the paper by Ellis and Steiner. This paper relates the theory to that of crossed modules, nn-ad homotopy groups, and the important nn-ad connectivity theorem, which is related to results on homotopical excision.

Idea

In quantum field theory, a given vacuum state is called stable if in a suitable sense it does not evolve into or from any orthogonal state.

In perturbative quantum field theory with specified S-matrix ùíÆ(gS int)\mathcal{S}(g S_{int}) it makes sense to say that a vacuum state is stable if there is vanishing quantum probability for the vacuum state to scatter into a non-vacuum state, or for a non-vacuum state to scatter into the vacuum state. (e.g. Nikolic 01, p. 6)

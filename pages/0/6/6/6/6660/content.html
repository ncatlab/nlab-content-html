Idea

An enhanced 2-category (or ‚Ñ±\mathcal{F}-category) is like a 2-category, but with two types of 1-morphisms, one of which we think of as ‚Äústricter‚Äù than the other. The stricter morphisms are called tight and the less strict ones are called loose.

Definition

Strict ‚Ñ±\mathcal{F}-categories

Let ‚Ñ±\mathcal{F} denote the category whose objects are functors that are fully faithful and injective on objects, and whose morphisms are commutative squares (a full subcategory of the arrow category of Cat). We call the objects of ‚Ñ±\mathcal{F}, for the nonce, full embeddings. Then ‚Ñ±\mathcal{F} is cartesian closed, complete and cocomplete, hence a Benabou cosmos.

A strict ‚Ñ±\mathcal{F}-category is a category enriched over ‚Ñ±\mathcal{F}. Therefore, between every two objects, an ‚Ñ±\mathcal{F}-category KK has an object K(x,y)‚àà‚Ñ±K(x,y)\in \mathcal{F}, hence a full embedding K(x,y) œÑ‚ÜíK(x,y) ŒªK(x,y)_\tau \to K(x,y)_\lambda. The objects of K(x,y) œÑK(x,y)_\tau are called tight morphisms x‚Üíyx\to y, and the objects of K(x,y) ŒªK(x,y)_\lambda are called loose morphisms x‚áùyx\rightsquigarrow y.

Since full embeddings are injective on objects, ‚Äúbeing tight‚Äù is a property of a loose morphism. (This would still be true in the ‚Äúup to unique isomorphism‚Äù sense even if we did not ask for injectivity on objects, but when dealing with strict things, it is easier to keep them as strict as possible.) And since full embeddings are fully faithful, the 2-cells between two tight morphisms are the same whether we regard them as tight or as loose.

For any ‚Ñ±\mathcal{F}-category KK, the objects, tight morphisms, and 2-cells form a strict 2-category K œÑK_\tau, and the objects, loose morphisms, and 2-cells form a strict 2-category K ŒªK_\lambda. There is an obvious strict 2-functor

K œÑ‚ÜíK ŒªK_\tau \to K_\lambda

which is the identity on objects, strictly faithful on 1-morphisms, and locally fully faithful. Since KK can be recovered from this 2-functor, an equivalent definition of a strict ‚Ñ±\mathcal{F}-category is as a strict 2-functor with these properties.

Strict ‚Ñ±\mathcal{F}-categories are equivalent to strict double categories with a functorial choice of companions, and for which the assignment of tight morphisms to loose morphisms is injective.

Weak ‚Ñ±\mathcal{F}-categories

Probably the best ‚Äúfully weak‚Äù version of ‚Ñ±\mathcal{F}-categories is obtained by redefining ‚Ñ±\mathcal{F} to consist of fully faithful functors, with squares that commute up to specified isomorphism, and then by considering ‚Ñ±\mathcal{F}-enriched bicategories rather than enriched categories. Such a thing would be equivalent to an identity-on-objects and locally-fully-faithful pseudofunctor between bicategories.

One could consider semi-strict versions as well, in which (for example) the tight morphisms form a strict 2-category.

Examples

  • Any proarrow equipment is an ‚Ñ±\mathcal{F}-category (perhaps weak, perhaps semi-strict).

  • An example of a semi-strict ‚Ñ±\mathcal{F}-category is the localization 2-functor Cat(S)‚ÜíCat(S)[W ‚àí1]Cat(S) \to Cat(S)[W^{-1}] for a class of weak equivalences WW.

  • For any (strict) 2-monad TT, there are strict ‚Ñ±\mathcal{F}-categories of TT-algebras whose tight and loose morphisms are, respectively:

    1. strict and pseudo TT-morphisms
    2. strict and lax TT-morphisms
    3. strict and colax TT-morphisms
    4. pseudo and lax TT-morphisms
    5. pseudo and colax TT-morphisms

    In fact, this can be generalized to any ‚Ñ±\mathcal{F}-monad on an ‚Ñ±\mathcal{F}-category.

  • Any 2-category gives rise to two ‚Ñ±\mathcal{F}-categories:

    • In a chordate ‚Ñ±\mathcal{F}-category, all morphisms are tight.
    • In an inchordate ‚Ñ±\mathcal{F}-category, only identities are tight.

  • The lax slice 2-category is an ‚Ñ±\mathcal{F}-category whose tight 2-category is the (pseudo) slice 2-category. This ‚Ñ±\mathcal{F}-category allows a definition of fibrations using lax F-adjunctions.

  • ‚Ñ±\mathcal{F} itself becomes an ‚Ñ±\mathcal{F}-category in the usual way. Its tight morphisms are just the morphisms in the underlying ordinary category ‚Ñ±\mathcal{F}, while its loose morphisms are simply functors between the loose parts (the codomains of the full embeddings).

‚Ñ±\mathcal{F}-weighted limits

The general machinery of enriched category theory applied to ‚Ñ±\mathcal{F} gives us a notion of weighted limit. Note first that an ‚Ñ±\mathcal{F}-enriched diagram in an ‚Ñ±\mathcal{F}-category is a diagram of morphisms in which some are required to be tight, and others are not (but could ‚Äúaccidentally‚Äù be tight).

In general, a weighted limit of such a diagram in a (strict) ‚Ñ±\mathcal{F}-category is a weighted (strict) 2-limit in its 2-category of loose morphisms, with the property that certain specified projections from the limit object are tight and ‚Äújointly detect tightness‚Äù, in the sense that a morphism into the limit is tight if and only if its composites with all of the specified projections are tight. Details and examples can be found in (LS).

One of the most important things about ‚Ñ±\mathcal{F}-categories is that they allow us to define the classes of rigged limits, which are the ‚Ñ±\mathcal{F}-weighted limits that are created by the forgetful functors from the various ‚Ñ±\mathcal{F}-categories of algebras and strict/pseudo/lax/colax morphisms over a 2-monad (or an ‚Ñ±\mathcal{F}-monad).

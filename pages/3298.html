Idea

Hochschild (co)homology is a homological construction which makes sense for any associative algebra, or more generally any dg-algebra or ring spectrum. It has multiple interpretations in higher category theory. Presently, everything below pertains to Hochschild homology of commutative algebras; an exposition of the noncommutative case remains to be written.

Thus, for AA a commutative ‚àû-algebra, its Hochschild homology complex is its (‚àû,1)-tensoring S 1‚ãÖAS^1 \cdot A with the ‚àû-groupoid incarnation of the circle. More generally, for SS any ‚àû\infty-groupoid/simplicial set, S‚ãÖAS \cdot A is the corresponding higher order Hochschild homology of AA.

In the presence of function algebras on ‚àû-stacks it may happen that A=ùí™(X)A = \mathcal{O}(X) is the algebra of functions on some ‚àû-stack XX and that ùí™(‚àí)\mathcal{O}(-) sends powerings of XX to tensorings of ùí™(X)\mathcal{O}(X). In that case it follows that the Hochschild homology complex of ùí™(X)\mathcal{O}(X) is the function complex ùí™(‚Ñí(X))\mathcal{O}(\mathcal{L}(X)) on the derived loop space ‚ÑíX\mathcal{L}X of XX.

The Hochschild complex

Originally the notion of Hochschild homology was introduced as the chain homology of a certain chain complex associated to any bimodule NN over some algebra AA: its bar complex, written

C ‚Ä¢(A,N):=N‚äó A‚äóA opB ‚Ä¢A, C_\bullet(A,N) := N \otimes_{A \otimes A^{op}} \mathrm{B}_\bullet A \,,

where NN and AA are regarded as A‚äóA opA \otimes A^{op}-bimodules in the obvious way.

Then it was understood that this complex is the result of tensoring the AA-bimodules NN with AA over A‚äóA opA \otimes A^{op} but using the derived functor of the tensor product functor ‚Äì the Tor functor ‚Äì in the ambient model structure on chain complexes:

C ‚Ä¢(A,N)=N‚äó A‚äóA op DA=Tor A‚äóA op ‚Ä¢(N,A). C_\bullet(A,N) = N \otimes^D_{A\otimes A^{op}} A = Tor^\bullet_{A\otimes A^{op}}(N,A) \,.

Then still a little later, it was understood that this is just the ordinary tensor product in the symmetric monoidal (‚àû,1)-category of chain complexes. If this is understood, we can just write again simply

C ‚Ä¢(A,N):=N‚äó A‚äóA opA. C_\bullet(A,N) := N \otimes_{A \otimes A^{op}} A \,.

This, generally, is the definition of the Hochschild homology object of any bimodule over an monoid in a symmetric monoidal (‚àû,1)(\infty,1)-category (symmetry is needed to make sense of A opA^{op}). Dually, the Hochschild cohomology object is

C ‚Ä¢(A,N):=Hom A‚äóA op(A,N). C^\bullet(A,N) := Hom_{A\otimes A^{op}}(A,N).

Of special interest is the case where N=AN = A. In this case the Hochschild cohomology object is also called the (‚Äú(‚àû,1)(\infty,1)-‚Äù or ‚Äúderived-‚Äù)center of AA:

Z(A):=Hom A‚äóA op(A,A). Z(A) := Hom_{A\otimes A^{op}}(A,A).

Dually, the Hochschild homology object when N=AN=A is called the universal trace or shadow. In this case, if A=ùí™(X)A = \mathcal{O}(X) can be identified with an ‚àû\infty-algebra of functions on an object XX, which is therefore commutative so that A op=AA^{op}= A, and if taking functions commutes with (‚àû,1)(\infty,1)-pullbacks, then

Z(ùí™(X))‚âÉùí™(X√ó X√óXX)‚âÉùí™(‚ÑíX) Z(\mathcal{O}(X)) \simeq \mathcal{O}(X \times_{X \times X} X) \simeq \mathcal{O}(\mathcal{L}X)

is the ‚àû\infty-algebra of functions on the free loop space object of XX.

Properties

By the Hochschild-Kostant-Rosenberg theorem and its generalizations, the Hochschild homology HH ‚Ä¢(ùí™(X),ùí™(X))HH_\bullet(\mathcal{O}(X),\mathcal{O}(X)) of an ordinary algebra tends to behave like the algebra of K√§hler differentials of ùí™(X)\mathcal{O}(X). More generally, this computes the cotangent complex of the ‚àû\infty-algebra ùí™(X)\mathcal{O}(X). The cup product gives the wedge product of forms and the S 1S^1-action the de Rham differential.

Dually this means that in derived geometry the free loop space object ‚ÑíX\mathcal{L} X consists of infinitesimal loops in XX (in ordinary geometry it would be equal to SpecASpec A, consisting only of constant loops).

Analogously, Hochschild cohomology HH ‚Ä¢(ùí™(X),ùí™(X))HH^\bullet(\mathcal{O}(X), \mathcal{O}(X)) of ùí™(X)\mathcal{O}(X) computes the multivector fields on XX. There are pairing operations on HH homology and cohomology that make them support a general differential calculus on XX, which makes sense even if ùí™(X)\mathcal{O}(X) is a noncommutative algebra.

Topological chiral homology

Notice that the tensoring that gives the Hochschild homology is given by the ‚àû\infty-colimit over the constant functor

K‚ãÖA‚âÉlim ‚Üí KA. K \cdot A \simeq {\lim_\to}_K A \,.

Pirashvili‚Äôs higher order Hochschild homology

We demonstrate how the above (‚àû,1)(\infty,1)-category theoretic definition of higher order Hochschild homology reproduces the simplicial definition by (Pirashvili).

Proposition

Let TT be a Lawvere theory regarded as a 0-truncated (‚àû,1)-algebraic theory.

Consider a model structure on simplicial T-algebras/on homotopy T-algebras presenting TAlg ‚àûT Alg_\infty such that

  1. it is a simplicial model category;

  2. tensoring with simplicial sets preserves weak equivalences and hence cofibrant replacement.

Then for ùí™(X)‚ààTAlg‚Ü™TAlg ‚àû\mathcal{O}(X) \in T Alg \hookrightarrow T Alg_\infty and K‚àà‚àûGrpdK \in \infty Grpd the higher order Hochschild homology complex K‚ãÖùí™(X)K \cdot \mathcal{O}(X) is presented by the ordinary tensoring K‚ãÖùí™(X)K \cdot \mathcal{O}(X) in the model category, for KK any simplicial set incarnation of the ‚àû\infty-groupoid.

Proof

The (‚àû,1)(\infty,1)-tensoring in an (‚àû,1)(\infty,1)-category presented by a simplicial model category is modeled by the ordinary tensoring of the latter on a cofibrant resolution of the given object. This is discussed in the section ‚àû-tensoring ‚Äì models.

Remark

We can always use the model structure on homotopy T-algebras to satisfy the assumption of the above proposition. That is a simplicial model category for every TT and every ordinary algebra is cofibrant in this structure.

Notice that in this model category even if ùí™(X)\mathcal{O}(X) is fibrant (which it is if ùí™X\mathcal{O}X is an ordinary algebra), then K‚ãÖùí™(X)K \cdot \mathcal{O}(X) is in general far from being fibant. Computing the simplicial homotopy groups of K‚ãÖùí™(X)K \cdot \mathcal{O}(X) and hence the Hochschild homology involves passing to a fibrant reolsution of K‚ãÖùí™(X)K \cdot \mathcal{O}(X) first, that will make it a homotopy T-algebra.

On the other hand, if we find a simplicial model structure on simplicial T-algebras (which are degreewise genuine TT-algebras) then the coproducts involved degreewise in forming K‚ãÖùí™(X)K \cdot \mathcal{O}(X) will be tensor products of algebras, and hence in particular themselves again algebras. For such a model the tensoring K‚ãÖùí™(X)K \cdot \mathcal{O}(X) yields explicitly (under the Dold-Kan correspondence).

This is the case for the tensoring of dg-algebras over simplicial sets and leads to Teimuraz Pirashvili‚Äôs formulation of higher order Hochschild homology for ordinary algebras (Pirashvili).

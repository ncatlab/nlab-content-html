Idea

A conjectural characterization of symmetric powers in symmetric monoidal categories enriched over modules over a â„š +\mathbb{Q}^{+}-associative algebra is described here. The description in terms of universal properties would be equivalent to a much longer one but much more constructive in algebraic terms. It would be similar to the characterization of products known as Fox theorem but where products are replaced by symmetric powers. We work in symmetric monoidal categories enriched over modules over a â„š +\mathbb{Q}^{+}-algebra. The adaptation to CMon-enriched symmetric monoidal categories would involve symmetric and divided powers which are equal in this simpler setting. This conjecture seems to be true and if it is really the case, the proof is not trivial.

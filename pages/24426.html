Idea

A conjectural characterization of symmetric powers in symmetric monoidal categories enriched over modules over a ℚ +\mathbb{Q}^{+}-associative algebra is described here. The description in terms of universal properties would be equivalent to a much longer one but much more constructive in algebraic terms. It would be similar to the characterization of products known as Fox theorem but where products are replaced by symmetric powers. We work in symmetric monoidal categories enriched over modules over a ℚ +\mathbb{Q}^{+}-algebra. The adaptation to CMon-enriched symmetric monoidal categories would involve symmetric and divided powers which are equal in this simpler setting. This conjecture seems to be true and if it is really the case, the proof is not trivial.

Conjecture

We suppose that the symmetric monoidal categories are strict monoidal categories.

Definition

We say that a symmetric monoidal category 𝒞\mathcal{C} possesses the nn-th symmetric power (n≥0n \ge 0) iff it possesses this coequalizer for every A∈𝒞A \in \mathcal{C}: and r n(A):A ⊗n→S n(A)r_{n}(A):A^{\otimes n} \rightarrow S_{n}(A) is a natural transformation.

Definition

A functorial special bicommutative graded bimonoid in a CMonCMon-enriched symmetric monoidal category is given by:

  • a family (S n:𝒞→𝒞) n≥0(S_{n}:\mathcal{C}\rightarrow \mathcal{C})_{n \ge 0} of endofunctors
  • a family (∇ n,p:S n(A)⊗S p(A)→S n+p(A)) n,p≥0(\nabla_{n,p}:S_{n}(A) \otimes S_{p}(A) \rightarrow S_{n+p}(A))_{n,p \ge 0} of natural transformations
  • a family (Δ n,p:S n+p(A)→S n(A)⊗S p(A)) n,p≥0(\Delta_{n,p}:S_{n+p}(A) \rightarrow S_{n}(A) \otimes S_{p}(A))_{n,p \ge 0} of natural transformations

such that:

  • S 0(A)=IS_{0}(A)=I

  • S 1(A)=AS_{1}(A)=A

  • for every A∈𝒞A \in \mathcal{C}, (A,(∇ n,p) n,p≥0,(Δ n,p) n,p≥0)(A,(\nabla_{n,p})_{n,p \ge 0}, (\Delta_{n,p})_{n,p \ge 0})) is a special bicommutative graded bimonoid ie.:

  • γ;∇ p,n=∇ n,p\gamma;\nabla_{p,n} = \nabla_{n,p} (commutativity)

  • Δ p,n;γ=Δ n,p\Delta_{p,n};\gamma = \Delta_{n,p} (cocommutativity)

  • ∇ n,0=1\nabla_{n,0}=1 (unitality)

  • Δ n,0=1\Delta_{n,0}=1 (counitality)

  • (∇ n,p⊗1);∇ n+p,q=(1⊗∇ p,q);∇ n,p+q(\nabla_{n,p} \otimes 1);\nabla_{n+p,q} = (1 \otimes \nabla_{p,q});\nabla_{n,p+q} (associativity)

Conjecture

Let RR be a ℚ +\mathbb{Q}^{+}-algebra. Let 𝒞\mathcal{C} be a symmetric monoidal category enriched over RR-modules. Let (S n:𝒞→𝒞) n≥0(S_{n}:\mathcal{C} \rightarrow \mathcal{C})_{n \ge 0} be a family of endofunctors such that S 0(A)=IS_{0}(A)=I and S 1(A)=AS_{1}(A) = A. Then, there is a bijection between families of natural transformations (r n(A):A ⊗ n→S n(A)) n≥0(r_{n}(A):A^{\otimes_{n}} \rightarrow S_{n}(A))_{n \ge 0} which make (S n(A))(S_{n}(A)) symmetric powers and families of natural transformations (∇ n,p:S n(A)⊗S p(A)→S n+p(A)) n,p≥0,(Δ n,p:S n+p(A)→S n(A)⊗S p(A)) n,p≥0(\nabla_{n,p}:S_{n}(A) \otimes S_{p}(A) \rightarrow S_{n+p}(A))_{n,p \ge 0}, (\Delta_{n,p}:S_{n+p}(A) \rightarrow S_{n}(A) \otimes S_{p}(A))_{n,p \ge 0} which make (S n(A))(S_{n}(A)) a functorial special bicommutative graded bimonoid.

Towards a proof

Symmetric powers in symmetric monoidal categories enriched over modules over a ℚ +\mathbb{Q}^{+}-algebra

In a symmetric monoidal category 𝒞\mathcal{C} enriched over modules over a ℚ +\mathbb{Q}^{+}-algebra, symmetric powers are equivalent to divided powers ie.:

Proposition

Let RR be a ℚ +\mathbb{Q}^{+}-algebra and 𝒞\mathcal{C} be a symmetric monoidal category enriched over RR-modules. Let n≥0n \ge 0 and S n:𝒞→𝒞S_{n}:\mathcal{C} \rightarrow \mathcal{C} be an endofunctor. There is a bijection between natural transformations r n:A ⊗n→S n(A)r_{n}:A^{\otimes n} \rightarrow S_{n}(A) which make (S n(A))(S_{n}(A)) symmetric powers and natural transformations s n:S n(A)→A ⊗ns_{n}:S_{n}(A) \rightarrow A^{\otimes n} which make (S n(A))(S_{n}(A)) divided powers ie. such that for every A∈𝒞A \in \mathcal{C}, we have this equalizer:

With this last equivalent definition, we have already replaced the universal property defining symmetric powers by two natural transformations which verify some equations. The work is now to show the equivalence between these two equations and the ones defining a functorial special bicommutative graded bimonoid.

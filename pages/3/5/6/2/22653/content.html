Definition

For n∈ℕn \in \mathbb{N}, consider the complex group algebra ℂ[Sym(n)]\mathbb{C}[Sym(n)] of the symmetric group on nn elements.

Definition

The Jucys-Murphy elements

J k∈ℂ[Sym(n)]k∈{1,⋯,n} J_k \;\in\; \mathbb{C}[Sym(n)] \;\;\;\; k \in \{1, \cdots, n\}

are the following sums of transpositions (i,j)∈Sym(n)⊂ℂ[Sym(n)](i,j) \in Sym(n) \subset \mathbb{C}[Sym(n)] in this group algebra:

(1)J 1≔0 J_1 \;\coloneqq\; 0
(2)J k≔∑i=1k−1(i,k) J_k \;\coloneqq\; \underoverset{i = 1}{k-1}{\sum} (i,k)

(Murphy 81, p. 287 (1 of 11), Murphy 83, p. 259 (2 of 8))

Notice that J kJ_k may be understood as the sum of all transpositions in Sym(k)Sym(k) that are not in the subgroup Sym(k−1)⊂Sym(k)Sym(k-1) \subset Sym(k).

Eigenvalues

Write sYTableaux nsYTableaux_n for the set of standard Young tableaux with nn boxes, and write

(3){v T,k∈ℂ[Sym(n)]} T∈sYTableaux n1≤m≤dim(S (|T|)) \big\{ v_{T,k} \in \mathbb{C}[Sym(n)]\big\}_{ { T \in sYTableaux_n} \atop { 1 \leq m \leq dim(S^{(\left\vert T\right\vert)}) } }

for the Gelfand-Tsetlin basis (Young’s seminormal representation).

We will notationally suppress the multiplicity index mm in the following.

Proposition

The v Tv_T (3) are joint eigenvectors of the Jucys-Murphy elements (Def. ) for eigenvalues the “content” j−ij - i of the box (i,j)(i,j) that contains the number kk in the standard Young tableau

J kv T=(j−i)v T,T i,j=k. J_k v_T \;=\; (j - i) v_T \,, \;\;\; T_{i,j} = k \,.

In particular, the Jucys-Murphy elements all commute with each other.

This is due to Jucys 71, recalled as Jucys 74 (12), and independently due to Murphy 81 (3.8).

Factorization of Cayley-Distance kernel

Proposition

The characteristic polynomial of the Jucys-Murphy elements is proportional to the Cayley distance kernel:

(t+J 1)(t+J 2)⋯(t+J n)=∑σ∈Sym(n)e ln(t)⋅#cycles(σ)σ∈ℂ[Sym(n)][t] \big( t + J_1 \big) \big( t + J_2 \big) \cdots \big( t + J_n \big) \;=\; \underset{ \sigma \in Sym(n) }{\sum} e^{ ln(t) \cdot \# cycles(\sigma) } \, \sigma \;\;\;\;\; \in \mathbb{C}[Sym(n)][t]

On the Wikipedia page this is attributed to Jucys, but without explicit reference. It might be in Jucys 71 (but is not mentioned in the review in Jucys 74). However, it is not much of a theorem, anyways:

Proof

By induction:

For n=1n = 1 the claim reduces to

(t+J 1)=e ln(t)⋅1, \big( t + J_1 \big) \;=\; e^{ ln(t) \cdot 1 } \,,

which holds by (1).

Now assume that the statement is true for n∈ℕn \in \mathbb{N}.

Observe that every permutation σ∈Sym(n+1)\sigma \in Sym(n+1) may be written as product

σ=c 1(σ)⋅c 2(σ)⋯c #cycles(σ)(σ) \sigma \;=\; c_1(\sigma) \cdot c_2(\sigma) \cdots c_{\# cycles(\sigma)}(\sigma)

of products of transpositions of the form

c i(σ)=(i ℓ i−1,i ℓ i−2)∘⋯∘(i 3,i 2)∘(i 2,i 1)∘(i 1,i ℓ i), c_i(\sigma) \;\; \;=\; (i_{\ell_i-1},i_{\ell_i-2}) \circ \cdots \circ (i_3,i_2) \circ (i_2,i_1) \circ (i_1,i_{\ell_i}) \,,

where ℓ i\ell_i is the length of the iith permutation cycle.

Here we may assume without restriction that

i j<i ℓ i,AAAandAAAi<j⇒i ℓ i<j ℓ j i_j \lt i_{\ell_i} \,, {\phantom{AAA}} \text{and} {\phantom{AAA}} i \lt j \;\; \Rightarrow \;\; i_{\ell_i} \lt j_{\ell_j}

because if not then we may (1) rearrange the c i(σ)c_i(\sigma) within their product (these commute with each other, since they contain permutations among elements within distinct cycles) and (2) cyclically rearrange the factors withing each c i(σ)c_i(\sigma).

Once all permutations are uniquely represented as products this way, the representative of any σ∈Sym(n+1)\sigma \in Sym(n+1) is uniquely obtained from the representative of an element in Sym(n)Sym(n), by either:

  1. multiplying from the right with a transposition (k,n+1)(k, n+1) for k≤nk \leq n, in which case the n+1n+1st element will be part of a cycle that was already present;

  2. by retaining the previous product as is, in which case the n+1n+1st element will be its own new cycle.

This means that

∑σ∈Sym(n+1)t #cycles(σ)σ=∑σ′∈Sym(n)t #cycles(σ′)σ′(t+J n+1) \underset{\sigma \in Sym(n+1)}{\sum} t^{ \# cycles(\sigma) } \, \sigma \; = \; \underset{\sigma' \in Sym(n)}{\sum} t^{ \# cycles(\sigma') } \, \sigma' (t + J_{n+1})

and hence the claim follows by the induction assumption.

Nuclear adjunctions

Definition

A nuclear adjunction or bimonadic adjunction is an adjunction that is both monadic and comonadic.

One point in favour of the terminology “nuclear” over “bimonadic” is that the term bimonadic functor is sometimes used in the literature for a functor that is both monadic and comonadic, which is a different concept that is related to bimonads.

Properties

  • Consider an adjunction F⊣GF \dashv G, generating a monad TT and a comonad DD. Under minimal assumptions on the categories, there is an adjunction between the category of TT-algebras, and the category of DD-coalgebras, and this adjunction is nuclear. See Pavlovic–Hughes and below.

  • Consider an adjunction F⊣G:B→AF \dashv G : B \to A, generating a monad TT and a comonad DD. Assuming that BB is Cauchy complete, FF is comonadic if and only if the free–forgetful adjunction associated to the Eilenberg–Moore category of TT is nuclear. See Mesablishvili.

Construction of nuclear adjunction

Let ℓ⊣r\ell \dashv r be an adjunction, inducing a monad T 1T_1. One can form the Eilenberg–Moore category of TT, which induces an adjunction f T 1⊣u T 1f_{T_1} \dashv u_{T_1}. This in turn induces a comonad D 2D_2 on the Eilenberg–Moore category. One can form the Eilenberg–Moore category of D 2D_2, which induces an adjunction u D 2⊣c D 2u_{D_2} \dashv c_{D_2}. This in turn induces a monad T 3T_3 on the Eilenberg–Moore of D 2D_2. We can then continue to iterate this construction, giving monads and comonads T 1,D 2,T 3,D 4,T 5,…T_1, D_2, T_3, D_4, T_5, \dots.

An obvious question is: does this process ever terminate? In unpublished work of Lack, it is claimed that this process terminates at T 3T_3. In other words, f T 3⊣u T 3f_{T_3} \dashv u_{T_3} is a nuclear adjunction. Thus T 3≃T 5≃…T_3 \simeq T_5 \simeq \dots and D 4≃D 6≃…D_4 \simeq D_6 \simeq \dots. Furthermore, if the domain of ℓ\ell is Cauchy complete, then it terminates at D 2D_2.

Under the simplifying assumptions of Cauchy completeness, this is proven in Pavlovic–Hughes. They observe that this construction forms a pseudo-idempotent 2-monad? on a 2-category of monads on CatCat.

Note that this construction is distinct from monadic decomposition, which instead constructs new monads from comparison functors, rather than (co)free–forgetful adjunctions.

Idea

In any cohesive ‚àû-topos, the shape modality  É may be interpreted as sending any cohesive space to its path ‚àû-groupoid. This follows by the defining adjunction  É‚ä£‚ô≠&#643; \dashv \flat from the interpretation of the flat modality as sending any delooping Bùí¢\mathbf{B} \mathcal{G} to the moduli stack for flat ‚àû-connections with structure ‚àû-group ùí¢\mathcal{G}:

because this identifies morphisms out of  ÉX&#643; X as higher parallel transport-functors for gauge group ùí¢\mathcal{G}.

Concretely, in the cohesive ‚àû-topos of smooth ‚àû-groupoids it is readily checked (dcct, see around here at smooth ‚àû-groupoid ‚Äì structures) that ‚ô≠Bùí¢\flat \mathbf{B}\mathcal{G} indeed classifies flat ‚àû-connections in the traditional sense.

It requires more work to show, even in this concrete smooth case, that  ÉX&#643; X is indeed modeled by a path ‚àû-groupoid in the traditional sense (say based on the idea of the smooth singular simplicial complex). That this is indeed the case is Prop. below, due to Dmitri Pavlov et al.

This fact has some remarkable consequences, which we develop further below.

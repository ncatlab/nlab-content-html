Idea

The recognition principle for finite relative loop spaces states that, for 1≤N<∞1 \leq N \lt \infty, a pair of pointed topological spaces is of the homotopy type of a relative NN-loop space pair of some (N−1)(N-1)-connected inclusion map if and only if it is a grouplike algebra over the Swiss cheese operad 𝒮𝒞 N\mathcal{SC}_N, in the sense of an algebra over an operad.

The recognition principle for infinite relative loop spaces states that a pair of pointed topological spaces is of the homotopy type of a relative ∞\infty-loop space pair of some 1-shifted morphism of connective spectra if and only if it is a grouplike algebra over 𝒮𝒞 ∞≔colim N→∞𝒮𝒞 N\mathcal{SC}_\infty\coloneqq \colim_{N\to\infty}\mathcal{SC}_N.

These are relative versions of May recognition theorem (May 72, May 74) of loop spaces.

The recognition principle for relative NN-loop spaces was proved in (Vieira 2020) for 3≤N≤∞3\leq N\leq \infty. The proof there works in the cases N=1,2N=1,2 if we assume the spaces connected. The general case for N=1N=1 was proved in (Hoefel, Livernet, Stasheff 2016). The unconnected case for N=2N=2 remains open.

Notoriously the loop space functors that appear in these recognition theorems are not right adjoint, thus they cannot be proved as an equivalence of homotopy categories induced by a Quillen equivalence. May’s original strategy is to define what essentially constitutes adjunction units up to resolutions induced by the two-sided bar construction. The strategy of (Vieira 2020) is to define a generalization of Quillen adjunction that allow for the units and counits to exist only up to functorially determined resolutions, providing a natural model theoretical axiomatization of the essential elements of May’s original proof. For the full proof, homotopical versions of the concepts of idempotent monad and idempotent adjunction require similar generalizations. This is because we are interested in the homotopy subcategories of grouplike algebras and (N−1)(N-1)-connected spaces/connective spectra.

Bellow the proof of the relative recognition of NN-loop spaces for 3≤N≤∞3\leq N\leq \infty using the machinery of idempotent quasiadjunctions in (Vieira 2020) is sketched.

Idempotent weak Quillen quasiadjunctions

Weak Quillen quasiadjunction

To construct the unit and counit natural transformations of an adjuction between homotopy categories of model categories it suffices to construct a unit natural span and counit natural cospan at the model categories level, plus some additional compatibility conditions.

Definition

(Vieira 2020, Definition 2.1.1) Let 𝒯\mathcal{T} and 𝒜\mathcal{A} be model categories. A weak Quillen quasiadjunction, or just quasiadjunction, between 𝒯\mathcal{T} and 𝒜\mathcal{A}, denoted by

(S⊣ 𝒞,ℱΛ):𝒯⇌𝒜, (S \dashv_{\ \mathscr{C},\mathscr{F}}\Lambda):\mathcal{T}\rightleftharpoons \mathcal{A},

is a quadruple of functors

𝒞:𝒯→𝒯,ℱ:𝒜→𝒜,S:𝒯→𝒜,Λ:𝒜→𝒯 \mathscr{C}:\mathcal{T} \to \mathcal{T} ,\quad \mathscr{F}:\mathcal{A} \to \mathcal{A} ,\quad S:\mathcal{T} \to \mathcal{A} ,\quad \Lambda:\mathcal{A} \to \mathcal{T}

with SS the left quasiadjoint and Λ\Lambda the right quasiadjoint, equipped with a natural span in 𝒯\mathcal{T} and a natural cospan in 𝒜\mathcal{A}

η′:𝒞⇒∼Id 𝒯,η:𝒞⇒ΛS,ϵ:SΛ⇒ℱ,ϵ′:Id 𝒜⇒∼ℱ \eta':\mathscr{C}\xRightarrow{\sim} Id_{\mathcal{T}} ,\quad \eta:\mathscr{C}\Rightarrow \Lambda S ,\qquad \epsilon:S \Lambda \Rightarrow \mathscr{F}, \quad \epsilon':Id_{\mathcal{A}}\xRightarrow{\sim}\mathscr{F}

such that

(i) SS is left derivable;

(ii) Λ\Lambda is right derivable;

(iii) 𝒞\mathscr{C} and ℱ\mathscr{F} preserve cofibrant and fibrant objects;

(iv) η′\eta' and ϵ′\epsilon' are natural weak equivalences;

(v) If X∈𝒯X\in\mathcal{T} is cofibrant then ϵ SXSη X≃ϵ′ SXSη X′\epsilon_{SX}S\eta_X\simeq \epsilon'_{SX}S\eta_X';

(vi) If Y∈𝒜Y\in\mathcal{A} is fibrant then Λϵ Yη ΛY≃Λϵ′ Yη′ ΛY\Lambda\epsilon_Y \eta_{\Lambda Y}\simeq \Lambda\epsilon'_Y\eta'_{\Lambda Y}.

If 𝒞\mathscr{C}, ℱ\mathscr{F}, η′\eta' and ϵ′\epsilon' are all identities and the homotopy equivalences in (v) and (vi) are equalities we recover the notion of a weak Quillen adjunction. If we further require SS to preserve cofibrations and Λ\Lambda to preserve fibrations we get a Quillen adjunction. Though the above information is weaker than that of a Quillen adjunction it suffices to construct an adjunction at the homotopy categories level.

Theorem

(Vieira 2020, Theorem 2.1.2) A quasiadjunction induces an adjunction

(𝕃S⊣ℝΛ):ℋo𝒯⇌ℋo𝒜 (\mathbb{L}S \dashv\mathbb{R}\Lambda):\mathcal{H}o \mathcal{T}\rightleftharpoons \mathcal{H}o \mathcal{A}

between the homotopy categories.

Idempotent quasi(co)monads

The following generalization of Quillen idempotent monads (see Bousfield-Friedlander theorem) was also introduced following the same principle of only requiring the existence of a unit natural span, and they also induce Bousfield localizations.

Definition

such that:

(i) η′\eta' is a natural weak equivalence;

(ii) QQ preserves weak equivalences;

(iii) QηQ\eta and η Q\eta_{Q} are natural weak equivalences;

The resulting homotopy category is the reflective subcategory

ℋo𝒯 Q:={X∈ℋo𝒯∣(i X:X→X⊔ 𝒞¯XQX)∈W}\mathcal{H}o \mathcal{T}_Q:=\{X\in \mathcal{H}o \mathcal{T}\mid (i_X:X\rightarrow X\sqcup_{\overline{\mathscr{C}} X}QX)\in W\}

The above definition can be dualized. The resulting idempotent quasicomonads induce right Bousfield localizations and associated coreflective homotopy subcategories.

A quasiadjunction (S⊣ 𝒞,ℱΛ):𝒯⇌𝒜(S\dashv_{\ \mathscr{C},\mathscr{F}}\Lambda):\mathcal{T}\rightleftharpoons \mathcal{A} induces the following natural span on 𝒯\mathcal{T} and natural cospan on 𝒜\mathcal{A}

Finite relative recognition theorem

In this section fix some 1≤N<∞1\leq N\lt \infty. Let 𝒮𝒞 N\mathcal{SC}_N be the cubical Swiss cheese relative operad, a colored operad over the colors {c,o}\{c, o\}, respectively referred to as the closed and open colors. It induces a monad SC NSC_N on the category Top * {c,o}Top_*^{\{c, o\}} of pairs of pointed topological spaces (see algebra over an operad). We denote pairs of pointed spaces as X=(X c,X o)X=(X_{c},X_{o}). Denote by 𝒮𝒞 N[Top]\mathcal{SC}_N[Top] the category of algebras over SC NSC_N, which we equip with the mixed model structure transferred from the one on Top * {c,o}Top_*^{\{c, o\}}.

Denote by Top * →Top_*^\rightarrow the category of pointed maps, equipped with the mixed projective model structure. We denote maps as Y=(Y:Y 0→Y 1)Y=(Y:Y_0\rightarrow Y_1).

Definition

The relative NN-loop pair functor is

Ω 2 N:Top * →→Top * {c,o},Ω 2 N(Y)≔(Y 1 𝕊 N,(Y 0× Y 1Y 1 I) 𝕊 N−1). \Omega^N_2:Top_*^{\rightarrow}\rightarrow Top_*^{\{c,o\}},\qquad \Omega^N_2(Y)\coloneqq(Y_1^{\mathbb{S}^N},(Y_0\times_{Y_1} Y_1^I)^{\mathbb{S}^{N-1}}).

The relative NN-suspension functor is

Σ → N:Top * {c,o}→Top * →,Σ → N(X)≔(X o∧𝕊 N−1→((X o∧I)∨(X c∧𝕊 1))∧𝕊 N−1). \Sigma^N_\to : Top_*^{\{c,o\}}\to Top_*^\to, \qquad \Sigma^N_\to(X)\coloneqq (X_{o} \wedge \mathbb{S}^{N-1} \to ((X_o \wedge I) \vee(X_c \wedge \mathbb{S}^1)) \wedge \mathbb{S}^{N-1} ).

Theorem

(Vieira 2020, Proposition 2.2.3) We have a weak Quillen adjunction

(Σ → N⊣Ω 2 N):Top * {c,o}⇋Top * →. (\Sigma^N_\to \dashv \Omega^N_2):Top_*^{\{c,o\}}\leftrightharpoons Top_*^\to.

This adjunction transfers a new model structure on Top * →Top_*^\to, with weak equivalences the commutative squares f∈Top * →(X,Y)f\in Top_*^\to(X,Y) such that f 0,*:π qX 0→π qY 0f_{0,*}:\pi_q X_0\to \pi_q Y_0 are isomorphisms for all q≥Nq\geq N and (f 0,f 1 I) *:π q(X 0× X 1X 1 I)→π q(Y 0× Y 1Y 1 I)(f_0,f_1^I)_*:\pi_q(X_0\times_{X_1}X_1^I)\to \pi_q(Y_0\times_{Y_1}Y_1^I) are isomorphisms for all q≥N−1q\geq N-1. All objects of Top * →Top_*^\to are fibrant in this model structure. We will say a pointed map Y∈Top * →Y\in Top_*^\to is (N−1)(N-1)-connected if Y 0Y_0 is (N−2)(N-2)-connected and Y 1Y_1 is (N−1)(N-1)-connected. The cofibrant objects are the Y∈Top * →Y\in Top_*^\to that are homotopy equivalent to (N−1)(N-1)-connected inclusions of relative CW-pairs. We denote the category of pointed maps equipped with this model structure as Top N−1 →Top^\to_{N-1}.

The images of Ω 2 N\Omega^N_2 are naturally algebras over 𝒮𝒞 N\mathcal{SC}_N, so we have an induced functor Ω 2 N:Top * →→𝒮𝒞 N[Top]\Omega^N_2:Top_*^\to\rightarrow \mathcal{SC}_N[Top]. This functor is not a right adjoint, but it does have a left weak Quillen quasiadjoint induced by the two-sided bar construction.

Definition

The relative NN-delooping functor is

B → N:𝒮𝒞 N[Top]→Top * →,B → N(X)≔B(Σ → N,SC N,X). B^N_\to:\mathcal{SC}_N[Top]\to Top_*^\to,\qquad B^N_\to(X)\coloneqq B(\Sigma^N_\to,SC_N,X).

Definition

The resolution of 𝒮𝒞 N\mathcal{SC}_N-spaces functor is

B¯ 2:𝒮𝒞 N[Top]→𝒮𝒞 N[Top],B¯ 2(X)=B(SC N,SC N,X). \overline{B}_2:\mathcal{SC}_N[Top]\to\mathcal{SC}_N[Top],\qquad \overline{B}_2(X)=B(SC_N,SC_N,X).

Theorem

(Vieira 2020, Theorem 4.3.5, Theorem 4.3.8) We have an idempotent weak Quillen quasiadjunction

(B → N⊣ B¯ 2,Id Top * →Ω 2 N):𝒮𝒞 N[Top]⇋Top N−1 →. (B^N_\to\dashv_{\ \overline{B}_2,Id_{Top_*^\to}}\Omega^N_2):\mathcal{SC}_N[Top]\leftrightharpoons Top^\to_{N-1}.

If N≥3N\geq 3 then this quasiadjunction induces an equivalence

(𝕃B → N⊣ℝΩ 2 N):ℋo𝒮𝒞 N[Top] grp⇋ℋoTop N−1 → (\mathbb{L}B^N_\to\dashv\mathbb{R}\Omega^N_2):\mathcal{H}o\mathcal{SC}_N[Top]_{grp}\leftrightharpoons \mathcal{H}o Top^\to_{N-1}

between the homotopy subcategory of grouplike Swiss cheese algebras and the homotopy category of (N−1)(N-1)-connected pointed maps.

Remark

In the reference Vieira 2020 a cofibrant resolution of 𝒮𝒞 N\mathcal{SC}_N is used. The fact that 𝒮𝒞 N\mathcal{SC}_N is a Σ\Sigma-cofibrant operad (see model structure on operads) when we consider the mixed model structure of collections means this assumption is not necessary.

Remark

The reason the proof of the above theorem doesn’t extend to the cases N=1,2N=1,2 is due to the fact that the constructed unit of the quasiadjunction is induced by a natural map α:SC N⇒Ω 2 NΣ → N\alpha:SC_N\Rightarrow \Omega^N_2\Sigma^N_\to which is a natural pair of group completions if and only if N≥3N\geq 3.

Infinite relative recognition theorem

Let SpSp be the category of sequential spectra, and Sp ↗Sp^{\nearrow} the category composed of pairs of spectra Y 0,Y 1∈SpY_0,Y_1\in Sp equipped with a 1-shifted spectra map Y:Y 0→Y 1[1]Y:Y_0\to Y_1[1]. Morphisms are pairs of spectra maps that commute with the shifted spectra maps in the appropriate sense. It admits a strict mixed model structure with weak equivalences the level-wise weak homotopy equivalences and with fibrations the level-wise Hurewicz fibrations.

Definition

The spectrification functor is

Ω˜:Sp→Sp,Ω˜(Y) •≔colim q→∞Y˜ •+q 𝕊 q \widetilde{\Omega}:Sp\to Sp,\qquad \widetilde{\Omega}(Y)_\bullet\coloneqq \colim_{q\to\infty} \widetilde Y_{\bullet+q}^{\mathbb{S}^q}

where Y˜\widetilde{Y} is a certain inclusion prespectrum constructed from YY, equipped with a quotient map Y→Y˜Y\to \widetilde{Y} (LMS 86, Appendix 1). If YY is already an inclusion spectrum then Y˜=Y\widetilde Y=Y.

This functor induces a spectrification functor

Ω˜ ↗:Sp ↗→Sp ↗,Ω˜ ↗(Y)≔(Ω˜(Y):Ω˜(Y 0)→Ω˜(Y 1)[1]). \widetilde{\Omega}_{\nearrow}:Sp^{\nearrow}\to Sp^{\nearrow} ,\qquad \widetilde{\Omega}_{\nearrow}(Y)\coloneqq(\widetilde{\Omega}(Y):\widetilde{\Omega}(Y_0)\to \widetilde{\Omega}(Y_1)[1]).

We have a natural stable weak homotopy equivalence ϵ′:Id Sp ↗⇒Ω˜ ↗\epsilon':Id_{Sp^{\nearrow}}\Rightarrow\widetilde{\Omega}_{\nearrow} which gives a Quillen idempotent monad structure on Ω˜ ↗\widetilde{\Omega}_{\nearrow}. The stable mixed model structure on Sp ↗Sp^{\nearrow} is the one induced by this idempotent monad, which is the left Bousfield localization of the strict mixed model structure on the pairs of stable weak homotopy equivalences. The fibrant objects in this model structure are the Omega-spectra.

The relative ∞\infty-loop pair of spaces functor is

Ω 2 ∞:=Λ 2 ∞Ω˜ ↗:Sp ↗→Top * {c,o}.\Omega^\infty_2:=\Lambda^\infty_2\widetilde{\Omega}_{\nearrow}:Sp^{\nearrow}\to Top_*^{\{c,o\}}.

The images of Ω 2 ∞\Omega^\infty_2 are naturally algebras over 𝒮𝒞 ∞\mathcal{SC}_\infty, so we have an induced functor Ω 2 ∞:Sp ↗→𝒮𝒞 ∞[Top]\Omega^\infty_2:Sp^{\nearrow}\rightarrow \mathcal{SC}_\infty[Top].

The resolution of 𝒮𝒞 ∞\mathcal{SC}_\infty-spaces functor is

B¯ 2:𝒮𝒞 ∞[Top]→𝒮𝒞 ∞[Top],B¯ 2(X)=B(SC ∞,SC ∞,X). \overline{B}_2:\mathcal{SC}_\infty[Top]\to\mathcal{SC}_\infty[Top],\qquad \overline{B}_2(X)=B(SC_\infty,SC_\infty,X).

Theorem

(Vieira 2020, Theorem 4.3.5, Theorem 4.3.8) We have an idempotent weak Quillen quasiadjunction

(B ↗ ∞⊣ B¯ 2,Ω˜ ↗Ω 2 ∞):𝒮𝒞 ∞[Top]⇋Sp ↗ (B^\infty_\nearrow\dashv_{\ \overline{B}_2,\widetilde{\Omega}_\nearrow}\Omega^\infty_2):\mathcal{SC}_\infty[Top]\leftrightharpoons Sp^\nearrow

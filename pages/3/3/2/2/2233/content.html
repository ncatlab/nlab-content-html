Idea

Extended quantum field theory (or multi-tiered quantum field theory) is the fully local formulation of functorial quantum field theory, formulated in higher category theory

Whereas a

  • 1-categoricalTQFT may be regarded as a rule that allows one to compute topological invariants Z(Œ£)Z(\Sigma) assigned to dd-dimensional manifolds by cutting these manifolds into a sequence {Œ£ i}\{\Sigma_i\} of dd-dimensional composable cobordisms with (d‚àí1)(d-1)-dimensional boundaries ‚àÇŒ£ i\partial \Sigma_i, e.g. Œ£=Œ£ 2‚àê ‚àÇŒ£ 1=‚àÇŒ£ 2Œ£ 1\Sigma = \Sigma_2 \coprod_{\partial \Sigma_1 = \partial \Sigma_2} \Sigma_1, then assigning quantities Z(Œ£ i)Z(\Sigma_i) to each of these and then composing these quantities in some way, e.g. as Z(Œ£)=Z(Œ£ 2)‚àòZ(Œ£ 1)Z(\Sigma) = Z(\Sigma_2)\circ Z(\Sigma_1);

we have that

  • in extended TQFT Z(Œ£)Z(\Sigma) may be computed by decomposing Œ£\Sigma into dd-dimensional pieces with piecewise smooth boundaries, whose boundary strata are of arbitrary codimension kk.

For that reason extended QFT is also sometimes called local or localized QFT. In fact, the notion of locality in quantum field theory is precisely this notion of locality. And, as also discussed at FQFT, this higher dimensional version of locality is naturally encoded in terms of n-functoriality of ZZ regarded as a functor on a higher category of cobordisms.

Definition

The category of extended cobordisms

The definition of a jj-cobordism is recursive. A (j+1)(j+1)-cobordism between jj-cobordisms is a compact oriented (j+1)(j+1)-dimensional smooth manifold with corners whose the boundary is the disjoint union of the target jj-cobordism and the orientation reversal of the source jj-cobordism. (The base case of the recursion is the empty set, thought of as a (‚àí1)(-1)-dimensional manifold.)

nCob dn Cob_d is an nn-category with smooth compact oriented (d‚àín)(d-n)-manifolds as objects and cobordisms of cobordisms up to nn-cobordisms, up to diffeomorphism, as morphisms.

There are various suggestions with more or less detail for a precise definition of a higher category nCob nn Cob_n of fully extended nn-dimensional cobordisms.

A very general (and very natural) one consists in taking a further step in categorification: one takes nn-cobordisms as nn-morphisms and smooth homotopy classes of diffeomorphisms beween them as (n+1)(n+1)-morphisms. Next one iterates this; see details at (‚àû,n)-category of cobordisms.

Extended TQFT

Fix a base ring RR, and let CC be the symmetric monoidal nn-category of nn-RR-modules.

An nn-extended CC-valued TQFT of dimension dd is a symmetric nn-tensor functor Z:nCob d‚ÜíCZ: n Cob_d \rightarrow C that maps

  • smooth compact oriented dd-manifolds to elements of RR

  • smooth compact oriented (d‚àí1)(d-1)-manifolds to RR-modules

  • cobordisms of smooth compact oriented (d‚àí1)(d-1)-manifolds to RR-linear maps between RR-modules

  • smooth compact oriented (d‚àí2)(d-2)-manifolds to RR-linear additive categories

  • cobordisms of smooth compact oriented (d‚àí2)(d-2)-manifolds to functors between RR-linear categories

  • etc ‚Ä¶

  • smooth compact oriented (d‚àín)(d-n)-manifolds to RR-linear (n‚àí1)(n-1)-categories

  • cobordisms of smooth compact oriented (d‚àín)(d-n)-manifolds to (n‚àí1)(n-1)-functors between RR-linear (n‚àí1)(n-1)-categories

with compatibility conditions and gluing formulas that must be satisfied‚Ä¶ For instance, since the functor ZZ is required to be monoidal, it sends monoidal units to monoidal units. Therefore, the dd-dimensional vacuum is mapped to the unit element of RR, the (d‚àí1)(d-1)-dimensional vacuum to the RR-module RR, the (d‚àí2)(d-2)-dimensional vacuum to the category of RR-modules, etc.

Here nn can range between 00 and dd. This generalizes to an arbitrary symmetric monoidal category CC as codomain category.

Classification of ETQFT‚Äôs

Assume Z:nCob d‚ÜínVect(R)Z: n Cob_d \rightarrow n Vect(R) is an extended TQFT. Since ZZ maps the (d‚àí1)(d-1)-dimensional vacuum to RR as an RR-vector space, by functoriality ZZ is forced to map a dd-dimensional closed manifold to an element of RR. Iterating this argument, one is naturally led to conjecture that, under the correct categorical hypothesis, the behaviour of ZZ is enterely determined by its behaviour on (d‚àín)(d-n)-dimensional manifolds. See details at cobordism hypothesis.

Idea

Parastatistics is an exotic kind of particle statistics where permutations of ‚Äúindistinguishable particles‚Äù transform their space of quantum states by higher dimensional irreducible linear representations of the symmetric group (Messiah & Greenberg 1964, cf. Halvorson 2007 ¬ß14.4.2-4, Wang & Hazzard 2025, below (4)).

In generality of its transformation properties, parastatistics is in-between ordinary boson/fermion-statistics and the yet more general braid group statistics. But while braid group statistics is usually associated only with particles (‚Äúanyons‚Äù) that are restricted to an effectively 2-dimensional surface, parastatistics ‚Äì to the extent that it applies at all ‚Äì is thought to apply in principle in any dimension. (Concretely for d=3d = 3 see Freedman, Hastings, Nayak, Qi, Walker & Wang 2011)

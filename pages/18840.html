Idea

In probability theory, the concept of noncommutative probability space or quantum probability space is the generalization of that of probability space as the concept of ‚Äúspace‚Äù is generalized to non-commutative geometry.

The basic idea is to encode a would-be probability space dually in its algebra of functions ùíú\mathcal{A}, typically regarded as a star algebra, and encode the probability measure as a state on this star algebra

Often ùíú\mathcal{A} is assumed/required to be a von Neumann algebra (e.g. Kuperberg 05, section 1.8). Often ùíú\mathcal{A} is taken to be the full algebra of bounded operators on some Hilbert space (e.g. Attal, def. 7.1).

The formulation of non-perturbative quantum field theory from the algebraic perspective of quantum probability is known as algebraic quantum field theory (AQFT).

The formulation of perturbative quantum field theory from the algebraic perspective of quantum probability is known as perturbative algebraic quantum field theory (pAQFT).

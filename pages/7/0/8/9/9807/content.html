The Cartan model

The Cartan model follows from the Weil model above by algebraically solving the horizontality constraint (9). This we discuss first below. Then we describe the resulting dgc-algebra further below.

Equivariant de Rham theorem

The point of the above dgc-algebra models is that, under suitable conditions, their cochain cohomology computes the real cohomology of the homotopy type of the homotopy quotient X‚´ΩHX \sslash H, which, as an actual topological space, may be presented by the Borel construction X√ó GEGX \times_G E G, hence the Borel equivariant de Rham cohomology of XX.

This is the equivariant cohomology-generalization of the plain de Rham theorem:

Proposition

(equivariant de Rham theorem)

  1. GG be a Lie group which is

    1. compact;

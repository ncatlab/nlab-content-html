Definition

Definition

An (‚àû,1)-topos is hypercomplete if the Whitehead theorem is valid in it.

Equivalently: if all its object are hypercomplete objects.

Properties

All geometric morphisms X‚ÜíYX \to Y out of a hypercomplete (‚àû,1)(\infty,1)-topos XX factor through the hypercompletion Y^\hat Y of YY:

Proposition

Every (‚àû,1)-topos which is locally of homotopy dimension ‚â§n\leq n for some finite n‚â•‚àí1n\geq -1 is hypercomplete.

See the discussion at homotopy dimension for details and further implications.

Examples

Hypercompletions

Every (‚àû,1)-topos has a hypercompletion, given by the full reflective sub-(‚àû,1)-category spanned by its hypercomplete objects.

Remark

There exist 1-sites CC such that the (1,1)-topos of sheaves of sets on CC has enough points in the 1-topos sense, but such that the corresponding 1-localic (‚àû,1)-topos ùí≥\mathcal{X} does not have enough points in the sense of def. . An example is given by the site of open subsets of the topological space ‚àè ‚Ñï{x,z,y}\prod_{\mathbb{N}} \{x,z,y\} where the topology on {x,z,y}\{x,z,y\} is generated by the two open subsets {x,y}\{x,y\} and {x,z}\{x,z\}. See HTT, Remark 6.5.4.7.

However, the hypercompletion ùí≥ ‚àß\mathcal{X}^\wedge of ùí≥\mathcal{X} will have enough points. This follows from the fact that ùí≥ ‚àß\mathcal{X}^\wedge may be presented by the Jardine model structure on simplicial presheaves on the given site of definition, and that in the presence of enough 1-topos points the weak equivalences of that model structure are the stalk-wise weak equivalences in the model structure on simplicial sets. (See at model structure on simplicial presheaves for details.)

Observation

An (‚àû,1)(\infty,1)-topos that has enough points is hypercomplete. WARNING: See the remark above.

This is HTT, remark 6.5.4.7. See [SAG, A.4.0.5] for the converse: If an ‚àû-topos is hypercomplete and locally coherent, then it has enough points.

Proof

Recall from def. that a point of an (‚àû,1)-topos H\mathbf{H} is a geometric morphism p:‚àûGrpd‚Üíp *‚Üêp *Hp : \infty Grpd \stackrel{\overset{p^*}{\leftarrow}}{\underset{p_*}{\to}} \mathbf{H}.

And by definition H\mathbf{H} has enough points if a morphism F:X‚ÜíYF : X \to Y in H\mathbf{H} is an equivalence if for all points pp, the stalk p *fp^* f is an equivalence in ‚àûGrpd.

But if ff is ‚àû\infty-connected in H\mathbf{H}, then so is p *fp^* f in ‚àûGrpd, which is hypercomplete, by Whitehead's theorem, so that p *fp^* f is an equivalence.

So in an (‚àû,1)(\infty,1)-topos with enough points all ‚àû\infty-connected morphisms are equivalences.

Non-examples

Goodwillie nn-jet toposes

For n>1n \gt 1 the Goodwillie n-jet (‚àû,1)-toposes are generically far from being hypercomplete.

Models

In terms of model category theory

Hypercomplete ‚àû-stack (‚àû,1)-toposes are precisely those that are presented by the local Joyal-Jardine model structure on simplicial presheaves, where weak equivalences of simplicial presheaves are those morphisms that induce isomorphisms on homotopy sheaves. In these models the fibrant objects are those simplical presheaves that satisfy descent over all hypercovers.

If the underlying ordinary sheaf topos has enough points, then this are equivalently the morphisms that induce stalkwise weak equivalences in the standard model structure on simplicial sets.

Contrary to that one can consider local models by left Bousfield localization of the global model structure on simplicial presheaves only at Cech covers. This yield in general a non-hypercomplete (‚àû,1)-categories of (‚àû,1)-sheaves.

Proposition

For CC site, write [C op,sSet] inj,loc[C^{op}, sSet]_{inj,loc} for the Joyal-Jardine local model structure on simplicial presheaves (whose weak equivalence are morphisms that induce isomorphisms on homotopy-sheaves).

The (‚àû,1)(\infty,1)-topos that this presents is the hypercompletion of the (‚àû,1)-category of (‚àû,1)-sheaves on CC:

([C op,sSet] inj,loc) ‚àò‚âÉSh (‚àû,1)(C)^. ([C^{op}, sSet]_{inj,loc})^\circ \simeq \widehat{ Sh_{(\infty,1)}(C)} \,.

This is HTT, prop. 6.5.2.14.

Proof

The strategy is to form the localization in a 2-step process, where we first just form the Cech-localization, and then from that the full hypercompletion. For that notice that among the weak equivalences in the Joyal-Jardine local model structure on simplicial presheaves are in particular the ordinary covering sieves S({U i})‚Ü™j(U)S(\{U_i\}) \hookrightarrow j(U) (here jj is the Yoneda embedding) associated with a coverin family {U i‚ÜíU}\{U_i \to U\} in the site CC:

since CC is an ordinary category, the simplicial presheaves S({U i})S(\{U_i\}) and j(U)j(U) have vanishing presheaves of homotopy groups in positive degree, while they coindide with their œÄ 0\pi_0-presheaves. Since the sheafification of S({U i})S(\{U_i\}) is isomorphic to j(U)j(U), by definition, it follows that the same holds for the œÄ 0\pi_0-presheaves and trivially for the œÄ n\pi_n-presheaves. So S({U i})‚Üíj(U)S(\{U_i\}) \to j(U) is a Joyal-Jardine weak equivalence.

We will now first localize with respect to these morphisms to obtain the Cech-localization whose fibrant objects are (‚àû,1)-sheaves. The point is that on these fibrant objects then, the Joyal-Jardine sheaves of homotopy groups can be seen to coincide with the (‚àû,1)-categorical homotopy sheaves in terms of which hypercompletion is defined.

In classical topos theory

In classical topos theory literature frequently simplicial objects in an ordinary topos are considered, with acyclic fibrations taken to be those morphisms Y ‚Ä¢‚ÜíX ‚Ä¢Y_\bullet \to X_\bullet such that for all horn inclusions the induced morphism

(X Œî[n]‚ÜíX ‚àÇŒî[n]√ó Y ‚àÇŒî[n]Y Œî[n]) (X^{\Delta[n]} \to X^{\partial \Delta[n]} \times_{Y^{\partial \Delta[n]}} Y^{\Delta[n]})

is an epimorphism in the topos.

See for instance page 17 of

  • Ieke Moerdijk, Classifying spaces and classifying topoi (LNM 1616)

(and it looks like this is the discussion planned for part E of the Elephant).

For sheaf toposes epimorphism means stalk-wise epimorphism. Therefore this amounts to using on simplicial sheaves the structure of a category of fibrant objects as defined in BrownAHT, where acyclic fibrations are the stalkwise acyclic Kan fibrations.

The homotopy category of this homotopical category is the same as that of the Joyal-Jardine model structure on simplicial presheaves in the presence of enough points (since in both cases weak equivalences are the stalkwise weak equivalences), hence is the same as the homotopy category of the hypercomplete (‚àû,1)-topos.

For more discussion of how this classical definition interplays with other definitions see also homotopy groups in an (‚àû,1)-topos.

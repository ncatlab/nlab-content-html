Idea

One usually defines cohomology with respect to some coefficient objects:

  • For group cohomology of a group GG, the coefficients come from a (left) GG-module.
  • For Lie algebra cohomology of a Lie algebra ğ”¤\mathfrak{g}, the coefficients come from a (left) ğ”¤\mathfrak{g}-module.
  • For Hochschild cohomology of an associative algebra AA, the coefficients come from an AA-bimodule.

Beck modules are a simultaneous generalisation of all three types of module.

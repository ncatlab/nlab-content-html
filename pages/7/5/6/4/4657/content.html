Idea

For {U i‚ÜíX}\{U_i \to X\} a cover of a space XX, the corresponding ƒåech groupoid is the internal groupoid

C({U i})=(‚àê ijU i‚à©U j‚áâ‚àê iU i) C(\{U_i\}) = (\coprod_{i j} U_i \cap U_j \rightrightarrows \coprod_i U_i)

whose set of objects is the disjoint union ‚àê iU i\coprod_i U_i of the covering patches, and the set of morphisms is the disjoint union of the intersections U i‚à©U jU_i \cap U_j of these patches.

This is the 22-coskeleton of the full ƒåech nerve. See there for more details.

If we speak about generalized points of the U iU_i (which are often just ordinary points, in applications), then

  • there is a unique morphism (x,i)‚Üí(x,j)(x,i) \to (x,j) for all pairs of objects labeled by the same xx such that x‚ààU i‚à©U jx \in U_i \cap U_j;

Codescent

We discuss (Prop. below) how the Cech groupoid co-represents matching families as they appear in the definition of sheaves.

For reference, we first recall that definition:

The hom-groupoid is computed as the end

[ùíû op,Grpd](C({U i} i),Y)=‚à´ V‚ààùíû[C({U i} i)(V),Y(V)], [\mathcal{C}^{op},Grpd] \left( C\left(\{U_i\}_i\right), \, \mathbf{Y} \right) \;=\; \int_{V \in \mathcal{C}} \left[ C\left(\{U_i\}_i\right)(V), \, \mathbf{Y}(V) \right] \,,

where the ‚Äúintegrand‚Äù is the functor category (here: a groupoid) from the Cech groupoid at a given VV to the set (regarded as a groupoid) assigned by Y\mathbf{Y} to VV.

Since Y(V)\mathbf{Y}(V) is just a set, that functor groupoid, too, is just a set, regarded as a groupoid. Its elements are the functors C({U i} i)(V)‚ü∂Y(V)C\left(\{U_i\}_i\right)(V) \longrightarrow \mathbf{Y}(V), which are equivalently those functions on sets of objects

‚àêiy(U i)(V)=Obj C({U i} i)(V)‚ü∂Obj Y(V)=Y(V) \underset{i}{\coprod} y(U_i)(V) = Obj_{C\left(\{U_i\}_i\right)(V)} \longrightarrow Obj_{\mathbf{Y}(V)} = \mathbf{Y}(V)

Hence the hom-groupoid is a subset of the end of these function sets:

‚à´ V‚ààùíû[C({U i} i)(V),Y(V)] ‚Ü™‚à´ V‚ààùíû[‚àêiy(U i)(V),Y(V)] ‚âÉ‚à´ V‚ààùíû‚àèi[y(U i)(V),Y(V)] ‚âÉ‚àèi‚à´ V‚ààùíû[y(U i)(V),Y(V)] ‚âÉ‚àèiY(U i) \begin{aligned} \int_{V \in \mathcal{C}} \left[ C\left(\{U_i\}_i\right)(V), \, \mathbf{Y}(V) \right] & \hookrightarrow \int_{V \in \mathcal{C}} \left[ \underset{i}{\coprod} y(U_i)(V), \, \mathbf{Y}(V) \right] \\ & \simeq \int_{V \in \mathcal{C}} \underset{i}{\prod} \left[ y(U_i)(V), \, \mathbf{Y}(V) \right] \\ & \simeq \underset{i}{\prod} \int_{V \in \mathcal{C}} \left[ y(U_i)(V), \, \mathbf{Y}(V) \right] \\ & \simeq \underset{i}{\prod} \mathbf{Y}(U_i) \end{aligned}

The end result is hence the same Cartesian product set that also the set of matching families is defined to be a subset of, in (4).

This shows that an element in ‚à´ V‚ààùíû[C({U i} i)(V),Y(V)] \int_{V \in \mathcal{C}} \left[ C\left(\{U_i\}_i\right)(V), \, \mathbf{Y}(V) \right] is a tuple (œï i‚ààY(U i)) i(\phi_i \in \mathbf{Y}(U_i))_i, subject to some condition. Examples

For XX a smooth manifold and {U i‚ÜíX}\{U_i \to X\} an atlas by coordinate charts, the ƒåech groupoid is a Lie groupoid which is equivalent to XX as a Lie groupoid: C({U i})‚Üí‚âÉXC(\{U_i\}) \stackrel{\simeq}{\to} X

For BG\mathbf{B}G the Lie groupoid with one object coming from a Lie group GG morphisms of Lie groupoids of the form

C({U i}) ‚Üíg BG ‚Üì ‚âÉ X \array{ C(\{U_i\}) &\stackrel{g}{\to}& \mathbf{B}G \\ \downarrow^{\mathrlap{\simeq}} \\ X }

Summary

General idea

Recall the following familiar 1-categorical statement:

  • Working in the 1-category Set of 0-categories is the same as doing set theory. The point of categories and sheaves is to pass to parameterized 0-categories, namely presheaf categories: these topoi behave much like the category Set but their objects are generalized spaces that may carry more structure, for instance they may be generalized smooth spaces if one considers (pre)sheaves on Diff.

One can think of Lurie‚Äôs book as a comprehensive study of the generalization of the above statement from 11 to (‚àû,1)(\infty,1) (recall the notion of (n,r)-category):

  • Working in the (‚àû,1)(\infty,1)-category ‚àûGrpd of (‚àû,0)-categories is the same as doing topology. The point of ‚àû-stacks is to pass to parameterized (‚àû,0)-categories, namely (‚àû,1)-presheaf categories: these (‚àû,1)-topoi behave much like the (‚àû,1)(\infty,1)category ‚àûGrpd but their objects are generalized spaces with higher homotopies that may carry more structure, for instance they may be ‚àû\infty- differentiable stack if one considers ‚àû-stacks on Diff.

First part, sections 1-4

Based on work by Andr√© Joyal on the quasi-category model for (‚àû,1)-categories, Lurie presents a comprehensive account of the theory of (‚àû,1)-categories including the definitions and properties of all the standard items familiar from category theory (limits, fibrations, etc.)

Second part, sections 5-7

Given the (‚àû,1)(\infty,1)-categorical machinery from the first part there are natural (‚àû,1)(\infty,1)-categorical versions of (‚àû,1)(\infty,1)-presheaf and (‚àû,1)(\infty,1)-sheaf categories (i.e. (‚àû,1)(\infty,1)-categories of ‚àû-stacks): the ‚Äú‚àû\infty-topoi‚Äù that give the book its title (more descriptively, these would be called ‚ÄúGrothendieck (‚àû,1)(\infty,1)-topoi‚Äù). Lurie investigates their properties in great detail and thereby in particular puts the work by Brown, Joyal, Jardine, To√´n on the model structure on simplicial presheaves into a coherent (‚àû,1)(\infty,1)-categorical context by showing that, indeed, these are models for ‚àû-stack (‚àû,1)-toposes.

How to read the book

1-categorical background

The book Higher topos theory together with Lurie‚Äôs work on Stable ‚àû-Categories is close to an (‚àû,1)(\infty,1)-categorical analog of the 1-categorical material as presented for instance in

Idea

The (co)bordism ring Œ© *=‚äï n‚â•0Œ© n\Omega_*=\oplus_{n\geq 0}\Omega_n is the graded ring whose

  • degree nn elements are classes of nn-dimensional smooth manifolds modulo cobordism;

  • product operation is given by the Cartesian product of manifolds;

  • addition operation is given by the disjoint union of manifolds.

Instead of bare manifolds one may consider manifolds with extra structure, such as orientation, spin structure, string structure, etc. and accordingly there is

  • the oriented cobordism ring Œ© * SO\Omega^{SO}_*,

  • the spin cobordism ring Œ© * Spin\Omega^{Spin}_*,

etc.

In this general context the bare cobordism ring is also denoted Œ© * O\Omega^O_* or Œ© * un\Omega^{un}_*, for emphasis.

A ring homomorphism out of the cobordism ring is a (multiplicative) genus.

Relation to cohomotopy

Let XX be a smooth manifold of dimension n‚àà‚Ñïn \in \mathbb{N} and let k‚â§nk \leq n. Then the Pontryagin-Thom construction induces a bijection

[X,S k]‚ü∂‚âÉŒ© n‚àík(X) [X, S^k] \overset{\simeq}{\longrightarrow} \Omega^{n-k}(X)

from the cohomotopy sets of XX to the cobordism group of (n‚àík)(n-k)-dimensional submanifolds with normal framing up to normally framed cobordism.

In particular, the natural group structure on cobordism group (essentially given by disjoint union of submanifolds) this way induces a group structure on the cohomotopy sets.

This is made explicit for instance in Kosinski 93, chapter IX.

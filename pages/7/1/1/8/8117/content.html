Idea

When symplectic geometry is used to model mechanics in physics, then a symplectic manifold (X,œâ)(X,\omega) encodes the phase space of a mechanical system and a symplectomorphism

œï:(X 1,œâ 1)‚Üí(X 2,œâ 2) \phi \;\colon\; (X_1,\omega_1) \to (X_2, \omega_2)

In other words, a symplectomorphism œï\phi as above constitutes a Lagrangian correspondence between (X 1,œâ 1)(X_1,\omega_1) and (X 2,œâ 2)(X_2, \omega_2). See for instance (Cattaneo-Mnev-Reshetikhin 12) for a review.

This suggests that instead of the category whose objects are symplectic manifolds and whose morphisms are symplectomorphisms, one might consider a kind of category of correspondences whose objects are symplectic manifolds, and whose morphisms include Lagrangian correspondences, so that composition is given by forming the fiber product along adjacent legs of correspondences.

Alan Weinstein called this would-be category the symplectic category and suggested that it is the natural domain for geometric quantization.

However, take at face value, symplectic manifolds with Lagrangian correspondences between them do not quite form a category, since the usual composition is only well-defined when the intersection of L 1√óL 2‚à©X 1√óŒî(X 2)√óX 3L_1 \times L_2 \cap X_1 \times \Delta(X_2) \times X_3 is transverse.

Proposals for how to rectify this are in (Wehrheim-Woodward) and in (Kitchloo) (by turning this into an (infinity,1)-category).

Definition

Let 𝒞\mathcal{C} be a fusion category. The vector space

Tube(𝒞)≔⨁ a,b,c∈ℐ𝒞(c⊗a,b⊗c), Tube (\mathcal{C}) \coloneqq \bigoplus_{a,b,c \in \mathcal{I}} \mathcal{C}(c \otimes a, b \otimes c),

for ℐ\mathcal{I} the set of simple objects of 𝒞\mathcal{C}, has an algebra structure. This is called the Tube (or Ocneanu) algebra. For ϵ∈𝒞(x⊗a,a⊗y)\epsilon \in \mathcal{C}(x \otimes a, a \otimes y), ϵ′∈𝒞(x′⊗a′,a′⊗y′)\epsilon' \in \mathcal{C}(x' \otimes a', a' \otimes y'), their product is given by

ϵ⋅ϵ′=δ y,x′∑ b,α(α⊗id y′)(id a⊗ϵ′)(ϵ⊗id a′)(id x⊗α *), \epsilon \cdot \epsilon' = \delta_{y,x'} \sum_{b,\alpha} (\alpha \otimes \text{id}_{y'}) (\text{id}_a \otimes \epsilon') (\epsilon \otimes \text{id}_{a'}) (\text{id}_x \otimes \alpha^* ),

for α∈Hom(a⊗a′,b)\alpha \in \text{Hom}(a \otimes a', b) (Ocneanu 94). It is furthermore a C-star-algebra (Ocneanu 01), and even a weak Hopf algebra (Müger 03, Jia et al 24).

Properties

Relation to the Drinfeld center

The category of representations of the tube algebra Tube(𝒞)Tube(\mathcal{C}) of 𝒞\mathcal{C} is equivalent to the Drinfeld center 𝒵(𝒞)\mathcal{Z}(\mathcal{C}) of 𝒞\mathcal{C} (Müger 03).

Relation to action on Hilbert spaces

In the context of a 2d CFT, twist fields are thought of as elements of a twist Hilbert space ℋ a\mathcal{H}_a for a∈ℐa \in \mathcal{I}. The action of b∈ℐb\in \mathcal{I} (essentially by conjugation) is described by an element x∈𝒞(b⊗a,c⊗b)x\in \mathcal{C}( b\otimes a, c \otimes b). Thus, the tube algebra Tube(𝒞)Tube(\mathcal{C}) acts on the total Hilbert space ℋ=⨁ a∈ℐℋ a\mathcal{H} = \bigoplus_{a\in \mathcal{I}} \mathcal{H}_a, meaning the latter is a representation of the tube algebra, and by the equivalence above is identified with an object in the Drinfeld center 𝒵(𝒞)\mathcal{Z}(\mathcal{C}) of 𝒞\mathcal{C}. See e.g. (Lin et al. 23).

Idea

Recall first that a (topological) line bundle over a (topological) field (or more generally a (topological) ring) 𝕂\mathbb{K} is, by definition, a rank=1 𝕂\mathbb{K}-vector bundle and hence a 𝕂\mathbb{K}-fiber bundle with structure group the group of units GL(1,𝕂)≃𝕂 ×GL(1,\mathbb{K}) \simeq \mathbb{K}^\times. It is then typically a theorem that (over paracompact topological spaces) such fiber bundles all arise, up to isomorphism, as pullbacks of the tautological line bundle on infinite projective space 𝕂P ∞\mathbb{K}P^\infty, so that their isomorphism classes are identified with the homotopy classes of continuous maps to this classifying space 𝕂P ∞\mathbb{K}P^\infty.

Now, the octonions 𝕆\mathbb{O} are not an (associative) ring and so a would-be group of units “𝕆 ×\mathbb{O}^\times” which could serve as the structure group of 𝕆\mathbb{O}-fiber bundles does not exist. This means that the usual definition of 𝕂\mathbb{K}-line bundles does not generalize to 𝕂≔𝕆\mathbb{K} \coloneqq \mathbb{O}.

However, the notion of a tautological line bundle over the octonionic projective line 𝕆P 1\mathbb{O}P^1 still does make sense, and this (rank=8 real vector bundle) is commonly called the tautological octonionic line bundle or the “canonical octonionic line bundle”.

By the above analogy it is then justified (though not common in the literature) to define general octonionic line bundles to be, up to isomorphism, the pullback bundles of this tautological one along continuous maps to 𝕆 P 1 \mathbb{O}P^1 .

Over ordinary rings

For RR a commutative ring, an associative unital RR-algebra is equivalently:

  • a monoid internal to the category R Mod R Mod of RR-modules equipped with the tensor product of modules âŠ—\otimes;

  • a pointed single-object category enriched over (RMod,âŠ—)(R Mod, \otimes);

  • a ring AA under RR such that the corresponding map Râ†’AR \to A lands in the center of AA.

If there is no danger for confusion, one often says simply â€˜associative algebraâ€™, or even only â€˜algebraâ€™.

More generally:

  • a (merely) associative algebra need not have a unit i:Râ†’Ai \colon R \to A; that is, it is a semigroup instead of a monoid;

  • an R R -ring is a monoid object in the category R BiMod R BiMod of RR-bimodules equipped with, crucially, the tensor product of bimodules.

Less generally, a commutative algebra (where associativity and unitality are usually assumed) is a commutative monoid objecy in R Mod R Mod .

For a given ring the algebras form a category, Alg, and the commutative algebras a subcategory, CommAlg.

Over semi-rings

Note that everywhere rings can be replaced by semi-rings in the previous paragraph. For example a commutative associative unital â„š +\mathbb{Q}^{+}-algebra is nothing more than a commutative semi-ring RR with a semi-ring homomorphism â„š +â†’R\mathbb{Q}^{+} \rightarrow R.

  • A dg-algebra is a monoid not in Vect but in the category of (co)chain complexes.

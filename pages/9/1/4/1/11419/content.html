Idea

In algebraic topology, a splitting principle for a classifying space BGB G for some topological group GG (delooping of some ‚àû-group GG) is a map from a torus-classifying space

B(T n)‚ü∂fBG B (T^n) \overset{f}{\longrightarrow} B G

such that the induced pullback of cohomology rings is an injective function:

f *:H ‚Ä¢(BG)‚Ü™H ‚Ä¢(BT n), f^\ast \;\colon\; H^\bullet(B G) \hookrightarrow H^\bullet(B T^n) \,,

hence allowing to view the cohomology of GG-principal ‚àû-bundles in terms of that of plain torus-principal bundles.

In particular for GG a classical compact Lie group such as the unitary group, the splitting principle holds and allows to express Chern classes of complex vector bundles as algebraic expressions in just first Chern classes of complex line bundles.

Complex vectors bundles and their Chern roots

For G=U(n)G = U(n) the unitary group, the universal characteristic classes are the Chern classes c k‚ààH ‚Ä¢(BU(n),‚Ñ§)c_k \in H^\bullet(B U(n), \mathbb{Z}). By the discussion at Chern class ‚Äì Properties ‚Äì Splitting principle and Chern roots. the universal splitting principle here says that

It follows that every complex vector bundle Œæ\xi of rank nn over a space XX when pulled back to its flag space bundle decomposes as a direct sum of complex line bundles Œ∂ i\zeta_i and has Chern classes c kc_k expressed in terms of the first Chern classes of these line bundles as

c k(p *Œæ)=œÉ k(c 1(Œ∂ 1),‚ãØ,c n(Œ∂ n)). c_k(p^\ast \xi ) = \sigma_k(c_1(\zeta_1), \cdots, c_n(\zeta_n)) \,.

This case is typically the default meaning of the ‚Äúsplitting principle‚Äù.

For the special unitary group the situation is the same, only that here the splitting is into a sum of line bundles whose tensor product is constrained to be trivializable.

Idea

Hilbert schemes are moduli spaces of subvarieties, hence configuration spaces in algebraic geometry.

For instance a scheme of 0-dimensional sub-schemes is called a Hilbert scheme of points, etc.

Specifically for quasi-projective variety with fixed Hilbert polynomial?, Hilbert schemes are well behave as moduli spaces go, in that they‚Äôre actually quasi-projective varieties themselves.

The existence and construction of Hilbert schemes is due to Grothendieck (FGA).

The Hilbert scheme of ‚ÑÇ 2\mathbb{C}^2 is widely studied in combinatorics and geometric representation theory for its connections to Macdonald polynomials and Cherednik algebras.

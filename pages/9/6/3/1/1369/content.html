Idea

The notion of monoidal (‚àû,1)(\infty,1)-category is the analogue of the notion of monoidal category in the context of (‚àû,1)-categories. Monoidal (‚àû,1)(\infty,1)-categories form an (‚àû,2)-category, Mon(‚àû,1)Cat.

There are various ways to state the monoidal structure. One is in terms of fibrations over the simplex category. This is the approach taken in

  • Jacob Lurie, Noncommutative algebra (arXiv)

Another is in terms of (‚àû,1)-operads (see there). This approach has been taken in (Francis). Both are described below.

Just as many ordinary (‚àû,1)(\infty,1)-categories (particularly, all of those that are locally presentable) can be presented by model categories, many monoidal (‚àû,1)(\infty,1)-categories can be presented by monoidal model categories. See for instance NikolausSagave15.

Higher monoidal structure

While for an ordinary monoid there is just one notion of commutativity (either it is or it is not commutative), already a monoidal category distinguishes between being just braided monoidal or fully symmetric monoidal.

This pattern continues, as expressed by the periodic table of k-tuply monoidal categories.

A higher category may be a k-tuply monoidal n-category or more generally k-tuply monoidal (n,r)-category for different values of kk. The lowest value of k=1k= 1 (since for k=0k = 0 there is no monoidal structure at all) corresponds to monoidal product which is ‚àû\infty-associative, i.e. associative up to higher coherent homotopies, but need not have any degree of commutativity.

One says that an nn-category is symmetric monoiodal if it is ‚Äúas monoidal as possible‚Äù, i.e. ‚àû\infty-tuply monoidal. In particular, in Noncommutative algebra and Commutative algebra we have

  • the 1-fold monoidal (‚àû,1)-categories described here;

  • ‚àû-tuply monoidal (‚àû,1)-categories.

Operadic/algebraic definition of monoidal structure

For each 1‚â§n‚â§‚àû1 \leq n \leq \infty let E nE_n denote the little n-disk operad whose topological space of E n kE_n^k of kk-ary operations is the space of embeddings of kk nn-dimensional disks (balls) in one nn-dimensional disk without intersection, and whose composition operation is the obvious one obtained from gluing the big outer disks into given inner disks.

In John Francis‚Äô PhD thesis the theory of (‚àû,1)-categories equipped with an action of the E nE_n-operad is established, so that

  • (‚àû,1)(\infty,1)-categories with an E 1E_1-action are precisely monoidal (‚àû,1)-categories ‚Äì 1-fold monoidal (‚àû,1)(\infty,1)-categories;

  • (‚àû,1)(\infty,1)-categories with an E ‚àûE_\infty-action are precisely symmetric monoidal (‚àû,1)-categories ‚Äì ‚àû\infty-tuply monoidal (‚àû,1)(\infty,1)-categories;

  • (‚àû,1)(\infty,1)-categories with an E nE_n-action for 1<n<‚àû1 \lt n \lt \infty are the corresponding nn-tuply monoidal (‚àû,1)(\infty,1)-categories in between.

Jacob Lurie is currently rewriting Higher Algebra such as to build in a discussion of E nE_n-operadic structures in the definition of kk-tuply monoidal (‚àû,1)(\infty,1)-categories.

Idea

A quadratic Gauss sum is a sum of square-powers of primitive roots of unity:

(1)∑ s=0 k−1e 2πiks 2∈ℂ,fork∈ℕ >0. \sum_{s = 0}^{k-1} e^{ \tfrac{2 \pi \mathrm{i}}{k} s^2 } \;\; \in \; \mathbb{C} \,, \;\;\;\;\;\; \text{for} \; k \in \mathbb{N}_{> 0} \,.

A generalized Gauss sum is a variant of this expression, admitting other prefectors or powers of ss in the exponent.

Properties

Proposition

(evaluation of the quadratic Gauss sum)
The basic quadratic Gauss sum (1) evaluates to:

(2)∑ s=0 k−1e 2πiks 2={(1+i)k | m=0mod4 k | m=1mod4 0 | m=2mod4 ik | m=3mod4. \sum_{s = 0}^{k-1} e^{ \tfrac{2 \pi \mathrm{i}}{k} s^2 } \;\; = \;\; \left\{ \begin{array}{ccl} (1 + \mathrm{i}) \sqrt{k} &\vert& m = 0 \;mod\; 4 \\ \sqrt{k} &\vert& m = 1 \;mod\; 4 \\ 0 &\vert& m = 2 \;mod\; 4 \\ \mathrm{i} \sqrt{k} &\vert& m = 3 \;mod\; 4 \mathrlap{\,.} \end{array} \right.

The original proof is due to Gauss 1811, an early alternative proof is due to Dirichlet 1835, reviewed in Dirichlet 1871. Modern review includes Doyle 2016 (1.1), Ram Murty & Pathak 2017 Thm. 1.1, Taylor 2022 Thm. 1.16.

Proposition

(evaluation of quadratic Gauss sum with halfed exponents)
For the variant of (2) with half the usual exponents we have

∑ s=0 k−1e πiks 2={ke πi/4 | k∈2ℕ >0 ? | k∈2ℕ+1 \sum_{s = 0}^{k-1} e^{ \tfrac{\pi \mathrm{i}}{k} s^2 } \;\; = \;\; \left\{ \begin{array}{ccl} \sqrt{k} \, e^{\pi \mathrm{i}/4} &\vert& k \in 2 \mathbb{N}_{\gt 0} \\ ? &\vert& k \in 2 \mathbb{N} + 1 \end{array} \right.

Proofs are given at MO:a/4232289 and MO:a/489863.

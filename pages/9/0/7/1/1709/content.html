Idea

By the discussion at cohomology, plain cohomology is the study of

  • (‚àû,1)-categorical hom-spaces in (‚àû,1)-toposes (for ‚Äúnonabelian cohomology‚Äù)

  • or their stabilizations to stable (‚àû,1)-categories of spectrum objects (for ‚Äúgeneralized (Eilenberg-Steenrod) cohomology‚Äù)

  • or generally in symmetric monoidal (‚àû,1)-categories

and maybe fully generally in any (‚àû,1)-category ùíû\mathcal{C} whatsoever.

So for A‚ààùíûA \in \mathcal{C} any object the cohomology of any other object XX with coefficients in AA is the mapping space ùíû(X,A)\mathcal{C}(X,A). Notice that this is equivalently the homotopy type of sections ùíû /X(X,X√óA)\mathcal{C}_{/X}(X, X \times A) of the trivial AA-fiber ‚àû-bundle over XX. The idea of twisted cohomology then is to consider general AA-fiber ‚àû-bundles œá\chi over XX and take the œá\chi-twisted cohomology of XX to the type of sections of this.

Given an ‚àû\infty-topos H\mathbf{H}, then also its arrow ‚àû\infty-category H I\mathbf{H}^I is an ‚àû\infty-topos, over ‚àûGrpd I\infty Grpd^I and it also sits over H\mathbf{H} by the codomain fibration, constituting an ‚Äúextension‚Äù of H\mathbf{H} by itself:

H ‚Üì incl H I ‚Üì cod H. \array{ \mathbf{H} \\ \downarrow^{\mathrlap{incl}} \\ \mathbf{H}^I \\ \downarrow^{\mathrlap{cod}} \\ \mathbf{H} } \,.

The intrinsic cohomology of H I\mathbf{H}^I under this fibration is nonabelian twisted cohomology as discussed in some detail in Principal ‚àû-bundles ‚Äì theory, presentations and applications.

Notice that ‚Äústable cohomology‚Äù, which is traditionally called generalized (Eilenberg-Steenrod) cohomology may be thought of as the lowest order Goodwillie approximation to nonabelian cohomology: where a cocycle in nonabelian cohomology is a map to any homotopy type, a cocycle in generalized (Eilenberg-Steenrod) cohomology is a map into a stable homotopy type.

In this sense the tangent (infinity,1)-topos THT \mathbf{H} is the lowest order linear approximation to the codomain fibration

Stab(H) ‚Üì incl TH ‚Üì cod H. \array{ \Stab(\mathbf{H}) \\ \downarrow^{\mathrlap{incl}} \\ T\mathbf{H} \\ \downarrow^{\mathrlap{cod}} \\ \mathbf{H} } \,.

Higher-order approximations should involve a notion of higher-order forms of the tangent (‚àû,1)-topos, in parallel with the relationship between the jet bundles and tangent bundle of a manifold. It is clear that whatever we may say in detail about the kkth-jet (‚àû,1)-topos J kHJ^k \mathbf{H}, its intrinsic cohomology is a version of twisted cohomology which is in between nonabelian cohomology and stable i.e. generalized (Eilenberg-Steenrod) cohomology.

It seems that a layered analysis of nonabelian cohomology this way in higher homotopy theory should eventually be rather important, even if it hasn‚Äôt received any attention at all yet. It seems plausible that a generalization of Chern-Weil theory which approximates classes of principal infinity-bundles not just by universal characteristic classes in ordinary cohomology and hence in stable cohomology, but that one wants to consider the whole Goodwillie Taylor tower of approximations to it.

old material, to be harmonized‚Ä¶

Let *‚ÜíB{*} \to B be a pointed object. Then

H [*](X,f)‚âÉH(X,A). \mathbf{H}_{[*]}(X,f) \simeq \mathbf{H}(X,A) \,.
Proof

H(X,A)‚âÉH [*](X,f). \mathbf{H}(X,A) \simeq \mathbf{H}_{[*]}(X,f) \,.

For this reason, when BB is pointed, it is customary to call the set of equivalence classes œÄ 0H [c](X;f)\pi_0\mathbf{H}_{[c]}(X;f) the cc-twisted AA-cohomology of XX, and to denote it by the symbol

H [c](X,A) H_{[c]}(X,A)
Remark

The cohomology fibration sequence H(X,A)‚ÜíH(X,B^)‚ÜíH(X,B)\mathbf{H}(X,A) \to \mathbf{H}(X,\hat B) {\to} \mathbf{H}(X,B) can be seen as an obstruction problem in cohomology:

Sections as twisted functions‚Ä¶

For VV a vector space and XX a manifold, both regarded a 0-truncated objects in the (‚àû,1)(\infty,1)-topos on the site CartSp (that of Lie infinity-groupoids), a cocycle X‚ÜíVX \to V is simply smooth VV-valued function on XX.

twisted K-theory

In the context of generalized (Eilenberg-Steenrod) cohomology a coefficient object for cohomology is a spectrum AA: the AA-cohomology of a topological space XX with coefficients in AA is the set of homotopy classes of maps X‚ÜíAX \to A. For instance, as a model of the degree-00 space in the K-theory spectrum one can take A=Fred(H)A = Fred(H), the space of Fredholm operators on a separable Hilbert space HH. There is a canonical action on this space of the projective unitary group G=PU(H)G = P U(H) of HH. Since PU(H)P U(H) has the homotopy type of an Eilenberg-Mac Lane space K(‚Ñ§,2)K(\mathbb{Z},2), a PU(H)P U(H)-principal bundle P‚ÜíXP \to X defines a class c‚ààH 3(X,‚Ñ§)c \in H^3(X,\mathbb{Z}) in ordinary integral cohomology (this may also be thought of as the class of a twisting bundle gerbe). The twisted K-theory (in degree 00) of XX with that class as its twist is the set of homotopy classes of sections X‚ÜíP√ó PU(H)Fred(H)X \to P \times_{P U(H)} Fred(H) of the associated bundle.

GG-Actions on spectra

The above example generalizes straightforwardly to the case that

  • AA is a connective spectrum, i.e. topological space that is an infinite loop space. (We need to assume a connective spectrum given by an infinite loop space so that AA can be regarded as living in the category of topologicall spaces along with the other objects, such as classifying spaces BG\mathbf{B}G of nonabelian groups);

  • with a (topological) group GG acting on AA by automorphisms and

  • a GG-principal bundle P‚ÜíX.P \to X.

In this case there is an established definition of generalized (Eilenberg-Steenrod) cohomology with coefficients AA twisted by a GG-principal bundle as follows.

From the GG-principal bundle P‚ÜíXP \to X we obtain the associated AA-bundle P√ó GA‚ÜíXP \times_G A \to X. Then a twisted AA-cocycle on XX is defined to be a section X‚ÜíP√ó GAX \to P \times_G A of this associated bundle. The collection of homotopy classes of these sections is the twisted AA-cohomology of XX with the twist specified by the class of PP.

This is the definition of twisted cohomology as it appears for instance essentially as definition 22.1.1 of the May‚ÄìSigursson reference below (when comparing with their definition take their GG to be the trivial group and identify their Œì\Gamma and Œ†\Pi with our GG).

It is clearly a particular case of the general definition of twisted cohomology given above:

  • the (‚àû,1)(\infty,1)-topos H\mathbf{H} is the (‚àû,1)(\infty,1)-category of Top of topological spaces

  • the object BB is the delooping BG\mathbf{B}G of the group GG.

  • the object B^\hat{B} is the homotopy quotient A//G‚âÉEG√ó GAA//G\simeq \mathbf{E}G\times_G A.

  • the twisting cocycle cc is the element in Top(X,BG)\mathbf{Top}(X,\mathbf{B}G) defining the principal GG-bundle P‚ÜíXP\to X.

Indeed, BB is pointed, we have a fibration sequence

A‚ÜíA//G‚ÜíBG A \to A//G \to \mathbf{B}G

and the homotopy pullback

P A ‚Üí A//G ‚Üì ‚Üìf X ‚Üíc BG \array{ P_A &\to& {A//G} \\ \downarrow && \downarrow{f} \\ X &\stackrel{c}{\to}& \mathbf{B}G }\,

is the AA-bundle P√ó GA‚ÜíXP\times_G A\to X.

The obstruction problem described by this example reads as folllows:

  • the obstruction to lifting a (‚Äúnonabelian‚Äù or ‚Äútwisted‚Äù) A//GA//G-cocycle g:X‚ÜíA//Gg : X \to A//G to an AA-cocycle g^:X‚ÜíA\hat g : X \to A is its image Œ¥g\delta g in first GG-cohomology Œ¥g‚ààH 1(X,G):=œÄ 0Top(X,BG)\delta g \in H^1(X,G) :=\pi_0 \mathbf{Top}(X, \mathbf{B} G).

Read the other way round it says:

  • AA-cocycles are precisely those GG-twisted AA-cocycles whose twist vanishes.
Remark

Since the associated bundle P√ó GAP \times_G A is in general no longer itself a spectrum, twisted cohomology is not an example of generalized Eilenberg-Steenrod cohomology.

To stay within the spectrum point of view, May‚ÄìSigurdsson suggested that twisted cohomology should instead be formalized in terms of parameterized homotopy theory, where one thinks of P√ó GAP \times_G A as a parameterized family of spectra.

What is called cohomology with local coefficients is twisted cohomology with the twist given by the class represented by the universal cover space of the base space, which is to say: by the action of the fundamental group of the base space.

In the classical case of ordinary cohomology, C. A. More generally, for any space XX, let AA be a coefficient object that is equipped with an action of the first fundamental group œÄ 1(X)\pi_1(X) of XX. (Such an action is also called an AA-valued local system on XX).

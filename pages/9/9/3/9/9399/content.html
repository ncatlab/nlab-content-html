Definition

Let F,G:C‚ÜíDF,G:C\to D be functors. A natural transformation Œ±:F‚ÜíG\alpha:F\to G is cartesian if for any morphism f:x‚Üíyf:x\to y in CC, the naturality square

There is an evident generalization to natural transformations between higher categories.

Properties

  • Given a functor G:C‚ÜíDG:C\to D, if CC has a terminal object 11, then to give a functor FF and an cartesian natural transformation F‚ÜíGF\to G is equivalent to giving a single object F1F1 and a morphism F1‚ÜíG1F1 \to G1. The rest of FF can then be constructed uniquely by taking pullbacks. This construction is important in the theory of clubs.

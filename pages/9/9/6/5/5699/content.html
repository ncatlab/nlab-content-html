Selected writings

Introducing what came to be called the Brandenberger-Vafa mechanism in string theoretic cosmology:

  • Robert Brandenberger, Cumrun Vafa: Superstrings in the Early Universe, Nucl. Phys. B 316 (1989) 391 [spire263348, doi:10.1016/0550-3213(89)90037-0]

On the quantum cohomology induced by supersymmetric ℂ P N \mathbb{C}P^N sigma-models:

  • Sergio Cecotti, Cumrun Vafa, Exact Results for Supersymmetric Sigma Models, Phys. Rev. Lett. 68 (1992) 903-906 [arXiv:hep-th/9111016, doi:10.1103/PhysRevLett.68.903]

  • Sergio Cecotti, Cumrun Vafa, On Classification of N=2N=2 Supersymmetric Theories, Comm. Math. Phys. 158 (1993) 569-644 [arXiv:hep-th/9211097, doi:10.1007/BF02096804]

On how the second quantization of the B-model yields Kodeira-Spencer gravity/BCOV theory:

  • Michael Bershadsky, Sergio Cecotti, Hirosi Ooguri, Cumrun Vafa, Kodaira-Spencer Theory of Gravity and Exact Results for Quantum String Amplitudes, Commun. Math. Phys. 165 (1994) 311-428 [arXiv:hep-th/9309140, doi:10.1007/BF02099774]

Introducing the idea of F-theory:

  • Cumrun Vafa, Evidence for F-theory, Nucl. Phys. B469:403-418, 1996, (arXiv:hep-th/9602022)

On F-theory KK-compactified on Calabi-Yau manifolds and duality between F-theory and heterotic string theory:

  • David Morrison, Cumrun Vafa, Compactifications of F-Theory on Calabi–Yau Threefolds – I, Nucl. Phys. B473 (1996) 74-92 (arXiv:hep-th/9602114)

Introducing the idea of quantum cohomology:

  • Cumrun Vafa, Topological mirrors and quantum rings, in Shing-Tung Yau (ed.) Essays on mirror manifolds, International Press (1992), republished in Mirror Symmetry I, AMS/IP Studies in Advanced Mathematics 9 (1998) [arXiv:hep-th/9111017, doi:10.1090/amsip/009]

On Chern-Simons theory as a topological string theory (open/closed string duality in interpreting 't Hooft double line notation in the large N limit and in AdS/CFT duality);

  • Rajesh Gopakumar, Cumrun Vafa, On the Gauge Theory/Geometry Correspondence, Adv. Theor. Math. Phys. 3 (1999) 1415-1443 (arXiv:hep-th/9811131)

Including Wilson loop observables in Chern-Simons theory as a topological string theory:

Idea

In the context of algebraic topology, conditional convergence refers to a more general kind of convergence of spectral sequences than actual (‚Äústrong‚Äù) convergence (def.). It is the right concept of convergence for spectral sequences that are not concentrated in the first or third quadrant.

Notably an EE-Adams spectral sequence is in general only conditionally convergent (to the EE-nilpotent completion), unless finiteness conditions are imposed

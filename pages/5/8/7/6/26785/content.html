Idea

A Riemannian immersion or isometric immersion Σ↪X\Sigma \hookrightarrow X of Riemannian manifolds is an immersion of their underlying smooth manifolds which is also an isometry with respect to their Riemannian metrics.

Similarly, an isometric embedding is an isometric immersion which is also an embedding of smooth manifolds, hence of the underlying topological spaces.

The isometric immersion/embedding problem is to find isometric immersions/embeddings of Riemannian manifolds into (large-dimensional) Euclidean spaces (e.g. Han & Hong 2006, Han & Lewicka 2023).

Properties

Adapted Darboux coframes

Given an immersion ϕ:Σ↪X\phi \colon \Sigma \hookrightarrow X into a Riemannian manifold XX — hence in particular with dim(Σ)≤dim(X)dim(\Sigma) \leq dim(X) — then a local orthonormal coframe field (e a) a=1 dim(X)\big(e^a\big)_{a = 1}^{dim(X)} on XX (i.e. such that the metric tensor on XX is locally g=e a⊗e ag = e^a \otimes e_a) is called a Darboux frame (Berger, Bryant & Griffith 1983, later authors also say Darboux co-frame, e.g. Mastrolia, Rigoli & Setti 2012, Def. 1.17, Giron 2020, §3.2.2, Chen & Giron 2021, §2.4) if, locally, the restriction of the frame field to its first dim(Σ)dim(\Sigma)-components and pulled back along ϕ\phi to Σ\Sigma

(1)(ϕ *e a) a=1 dim(Σ) \big( \phi^\ast e^a \big) ^{ dim(\Sigma) } _{ a=1 }

is a frame field for Σ\Sigma.

Proposition

Given an immersion into a Riemannian manifold, local Darboux frames always exist.

Proof

Given an immersion ι:Σ→X\iota \colon \Sigma \to X, consider any point σ∈Σ\sigma \in \Sigma. Since the immersion is locally an embedding (see here), there exists an open neighbourhood σ∈U⊂Σ \sigma \in U \subset \Sigma such that ϕ |U:U→X\phi_{\vert U} \colon U \to X is the embedding of a submanifold. Therefore (by this Prop.) there exists an open neighbourhood U′⊂XU' \subset X of ι(σ)∈X\iota(\sigma) \in X which serves as a coordinate chart X⊃U′→ϕℝ nX \supset U' \xrightarrow{\phi} \mathbb{R}^n for XX and a slice chart for Σ⊂X\Sigma \subset X in that it exhibits Σ∩U′\Sigma \cap U' as a rectilinear hyperplane in ϕ(U′)⊂ℝ n\phi(U') \subset \mathbb{R}^n.

Hence in this slice coordinate chart a local frame for XX is given by the nn canonical coordinate vector fields on with the first kk of them forming a local chart of Σ\Sigma

{∂ 1,⋯,∂ k}↪{∂ 1,⋯,∂ k,∂ k+1,⋯,∂ n}. \big\{ \partial_1, \cdots, \partial_{k} \big\} \hookrightarrow \big\{ \partial_1, \cdots, \partial_{k}, \, \partial_{k+1}, \cdots, \partial_n \big\} \,.

From this local the Gram-Schmidt process produces an orthonormal local frame, first for Σ\Sigma and then extended to XX:

{v 1,⋯,v k}↪{v 1,⋯,v k,v k+1,⋯,v n},g(v a,v b)=δ ab \big\{ v_1, \cdots, v_{k} \big\} \hookrightarrow \big\{ v_1, \cdots, v_{k}, \, v_{k+1}, \cdots, v_n \big\} \,, \;\;\; g(v_a, v_b) = \delta_{a b} \,

(cf. Kayban 2021, Prop. 3.1).

It just remains to turn this frame into the desired coframe:

By construction, the matrix (v a μ) a,μ\big(v_a^\mu\big)_{a,\mu} of components (given by v a μ∂ μ=v av_a^\mu \partial_\mu = v_a) is block upper-triangular (the upper diagonal block being the local frame on Σ\Sigma), with the total matrix and its upper diagonal block (and thus also its lower diagonal block) being invertible matrices.

This means (cf. e.g. here) that also the inverse matrix

(e μ a) μ,a≔((v a μ) μ,a) −1 \big(e^a_\mu\big)_{\mu,a} \,\coloneqq\, \Big(\big(v_a^\mu\big)_{\mu,a}\Big)^{-1}

is block upper-triangular, with its upper left block being the inverse matrix of the original upper left block.

This gives the desired coframe field:

e a≔e μ adx μ e^{a} \,\coloneqq\, e^a_\mu \, \mathrm{d}x^\mu

which is orthonormal on XX

e a⊗e a=g(−,−) e^a \otimes e_a \;=\; g(-,-)

because

v a μg μνv b ν=δ ab⇔g μν=e μ aδ abe ν b, v_a^\mu g_{\mu \nu} v_b^\nu \;=\; \delta_{a b} \;\;\;\; \Leftrightarrow \;\;\;\; g_{\mu \nu} \;=\; e^a_\mu \delta_{a b} e^b_\nu \,,

and whose pullback to the Σ\Sigma-slice is, by the above block-triangular properties, a coframe field on Σ\Sigma.

(For the further generality of sequences of Darboux frames for suitable sequences of immersions, see Giron 2020 and Chen & Giron 2021, Thm. 2.2.)

Hence an immersion ι:Σ↪X\iota \colon \Sigma \hookrightarrow X of Riemannian manifolds is isometric iff around each point ι(σ)\iota(\sigma) any of its Darboux coframe fields pull back to locally induce the given metric on Σ\Sigma.

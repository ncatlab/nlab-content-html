Idea

Let

(1)[X‚ü∂fY] \big[ X \overset{f}{\longrightarrow} Y \big]

be a morphism in the stable homotopy category (for instance the stabilization of a morphism in the classical homotopy category) and let EE be a Whitehead-generalized cohomology theory (usually assumed to be at least multiplicative).

Then the dd-invariant of ff with respect to EE (Adams 66, Sec. 3, p. 26, short for ‚Äúdegree invariant‚Äù, see Example below) is the class of the corresponding pullback morphism f E *f^\ast_E, regarded as an element of the 0th Ext-group (i.e. the plain hom-object) between the EE-cohomologies of XX and YY:

(2)d(f)‚âîf E *‚àà(E ‚Ä¢(Y);E ‚Ä¢(X))=Ext 0(E ‚Ä¢(Y);E ‚Ä¢(X)). d(f) \;\coloneqq\; f^\ast_E \;\in\; \big( E^\bullet(Y); \, E^\bullet(X) \big) \;=\; Ext^0 \big( E^\bullet(Y); \, E^\bullet(X) \big) \,.

The fancy name is justified by the fact that this is beginning of a hierarchy of more interesting invariants of stable maps seen in EE-cohomology, each of which defined when the previous one vanishes: The next is the e-invariant, then comes the f-invariant (e.g. BL 09, Section 2). These are the elements that appear in the first lines on the second page of the EE-Adams spectral sequence for [X,Y] ‚Ä¢[X,Y]_\bullet.

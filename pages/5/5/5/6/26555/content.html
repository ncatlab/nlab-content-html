Idea

According to Kornell 2020, and in mild paraphrase (following the discussion at dependent linear type and quantum circuits via dependent linear types):

  • quantum sets are indexed sets of finite-dimensional Hilbert spaces — hence finite-rank Hilbert-vector bundles over discrete topological spaces regarded as a sets — and regarded as equipped with the external tensor product ⊠\boxtimes of vector bundles;

  • a quantum relation between quantum sets (x:X)×ℋ x(x \colon X) \times \mathscr{H}_x and (x′:X′)×ℋ′ x(x' \colon X') \times \mathscr{H}'_x is a monomorphism from a quantum set ((x,x′):X×X′)×ℛ (x,x′)\big((x,x') \colon X \times X'\big) \times \mathscr{R}_{(x,x')} to ℋ • *⊠ℋ′ •\mathscr{H}^\ast_\bullet \boxtimes \mathscr{H}'_\bullet:

    (x,x′):X×X′⊢ℛ (x,y)↪ℋ (x,y) *⊗ℋ (x,y) ′ (x,x') \colon X \times X' \;\;\; \vdash \;\;\; \mathscr{R}_{(x,y)} \hookrightarrow \mathscr{H}^\ast_{(x,y)} \otimes \mathscr{H}^'_{(x,y)}

With composition the evident matrix multiplication (Kornell 2020 (5)), quantum relations between quantum sets form a category qRelqRel, which is a dagger-compact category.

As such, this serves as categorical semantics for quantum programming languages like Quipper equipped with term recursion, via quantum CPOs (Kornell, Lindenhovius & Mislove 2021).

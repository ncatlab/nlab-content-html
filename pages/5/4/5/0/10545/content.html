Idea

For pp a prime number, the Steenrod algebra ùíú\mathcal{A} is the associative algebra over the prime field ùîΩ p\mathbb{F}_p of cohomology operations on ordinary cohomology with coefficients in ùîΩ p\mathbb{F}_p. For p=2p = 2 this is the ùîΩ 2\mathbb{F}_2-algebra generated by the Steenrod square operations. (Often the case p=2p = 2 is understood by default.)

For any prime pp, the mod-pp Steenrod algebra furthermore has the structure of a Hopf algebra over ùîΩ p\mathbb{F}_p, non-commutative but co-commutative. Under forming linear duals this gives the dual Steenrod algebra, traditionally denoted ùíú *\mathcal{A}_\ast instead of ùíú *\mathcal{A}^\ast. This is a Hopf algebra over ùîΩ p\mathbb{F}_p that is commutative, but non-co-commutative.

The dual ùîΩ p\mathbb{F}_p Steenrod algebra is a special case of a commutative Hopf algebroid structure canonically induced on the self generalized homology E ‚Ä¢(E)E_\bullet(E) of any ring spectrum EE for which E ‚Ä¢‚ÜíE ‚Ä¢(E)E_\bullet \to E_\bullet(E) is a flat morphisms. Therefore in this general case one sometimes speaks of ‚Äúdual EE-Steenrod algebras‚Äù.

The Ext-groups between comodules for these commutative Hopf algebroids E ‚Ä¢(E)E_\bullet(E) prominently appear on the second page of the EE-Adams spectral sequence, see there for more.

Hopf algebroid structure

For ordinary cohomology

The Steenrod algebra for mod pp coefficients is a Hopf algebra over ùîΩ p\mathbb{F}_p which is graded commutative and non-co-commutative.

This is due to (Milnor 58). A review is in Ravenel, ch. 3, section 1.

Relation to Adams spectral sequence

For ordinary cohomology

By construction, the total cohomology H ‚Ä¢(X,‚Ñ§ 2)H^\bullet(X,\mathbb{Z}_2) of a topological space XX, naturally is a module over the Steenrod algebra:

a cohomology element is represented by a cocycle which is a map X‚ü∂B n‚Ñ§ 2X \longrightarrow B^n \mathbb{Z}_2 and the action of a Steenrod square on this is just by composition.

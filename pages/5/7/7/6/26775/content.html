Idea

For a pair of coprime natural numbers n +,n −∈ℕ ≥1n_+, n_- \in \mathbb{N}_{\geq 1}, qcd(n +,n −)=1qcd(n_+, n_-) = 1, the (n +,n −)(n_+, n_-)-spindle is the 2-dimensional orbifold whose underlying coarse topological space is the 2-sphere/Riemann sphere ℂP 1\mathbb{C}P^1, but where the poles 0,∞∈ℂP 10, \infty \in \mathbb{C}P^1 are cone tips of order n +,n −n_+, n_-, respectively.

This is also sometimes called the (n +,n −)(n_+,n_-)-football and is denoted “S 2(n +,n −)S^2(n_+, n_-)” or “Σ(n +,n −)\Sigma(n_+, n_-)” or sometimes just “(n +,n −)(n_+, n_-)”. It is the orbifold incarnation of the weighted projective space ℂP(n +,n −)\mathbb{C}P(n_+, n_-).

If either n ±=1n_\pm = 1 but n ∓>1n_{\mp} \gt 1 then the spindle orbifold reduces to the orbifold known as the teardrop.

If both n +=n −=1n_+ = n_- = 1 then the spindle orbifold reduces is a smooth manifold: the ordinary (Riemann) 2-sphere S 2S^2.

As a proper étale Lie groupoid

We describe the spindle orbifold as a proper étale Lie groupoid (the following description is far from unique, as far as Morita equivalent proper étale Lie groupoids go, but it is about the simplest evident realization):

  1. the smooth manifold of objects is the disjoint union of two copies of the plane ℝ 2\mathbb{R}^2 — which for notational purposes, at least, will be useful to think of as the complex line ℂ\mathbb{C}:

    (1)Obj(ℂP(n +,n −))≔ℂ +⊔ℂ −, Obj\big(\mathbb{C}P(n_+, n_-)\big) \;\coloneqq\; \mathbb{C}_+ \;\sqcup\; \mathbb{C}_- \mathrlap{\,,}

  2. the smooth manifold of morphisms is the disjoint union of

    1. (these) two copies of ℂ\mathbb{C} times the underlying set of the cyclic group C n ±C_{n_{\pm}} of order n ±n_\pm, respectively,

    2. two copies of its punctured version ℂ ×≔ℂ∖{0}\mathbb{C}^\times \coloneqq \mathbb{C} \setminus \{0\} (its complement of the origin), times the underlying set of the product cyclic group C n +×C n −C_{n_{+}} \times C_{n_-}:

(2)Mor(ℂP(n +,n −))≔((ℂ +×C n +)⊔(ℂ −×C n −))⊔((ℂ +− ××C n +×C n −)⊔(ℂ −+ ××C n −×C n +)), Mor\big(\mathbb{C}P(n_+, n_-)\big) \;\coloneqq\; \Big( (\mathbb{C}_+ \times C_{n_+}) \;\sqcup\; (\mathbb{C}_- \times C_{n_-}) \Big) \;\sqcup\; \Big( (\mathbb{C}_{+-}^\times \times C_{n_+} \times C_{n_-}) \;\sqcup\; (\mathbb{C}_{-+}^\times \times C_{n_-} \times C_{n_+}) \Big) \mathrlap{\,,}

where the subscripts on the copies of ℂ \mathbb{C} only serve to index the direct summands.

Here the first pair of direct summands may be referred to as the “internal” morphisms (as they will operate within either chart, implementing here the actual orbi-singularities), and the second pair of summands as the “external” or “gluing” morphisms (as they operate between charts, implementing nothing but their gluing).

Concretely, with the abbreviation

q ±≔e 2πi/n ±, q_{\pm} \coloneqq e^{2 \pi \mathrm{i}/n_{\pm}} \,,

the source and target maps for the internal morphisms and for the weightless gluing morphisms are:

Mor(ℂP(n +,n −)) ⟶(s,t) Obj(ℂP(n +,n −)) 2 ((z,±),[k ±]) ↦ ((z,±),(zq ± k ±,±)) ((v,±∓),[0],[0]) ↦ ((v n ±,±),(v −n ∓,∓)) \begin{array}{ccc} Mor\big(\mathbb{C}P(n_+, n_-)\big) &\overset{(s,t)}{\longrightarrow}& Obj\big(\mathbb{C}P(n_+, n_-)\big)^2 \\ \big( (z,\pm), [k_\pm] \big) &\mapsto& \big( (z, \pm) , (z q_{\pm}^{k_{\pm}}, \pm) \big) \\ \\ \big( (v,\pm \mp), [0], [0] \big) &\mapsto& \Big( \big( v^{n_\pm}, \pm \big) , \big( v^{-n_\mp}, \mp \big) \Big) \mathrlap{\,} \end{array}

and the composition map for the “internal” morphisms is that of the action groupoid ℂ ±⫽C ±\mathbb{C}_{\pm} \sslash C_{\pm}:

Mor(ℂP(n +,n −)) s× tMor(ℂP(n +,n −)) ⟶(−)∘(−) Mor(ℂP(n +,n −)) (((zq ± k ±,±),[k′ ±]),((z,±),[k ±])) ↦ ((z,±),[k′ ±+k ±]). \begin{array}{ccc} Mor\big(\mathbb{C}P(n_+, n_-)\big) {}_s \times_t Mor\big(\mathbb{C}P(n_+, n_-)\big) &\overset{(-)\circ (-)}{\longrightarrow}& Mor\big(\mathbb{C}P(n_+, n_-)\big) \\ \Big( \big( (z q_{\pm}^{k_{\pm}} ,\pm),[k'_\pm] \big) , \big( (z,\pm),[k_\pm] \big) \Big) &\mapsto& \big( (z,\pm), [k'_\pm + k_\pm] \big) \,. \end{array}

Finally, the general gluing morphism is the unique composite

((v,±∓),[k ±],[k ∓])≡((v −n ∓,∓),[k ∓]))∘((v,±,∓),[0],[0])∘((v n ±⋅q −k ±,±),[k ±]), \big( (v,\pm\mp), [k_\pm], [k_\mp] \big) \;\equiv\; \big( (v^{-n_\mp},\mp), [k_\mp]) \big) \circ \big( (v,\pm,\mp), [0], [0] \big) \circ \big( (v^{n_\pm} \cdot q^{-k_{\pm}}, \pm), [k_\pm] \big) \,,

which defines either side by the other (meaning that the gluing morphisms are a groupoid torsor from either side over these action groupoids). Its source and target maps are

(s,t):((v,±∓),[k ±],[k ∓])↦((v −n ∓⋅q +k ∓,±),(v n ±⋅q −k ±,∓)). (s,t) \;\colon\; \big( (v,\pm\mp), [k_\pm], [k_\mp] \big) \mapsto \big( (v^{-n_{\mp}} \cdot q^{+ k_{\mp}}, \pm) , (v^{n_{\pm}} \cdot q^{- k_{\pm}}, \mp) \big) \mathrlap{\,.}

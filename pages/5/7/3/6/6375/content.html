Idea

Emil Artin‘s reciprocity law is a reciprocity law in class field theory for global fields. It states, in consequence, that for each 1-dimensional Galois representation σ\sigma there exists a Dirichlet character χ\chi such that the Artin L-function L σL_\sigma equals the Dirichlet L-function L χL_\chi. (The generalization of this reciprocal correspondence to higher dimensional Galois representations is the content of the conjectured Langlands correspondence).

For KK a global field there is a canonical map

𝕀 K⟶Gal(K ab/K) \mathbb{I}_K \longrightarrow Gal(K^{ab}/K)

from its group of ideles to the abelianization of its Galois group, given by

(⋯,a v,⋯)↦∏ vFrob v ord v(a v). (\cdots, a_v, \cdots) \mapsto \prod_v Frob_v^{ord_v(a_v)} \,.

For KK a number field, Artin’s reciprocity law says that this map is surjective, that it factors through the idele class group K ×\𝕀 KK^\times \backslash \mathbb{I}_K and moreover that further quotienting this by the connected component 𝒪\mathcal{O} of 1 in the idele class group yields an isomorphism

K ×\𝕀 K/𝒪⟶≃Gal(K ab/K). K^\times \backslash \mathbb{I}_K / \mathcal{O} \stackrel{\simeq}{\longrightarrow} Gal(K^{ab}/K) \,.

For K=ℚK = \mathbb{Q} this is also the statement of the Kronecker-Weber theorem, and together this is a starting point of the Langlands correspondence conjecture, see there for more.

For KK a function field the map is no longer surjective, but yields on the quotient by the restricted product ∏ v𝒪 v ×\prod_v \mathcal{O}_v^\times an injection with dense image

K ×\𝕀 K/∏ v𝒪 v ×↪Gal(K ab/K). K^\times \backslash \mathbb{I}_K / \prod_v \mathcal{O}_v^\times \hookrightarrow Gal(K^{ab}/K) \,.

(e.g. Toth 11, p. 3)

Notice that the double quotients appearing here are by the Weil uniformization theorem analogous to moduli stacks of bundles on the arithmetic curve on which KK is the field of rational functions. Under this function field analogy the analog of Artin’s reciprocity law plays a central role in the geometric Langlands correspondence.

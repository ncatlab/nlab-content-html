Idea

In representation theory, the term intertwiner is a synonym for homomorphism of representations/actions/modules.

Notably for linear representations ρ 1\rho_1, ρ 2\rho_2 of some group or associative algebra AA on vector spaces (or more general modules) V 1,V 2V_1, V_2, a linear map u:V 1→V 2u \colon V_1 \to V_2 is said to intertwine ρ 1\rho_1 with ρ 2\rho_2 if for all a∈Aa \in A we have that

u∘ρ 1(a)=ρ 2(a)∘u, u \circ \rho_1(a) \;=\; \rho_2(a)\circ u \,,

Definition

A wallpaper group is a crystallographic group (space group) in dimension 2, hence a subgroup G⊂Iso(ℝ 2)G \subset Iso(\mathbb{R}^2) of the isometry group of the Euclidean plane such that

  1. the part of GG inside the translation group is generated by two linearly independent vectors;

  2. the point group is finite.

Equivariant cell structure on 2-tori

The following shows, for the symmorphic 2D crystallographic groups (wallpaper groups) G⋉ℤ 2⊂Iso(2) G \ltimes \mathbb{Z}^2 \subset Iso(2) , the G G -CW complex structure on the resulting tori T 2≔ℝ 2/ℤ 2T^2 \coloneqq \mathbb{R}^2/\mathbb{Z}^2 (graphics from SS25).

(The remaining non-symmorphic cases follow.)

The point groups GG arising are the cyclic groups ℤ /1=\mathbb{Z}_{/1} = 1 1 , ℤ /2 \mathbb{Z}_{/2} , ℤ /3 \mathbb{Z}_{/3} , ℤ /4 \mathbb{Z}_{/4} , ℤ /6\mathbb{Z}_{/6} and the dihedral groups Dih 1Dih_1, Dih 2Dih_2, Dih 3Dih_3, Dih 4Dih_4, and Dih 6Dih_6 (making 10 distinct point groups GG, but the first three of the latter come with two inequivalent actions each).

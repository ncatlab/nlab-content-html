Definition

Definition

Segal’s category, denoted by Γ\Gamma, is the category opposite to the skeleton of the category FinSet */FinSet^{*/} of pointed finite sets:

Γ op≃FinSet */.\Gamma^{op} \simeq FinSet^{*/}.

Equivalently, Γ\Gamma is the category opposite to the category FinSetPartFinSetPart of finite sets and partially defined maps. Objects are finite sets. Morphisms A→BA\to B are pairs (A′,f)(A',f), where A′⊂AA'\subset A and f:A′→Bf\colon A'\to B is a map of sets. Identity morphisms are specified by id A=(A,id A)id_A=(A,id_A). Composition is defined as follows:

(A′,f):A→B,(B′,g):B→C,(B′,g)∘(A′,f)=(A′∩f −1B′,g∘f| A′∩f −1B′).(A',f)\colon A\to B,\qquad (B',g)\colon B\to C,\qquad(B',g)\circ(A',f)=(A'\cap f^{-1}B',g\circ f|_{A'\cap f^{-1}B'}).

Equivalently (Segal, Definition 1.1), Γ\Gamma admits the following covariant description. Objects are finite sets. Morphisms A→BA\to B are maps f:A→P(B)f\colon A\to P(B) such that a 1≠a 2a_1\ne a_2 implies f(a 1)∩f(a 2)=∅f(a_1)\cap f(a_2)=\emptyset. Identity morphisms are specified by id A(a)={a}id_A(a)=\{a\}. Composition is defined as follows:

f:A→P(B),g:B→P(C),(g∘f)(a)=⋃ b∈f(a)g(b).f\colon A\to P(B),\qquad g\colon B\to P(C),\qquad (g\circ f)(a)=\bigcup_{b\in f(a)}g(b).
Remark

The category Γ\Gamma is related to (infinity,1)-operads in a way similar to how the simplex category (non-empty and linearly ordered finite sets) is related to (∞,1)-categories.

Comparison to the simplex category

Recall the simplex category Δ\Delta, whose objects are finite inhabited totally ordered sets and morphisms are order-preserving maps of sets. We set

[m]={0<⋯<m}.[m]=\{0\lt\cdots\lt m\}.

Interpreting a simplex A∈ΔA\in\Delta as a (posetal) category, a spine edge in AA is a morphism in AA that is not an identity morphism and cannot be decomposed as the composition of two nonidentity morphisms. That is to say, in a standard simplex [m][m], a spine edge is a pair of the form (i−1,i)(i-1,i), 0<i≤m0\lt i\le m, which encodes the morphism (i−1)→i(i-1)\to i.

The canonical functor

ι:Δ→Γ\iota\colon\Delta\to\Gamma

sends a simplex to its set of spine edges:

[m]={0<1<2<⋯<m}↦{(0,1),(1,2),…,(m−1,m)}=ι[m].[m]=\{0\lt1\lt2\lt\cdots\lt m\}\mapsto \{(0,1),(1,2),\ldots,(m-1,m)\}=\iota[m].

To define ι\iota on morphisms, observe that a morphism of simplices f:[m]→[n]f\colon[m]\to[n] can be interpreted as a functor between posetal categories. This functor sends a spine edge in [m][m] to the composition of a set of spine edges in [n][n]. More specifically, ff sends a spine edge i−1→ii-1\to i to the composition of spine edges

f(i−1)→f(i−1)+1→⋯→f(i)−1→f(i).f(i-1)\to f(i-1)+1\to \cdots \to f(i)-1\to f(i).

Using Segal’s covariant description of Γ\Gamma, we can define a morphism ι(f):ι[m]→ι[n]\iota(f)\colon\iota[m]\to\iota[n] by sending a spine edge ee in [m][m] to the set of spine edges in [n][n] whose composition yields the image of ee under the functor f:[m]→[n]f\colon[m]\to [n].

In terms of the category FinSetPart, we can describe ι(f)\iota(f) as a partial map of finite sets ι[n]→ι[m]\iota[n]\to\iota[m] that sends (j−1,j)∈ι[n](j-1,j)\in\iota[n] to the unique pair (i−1,i)∈ι[m](i-1,i)\in\iota[m] such that f(i−1)<j≤f(i)f(i-1)\lt j\le f(i). (If no such pair exists, then ι(f)\iota(f) is undefined on (j−1,j)(j-1,j).)

Monoidal structure

The category Γ\Gamma is a symmetric monoidal category, induced from the cartesian monoidal structure on the category Set:

A 1⊗A 2=A 1×A 2,(A′ 1,f 1)⊗(A′ 2,f 2)=(A′ 1×A′ 2,f 1×f 2).A_1\otimes A_2=A_1\times A_2,\qquad (A'_1,f_1)\otimes(A'_2,f_2)=(A'_1\times A'_2,f_1\times f_2).

The reader should keep in mind that ×\times is not a product in the category FinSetPart, but only in Set.

In terms of the category FinSet *FinSet_*, the monoidal structure is given by the smash product of pointed sets:

A 1⊗A 2=(A 1×A 2)/({*}×A 2∪A 1×{*}).A_1\otimes A_2=(A_1\times A_2)/(\{*\}\times A_2\cup A_1\times \{*\}).

Applications

A Γ-object in a cartesian monoidal category (more generally, cartesian monoidal (∞,1)-category) CC is a functor

X:Γ op→CX\colon \Gamma^{op} \to C

(equivalently, a functor FinSetPart→CFinSetPart \to C) such that for all A,B∈FinSetPartA,B\in FinSetPart the map

X A⊔B→X A×X BX_{A\sqcup B}\to X_A\times X_B

induced by the morphisms

(A,id A):A⊔B→A,(B,id B):A⊔B→B(A,id_A)\colon A\sqcup B\to A, \qquad (B,id_B)\colon A\sqcup B\to B

is an equivalence, as is the map X ∅→1X_\emptyset\to1. Here A⊔BA\sqcup B denotes the coproduct in the category Set (i.e., disjoint union). It does not denote the coproduct in the category FinSetPart.

Γ-objects in CC encode E ∞\mathrm{E}_\infty-monoids in CC, i.e., homotopy coherent commutative monoids. See the article Γ-space for more information.

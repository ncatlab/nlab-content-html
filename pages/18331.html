Idea

Given a separable Hilbert space ℋ\mathcal{H}, such as the sequence space 𝓁 2\mathcal{l}_2, write B(ℋ)B(\mathcal{H}) for its C*-algebra of bounded linear operators and D(ℋ)D(\mathcal{H}) for the subalgebra of diagonal operators.

The Kadison-Singer problem is the question:

Does every pure state ψ\psi on D(ℋ)D(\mathcal{H}) extend to a state ρ\rho on B(ℋ)B(\mathcal{H})?

D(ℋ) ⟶ψ ℂ ↓ ↗ ∃ρ B(ℋ) \array{ D(\mathcal{H}) &\overset{\psi}{\longrightarrow}& \mathbb{C} \\ \downarrow & \nearrow_{\mathrlap{\exists \rho}} \\ B(\mathcal{H}) }

This was proven to be the case in Marcus-Spielman-Srivastava 13.

References

The problem was stated in

  • Richard V. Kadison, Isadore Singer, Extensions of pure states, American Journal of Mathematics, 81(2):383–400, 1959 (jstor:2372748)

and its solution was proven in

  • Adam Marcus, Daniel A. Spielman, and Nikhil Srivastava, Interlacing Families II: Mixed Characteristic Polynomials and The Kadison-Singer Problem, 2013. (arXiv:1306.3969)

Review includes

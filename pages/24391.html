Idea

Super convex spaces generalize the idea of a convex space by replacing finite affine sums with countable affine sums.

Definition

Let 𝒢(ℕ)\mathcal{G}(\mathbb{N}) denote the set of all probability measures on the set of natural numbers, hence every p\mathbf{p} can be represented as p=∑ i∈ℕp iδ i\mathbf{p} = \sum_{i \in \mathbb{N}} p_i \delta_i where ∑ i∈ℕp i=1\sum_{i \in \mathbb{N}} p_i=1 with each p i∈[0,1]p_i \in [0,1].Here 𝒢\mathcal{G} is the Giry monad, and because we want to forget the σ\sigma-algebra associated with that measurable space we let Δ ℕ\Delta_{\mathbb{N}} denote the underlying set of 𝒢(ℕ)\mathcal{G}(\mathbb{N}).

Given any set AA, a sequence a:ℕ→A\mathbf{a}: \mathbb{N} \rightarrow A, and any p∈𝒢(ℕ)\mathbf{p} \in \mathcal{G}(\mathbb{N}), which is equivalent to saying that p∈Δ ℕ\mathbf{p} \in \Delta_{\mathbb{N}}, we refer to the formal expression ∑ i∈ℕp ia i\sum_{i \in \mathbb{N}} p_i a_i as a countably affine sum of elements of AA, and for brevity we use the notation ∑ i∈ℕp ia i\sum_{i\in \mathbb{N}} p_i a_i to refer to a countably affine sum dropping the explicit reference to the condition that the limit of partial sums ∑ i=0 Np i\sum_{i=0}^N p_i converges to one. An alternative notation to the countable affine sum notation is to use the integral notation

∫ ℕadp:=∑ i∈ℕp ia i. \int_{\mathbb{N}} \mathbf{a} \, d\mathbf{p} := \sum_{i \in \mathbb{N}} p_i a_i.

We say a set AA has the structure of a super convex space AA if and only if the following three axioms are satisfied:

Axiom 1. For every sequence a:ℕ→A\mathbf{a}: \mathbb{N} \rightarrow A and every p∈𝒢(ℕ)\mathbf{p} \in \mathcal{G}(\mathbb{N}) it follows that

∫ ℕadp∈A. \int_{\mathbb{N}} \mathbf{a} \, d\mathbf{p} \in A.

Axiom 2. For every sequence a:ℕ→A\mathbf{a}: \mathbb{N} \rightarrow A and every j∈ℕj \in \mathbb{N} the property

∫ ℕadδ j=a j \int_{\mathbb{N}} \mathbf{a} \, \, d\delta_j = a_j

holds.

Axiom 3. If p∈𝒢(ℕ)\mathbf{p} \in \mathcal{G}(\mathbb{N}) and Q:ℕ→𝒢(ℕ)\mathbf{Q}: \mathbb{N} \rightarrow \mathcal{G}(\mathbb{N}) is a sequence of probability measures on (ℕ,powersetℕ)(\mathbb{N}, \powerset{\mathbb{N}}) then

∫ j∈ℕ(∫ ℕadQ j)dp=∫ ℕad(∫ j∈ℕQ •dp). \int_{j \in \mathbb{N}} \big( \int_{\mathbb{N}} \mathbf{a} \, d\mathbf{Q}^j \big) \, d\mathbf{p} = \int_{\mathbb{N}} \mathbf{a} \, d( \int_{j \in \mathbb{N}} \mathbf{Q}^{\bullet} \, d\mathbf{p} \big).

Alternative Definition

Viewing Δ ℕ\Delta_{\mathbb{N}} as a monoid we can model a super convex space AA as the subcategory of Set Δ ℕ op\mathbf{Set}^{\Delta_{\mathbb{N}}^{op}} consisting of those functors given by A^∈Set Δ ℕ op\widehat{A} \in \Set^{\Delta_{\mathbb{N}}^{op}} where A^(⋆)=Set(ℕ,A)\widehat{A}(\star)=\Set(\mathbb{N}, A) and for all p∈𝒢ℕ\mathbf{p} \in \mathcal{G}{\mathbb{N}} (hence p∈Δ ℕ\mathbf{p} \in \Delta_{\mathbb{N}} and vice-versa)

A^(p)(a)=∫ ℕadp, \widehat{A}(\mathbf{p})(\mathbf{a}) = \int_{\mathbb{N}} \mathbf{a} \, d\mathbf{p},

which we identify with the constant sequence in AA with the value ∫ ℕadp \int_{\mathbb{N}} \mathbf{a} \, d\mathbf{p}.

A natural transformation m:A^⇒B^m: \widehat{A} \Rightarrow \widehat{B} given at the single component is the function m ⋆=Set(ℕ,m)m_{\star} = \mathbf{Set}(\mathbb{N}, m) which is required to make the Set\mathbf{Set}-diagram

Set(ℕ,A) →m ⋆ Set(ℕ,B) p ↓ ↓ p Set(ℕ,A) →m ⋆ Set(ℕ,B) \array{& \mathbf{Set}(\mathbb{N},A) & \overset{m_{\star}}\rightarrow & \mathbf{Set}(\mathbb{N},B) & \\ \mathbf{p} & \downarrow &&\downarrow & \mathbf{p}\\ &\mathbf{Set}(\mathbb{N},A) & \underset{m_{\star}}\rightarrow& \mathbf{Set}(\mathbb{N},B) & \\ }

commute, which requires m(∫ ℕadp=∫ ℕ(m∘a)pm(\int_{\mathbb{N}} \mathbf{a} d\mathbf{p}=\int_{\mathbb{N}}(m \circ \mathbf{a})\mathbf{p}.

Properties

The category SCvx\mathbf{SCvx} has all limits and colimits. Furthermore it is a symmetric monoidal closed category under the tensor product. The proof of the latter condition follows the proof by Meng, replacing finite sums with countable sums.

Examples

A fundamental super convex space is the set ℕ\mathbb{N} with the super convex space structure defined by

∫ ℕsdp=inf i{s i|p i>0} \int_{\mathbb{N}} \mathbf{s} d\mathbf{p} = \inf_i \{\mathbf{s}_i \, | \, p_i>0 \}

Idea

A Galois representation is a linear representation of a Galois group (often the absolute Galois group of some field). In other words, given a field extension EE of FF, and a vector space VV over some field kk, a Galois representation œÅ\rho is a group homomorphism

œÅ:Gal(E/F)‚ÜíGL(V)\rho:\Gal(E/F)\to \GL(V)

where GL(V)\GL(V) is the group of linear transformations of VV. If VV is an nn-dimensional vector space, GL(V)\GL(V) is the same as the general linear group GL n(k)\GL_{n}(k). A Galois module is a generalization of a Galois representation to modules instead of vector spaces.

Continuous Galois representations

When the Galois group is the absolute Galois group of a number field or a finite extension of the p-adic numbers, and the field of scalars of the underlying vector space of the representation is a topological field, one often considers continuous Galois representations (noting that the absolute Galois group is a topological group).

Conjectures on Galois Representations

Fontaine-Mazur Conjecture

The Fontaine-Mazur conjecture gives a criterion for when an ‚Ñì\ell-adic Galois representation ‚Äúcomes from geometry‚Äù, i.e. is obtained from the ‚Ñì\ell-adic etale cohomology of a variety. Namely, a Galois representation comes from geometry if and only if it is unramified at almost all places, and de Rham (see p-adic Hodge theory) at the places over ‚Ñì\ell (LiLFunctions).

Langlands Correspondence

A restricted version of the global Langlands correspondence (BuzzardMSRI) for GL n\GL_{n} states that algebraic automorphic representations of GL n\GL_{n} over ‚Ñö\mathbb{Q} are in bijection with compatible systems of ‚Ñì\ell-adic Galois representations. A more general form is conjectured for number fields instead of ‚Ñö\mathbb{Q}.

The local Langlands correspondence for GL n\GL_{n} states that irreducible admissible representations of GL n(‚Ñö p)\GL_{n}(\mathbb{Q}_{p}) are in correspondence with F-semisimple Weil-Deligne representations of Gal(‚Ñö p¬Ø/‚Ñö p)\Gal(\overline{\mathbb{Q}_{p}}/\mathbb{Q}_{p}). This has been proved. A more general form is also true for more general local fields instead of ‚Ñö p\mathbb{Q}_{p}.

For reductive groups other than GL n\GL_{n}, Galois representations need to be replaced by the more general concept of L-parameters (and the statement becomes a classification, in terms of a partition into L-packets, rather than a bijection).

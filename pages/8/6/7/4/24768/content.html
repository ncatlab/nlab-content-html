Idea

The counterpart to function extensionality but for products/pairs of elements (a,b)(a, b). Product extensionality characterizes the equality of elements in cartesian products in set theory and product types in type theory.

Definition

In set theory

Product extensionality states that for all elements a∈Aa \in A, a′∈Aa' \in A, b∈Bb \in B, and b′∈Bb' \in B, if a=a′a = a' and b=b′b = b', then the ordered pairs (a,b)=(a′,b′)(a, b) = (a', b').

In type theory

In intensional type theory, equality is represented by the identity type, and furthermore, there might be more than one element of the identity type in intensional type theory, so a naive translation of product extensionality into intensional type theory doesn’t result in the right statement.

Instead, we have the following:

For every type AA and BB and element a:A×Ba:A \times B and b:A×Bb:A \times B, there is a canonical function

idtocomponentid(a,b):a= A×Bb→((π 1(a)= Aπ 1(b))×(π 2(a)= Bπ 2(b)))\mathrm{idtocomponentid}(a, b):a =_{A \times B} b \to \left((\pi_1(a) =_A \pi_1(b)) \times (\pi_2(a) =_B \pi_2(b))\right)

inductively defined by

idtocomponentid(a,a)(refl A×B(a))≡(refl A(π 1(a)),refl B(π 2(a)):Ω(A,π 1(a))×Ω(B,π 2(a))\mathrm{idtocomponentid}(a, a)(\mathrm{refl}_{A \times B}(a)) \equiv (\mathrm{refl}_{A}(\pi_1(a)), \mathrm{refl}_{B}(\pi_2(a)):\Omega(A, \pi_1(a)) \times \Omega(B, \pi_2(a))

where Ω(A,a)\Omega(A, a) is the loop space type a= Aaa =_A a of AA at a:Aa:A.

Product extensionality is the statement that the function idtocomponentid(a,b)\mathrm{idtocomponentid}(a, b) is an equivalence of types for all elements a:A×Ba:A \times B and b:A×Bb:A \times B:

prodext(a,b):isEquiv(idtocomponentid(a,b))\mathrm{prodext}(a, b):\mathrm{isEquiv}(\mathrm{idtocomponentid}(a, b))

Definitional product extensionality

One could replace the equivalence of types above with a definitional equality of types, resulting in definitional product extensionality, that for all types AA and BB and elements a:A×Ba:A \times B and b:A×Bb:A \times B,

a= A×Bb≡(π 1(a)= Aπ 1(b))×(π 2(a)= Bπ 2(b))a =_{A \times B} b \equiv (\pi_1(a) =_A \pi_1(b)) \times (\pi_2(a) =_B \pi_2(b))

or equivalently, for all types AA and BB and elements a:Aa:A, a′:Aa':A, b:Bb:B, and b′:Bb':B,

(a,b)= A×B(a′,b′)≡(a= Aa′)×(b= Bb′)(a, b) =_{A \times B} (a', b') \equiv (a =_A a') \times (b =_B b')

Definitional product extensionality holds in cubical type theory and higher observational type theory.

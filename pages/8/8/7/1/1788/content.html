Idea

In homological algebra one usually studies functors of the form 𝒜→Ab,k−mod,…\mathcal{A}\to Ab, k-mod, \dots defined via derived functors where 𝒜\mathcal{A} is some category of groups, algebras over a ring kk etc., and Ab Ab , k − Mod k-Mod are categories of abelian groups and k k -modules, where kk is some ground ring.

For instance, for the category of groups 𝒜=\mathcal{A}= Grp, using the left derived functors L q(ℤ⊗ ℤG(−))L_q(\mathbb{Z}\otimes_{\mathbb{Z}G}(-)) of the functor of coinvariants ℤ⊗ ℤG(−):ℤG−mod→Ab\mathbb{Z}\otimes_{\mathbb{Z}G}(-): \mathbb{Z}G-mod\to Ab one usually defines group homology H q(G;A)=L q(ℤ⊗ ℤG(−))(A)H_q(G;A) =L_q(\mathbb{Z}\otimes_{\mathbb{Z}G}(-))(A) of a group GG with coefficients in a ℤG\mathbb{Z}G-modules AA.

The idea of a higher limit approach is to use categories Pres(A)Pres(A) of extensions 0→I→P→A→00\to I\to P\to A\to 0 where PP is a projective object in a category of algebraic objects 𝒜\mathcal{A} (e.g free presentations of groups) and describe such functors of homological nature H:𝒜→Ab,k−mod,…H:\mathcal{A}\to Ab, k-mod,\dots using derived/higher (co)limits of some simple functors ℱ\mathcal{F} from the category H≃lim *,colim *(ℱ:Pres(A)→Ab,k−mod,…)H\simeq lim^{*}, colim_{*}(\mathcal{F} \colon Pres(A)\to Ab, k-mod, \dots).

This approach originates in the work [Quillen 1989], where, for instance, he derived the formula:

HC 2n(A)=lim(F/(I n+1+[F,F])), HC_{2n}(A) \;=\; lim\big( F/(I^{n+1}+[F,F]) \big) \,,

where HC 2n(A)HC_{2n}(A) is cyclic homology of AA over a field of characteristic zero kk and F/(I n+1+[F,F])F/(I^{n+1}+[F,F]) takes a free algebra extension 0→I→F→A→00\to I\to F\to A\to 0 and sends it to the abelian group F/(I n+1+[F,F])F/(I^{n+1}+[F,F]).

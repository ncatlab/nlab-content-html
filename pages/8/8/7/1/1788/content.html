The Su-Schrieffer-Heeger model (SSH) is a simple spin chain-like model for a type of 1-dimensional topological insulator quantum material. In theoretical physics it mainly serves as an instructive toy model example of topological phases of matter, while experimentally it gives an approximate description of the electron band-structure of materials like molecular trans-polyacetylene chains, which was the original motivation of Su, Schrieffer & Heeger 1979.

As a lattice model, SSH has a unit cell with two sites, traditionally denoted “AA” and “BB”, hence with a 2-dimensional (2 “band”) Hilbert space ℋ n≃ℂ 2≃ℂ⟨A,B⟩\mathscr{H}_n \simeq \mathbb{C}^2 \simeq \mathbb{C}\langle A, B\rangle at each site, and the Hamiltonian operator is the sum of

  1. an intracell hopping term between sites AA and BB in the same cell

  2. an extracell hopping term between the BB-site of one cell with the AA-site of the next one.

The parameter space of the model is that of the coefficients of these two terms:

  • v∈ℝv \in \mathbb{R}, the intracell hopping strength,

  • w∈ℝw \in \mathbb{R}, the intercell hopping strength

After Fourier transform, the Bloch Hamiltonian of the SSH model

H=∫ [k]∈S 1H k∈End(∫ k∈S 1ℂ 2) H = \textstyle{\int_{[k] \in S^1}} H_{k} \;\in\; End\Big( \textstyle{\int_{k \in S^1}} \mathbb{C}^2 \Big)

is given (up to arbitrary energy shift and unitary transformation) by (cf. Asbóth, Oroszlány & Pályi 2016 (1.18), Batra & Sheet 2020):

(1)[k]↦H [k]≔(v+w⋅cos(k))σ x+(w⋅sin(k))σ y, [k] \mapsto H_{[k]} \coloneqq \big( v + w \cdot cos(k) \big) \sigma_x + \big( w \cdot sin(k) \big) \sigma_y \,,

where σ (−)∈End(ℂ 2)\sigma_{(-)} \in End(\mathbb{C}^2) denotes the Pauli matrices and

v,w∈ℝ >0 v, w \in \mathbb{R}_{\gt 0}

are the two parameters of the model.

To note here that these Bloch Hamiltonians H [k]H_{[k]}

  1. have no contribution of the Pauli matrix σ z\sigma_z, which more abstractly means that they satisfy “chiral symmetry” in the sense that

    (2)σ zH [k]σ z=−H [k], \sigma_z H_{[k]} \sigma_z = - H_{[k]} \,,

  2. generically have two eigenvalues of opposite sign (labeling the valence band of negative energy and the conduction band of positive energy, respectively), except when H [k]=0H_{[k]} = 0, where the energy gap between these eigenvalues closes, which happens for v=wv = w.

Hence for v=wv = w the SSH model describes an ordinary conductor and for v≠wv \neq w an insulator.

In the latter case there are still two qualitatively different regimes: When v<wv \lt w, then the Bloch Hamiltonian (1) regarded as a map to the subspace of gapped and chiral (2) 2-band Hamiltonians

H (−):S 1⟶End(ℂ 2) gap,chr≔{H∈End(ℂ 2)|H †=H,σ zHσ z=−H Eig >0(H)≃ℂ,Eig <0(H)≃ℂ}≃ℝ 2∖{0}≃hmptyS 1 H_{(-)} \colon S^1 \longrightarrow End(\mathbb{C}^2)_{gap, chr} \coloneqq \left\{ H \in End(\mathbb{C}^2) \,\bigg\vert\, \substack{ H^\dagger = H,\, \sigma_z H \sigma_z = - H \\ Eig_{\gt 0}(H) \simeq \mathbb{C} ,\, Eig_{\lt 0}(H) \simeq \mathbb{C} } \right\} \simeq \mathbb{R}^2 \setminus \{0\} \underset{hmpty}{\simeq} S^1

has a non-trivial homotopy class and hence describes a topological insulator phase, namely it has Hopf degree (“winding number”) equal to 1, while for v>wv \gt w the winding number vanishes, which hence describes an ordinary insulator phase.

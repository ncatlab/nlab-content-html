Quaternionic Ravenel Orientation of Orbi-K-theory and M5 charges

In theoretical physics, Chern phases of topological quantum materials as well as brane charges on 11D supergravity orbifolds have famously been argued to be classified by (equivariant) topological K-theory, or possibly by other stable and complex-oriented cohomology theories, such as elliptic cohomology or stable Cobordism. However, closer inspection has revealed the more fine-grained “fragile” classification in both cases to plausibly be in (equivariant) Cohomotopy, which is the primordial “unstable” and non-abelian generalized cohomology. Coarsening should hence take the latter (fragile) to the former (stable) cohomology along a kind of extraordinary character map. But what then is the role of complex orientation of the stable cohomology theory?

We observe here that (1.) on X submanifolds in the Brillouin torus, and on probe M5-brane submanifolds in 11D spacetime, the cohomotopical phases/charges are factored through the complex/quaternionic Hopf fibration, and (2.) measuring that situation in stable cohomology means exactly to ask for complex/quaternionic orientation, at least in the low-dimensional sense of Ravenel.

Then we give a concrete realization of the canonical complex/quaternionic Ravenel orientation on Sp(1)Sp(1)/Sp(2)Sp(2)-equivariant K-theory, using 2-component complex/quaternionic spinor formalism.


A universal real, complex or quaternionic “orientation” of (vector bundles in) a multiplicative\;generalized cohomology theory is well-known to be a class on 𝕂 P ∞ \mathbb{K}P^\infty which restricts to unity on 𝕂 P 1 \mathbb{K}P^1 , for 𝕂 ∈ { \mathbb{K} \in \{ ℝ \mathbb{R} , ℂ \mathbb{C} , ℍ \mathbb{H} }\}, respectively. Using 2-component 𝕂\mathbb{K}-spinor formalism for 𝕂 ∈ { ℝ , ℂ , ℍ , 𝕆 } \mathbb{K} \in \{\mathbb{R}, \mathbb{C},\mathbb{H}, \mathbb{O}\} , known from higher dimensional supergravity, we give a unified construction of canonical (3dim ℝ(𝕂)−2)(3 dim_{\mathbb{R}}(\mathbb{K})-2)-dimensional 𝕂\mathbb{K}-orientations of complex K-theory, equivariant with respect to finite subgroups of the spin groups U(𝕂,2)U(\mathbb{K},2). We close with an outlook on application to physics, where these orientations serve as extraordinary character maps from unstable equivariant Cohomotopy to equivariant\;complex K-theory, both for 𝕂\mathbb{K}-Chern phases of crystalline topological quantum materials, as well as for proper flux quantized charges on orbifold M-branes.

Let XX be a compact Hausdorff space. By a vector bundle (over XX) we mean a topological complex vector bundle.

Let XX be equipped with an involution σ:X→X\sigma \,\colon\, X \to X (a homeomorphism such that σ∘σ=Id X\sigma \circ \sigma = Id_X is the identity function).

Definition

A σ\sigma-quadratic form on a topological complex vector bundle 𝒱\mathscr{V} over XX is:

  • a morphism of complex topological bundles from the tensor product of 𝒱\mathscr{V} with the pullback of itself along σ\sigma to the trivial complex line bundle:

    g:𝒱⊗σ *𝒱⟶ℂ̲ g \,\colon\, \mathscr{V} \otimes \sigma^\ast \mathscr{V} \longrightarrow \underline{\mathbb{C}}

(notice that fiber-wise this is a bilinear map, not a sesquilinear map)

such that:

  1. gg is non-degenerate, in that its adjunct is an isomorphism

    𝒱 ⟶∼ (σ *𝒱) * v ↦ (v′↦g(v,v′)) \begin{array}{ccl} \mathscr{V} & \overset{ \sim }{\longrightarrow} & \big(\sigma^\ast \mathscr{V}\big)^\ast \\ v &\mapsto& \big(v' \mapsto g(v, v') \big) \end{array}

  2. gg is σ\sigma-symmetric, in that

    v 1∈𝒱 x=(σ *𝒱) σ(x) v 2∈(σ *𝒱) x=𝒱 σ(x)}⊢g x(v 1,v 2)=g σ(x)(v 2,v 1) \left. \begin{array}{l} v_1 \,\in\, \mathscr{V}_x \,=\, (\sigma^\ast \mathscr{V})_{\sigma(x)} \\ v_2 \,\in\, (\sigma^\ast \mathscr{V})_{x} \,=\, \mathscr{V}_{\sigma(x)} \end{array} \right\} \;\;\;\;\;\; \vdash \;\;\;\;\;\; g_x(v_1, v_2) \;=\; g_{\sigma(x)}(v_2, v_1)

A homomorphism of such vector bundles with σ\sigma-quadratic form is a morphism of the underling topological complex vector bundles f:𝒱→𝒱f \,\colon\, \mathscr{V} \to \mathscr{V} over XX, which is an isometry in that g′(f(−),f(−))=g(−,−)g'\big(f(-),f(-)\big) = g(-,-).

Write

QVect ℂ(X,σ) QVect_{\mathbb{C}}(X,\sigma)

for the resulting category.

The direct sum of underlying vector bundles with σ\sigma-quadratic form (Def. ) inherits a σ\sigma-quadratic form in the evident summand-wise way. This operation makes the set of isomorphism classes into a commutative monoid:

(2)(QVect ℂ(X,σ) /∼,⊕)∈CMnd. \big( QVect_{\mathbb{C}}(X,\sigma)_{/\sim}, \oplus \big) \;\;\; \in \;\;\; CMnd.

Definition

The group completion of the monoid (2) is the ℤ/2\mathbb{Z}/2-equivariant Hermtian K-theory of (X,σ)(X, \sigma):

KH(X,σ)≔G(QVect ℂ(X,σ) /∼,⊕). KH(X, \sigma) \;\; \coloneqq \;\; G \big( QVect_{\mathbb{C}}(X,\sigma)_{/\sim}, \oplus \big) \,.

Example

(hyperbolic functor) For 𝒱\mathscr{V} an ordinary complex topological vector bundle over XX, the direct sum with the dual of its pullback along σ\sigma

𝒱↦𝒱⊗(σ *𝒱) * \mathscr{V} \,\mapsto\, \mathscr{V} \otimes (\sigma^\ast\mathscr{V})^\ast

is canonically self-σ\sigma-dual

σ *(𝒱⊗(σ *𝒱) *) * ≃ 𝒱⊗(σ *𝒱) * \array{ \sigma^\ast \Big( \mathscr{V} \otimes (\sigma^\ast\mathscr{V})^\ast \Big)^\ast &\simeq& \mathscr{V} \otimes (\sigma^\ast\mathscr{V})^\ast }

and carries a canonical σ\sigma-quadratic form (Def. ), given componentwise by the evaluation map ev:𝒱⊗𝒱 *→ℂ̲ev \,\colon\,\mathscr{V} \otimes \mathscr{V}^\ast \to \underline{\mathbb{C}}.

This construction extends to an additive functor from the core groupoid of Vect ℂ ( X ) Vect_{\mathbb{C}}(X) to QVect ℂ(X,σ)QVect_{\mathbb{C}}(X,\sigma), called the hyperbolic functor, and hence define a group homomorphism from the ordinary topological K-theory-group of XX to its topological Hermtian K-theory (?):

K(X)→KH(X,σ). K(X) \to KH(X,\sigma) \,.

Proposition

There is an isomorphism

KH(X,σ)≃KR(X,σ) KH(X,\sigma) \,\simeq\, KR(X,\sigma)

between the ℤ/2\mathbb{Z}/2-equivariant topological Hermitian K-theory of Def. and the KR-theory of the real space (X,σ)(X,\sigma).

Proof

To define a comparison map

KH(X,σ)⟶KR(X,σ) KH(X,\sigma) \longrightarrow KR(X,\sigma)

given any representative (𝒱,g)(\mathscr{V}, g), we may choose (see here) a Hermitian inner product of vector bundles

⟨⋅|⋅⟩:𝒱⊗𝒱→ℂ̲. \langle \cdot \vert \cdot \rangle \;\colon\; \mathscr{V} \otimes \mathscr{V} \to \underline{\mathbb{C}} \,.

Now since both g(−,−)g(-,-) and ⟨−|−⟩\langle-\vert-\rangle are non-degenerate, with adjunct bijections

g˜:𝒱→(σ *𝒱) *,h˜:𝒱→𝒱 *, \tilde g \,\colon\, \mathscr{V} \to (\sigma^\ast\mathscr{V})^\ast \,, \;\;\;\;\;\; \tilde h \,\colon\, \mathscr{V} \to \mathscr{V}^\ast \,,

we obtain an anti-linear isomorphism

τ≔σ *(h˜) −1∘g˜:𝒱→σ *𝒱 \tau \,\coloneqq\, \sigma^\ast(\tilde h)^{-1} \circ \tilde g \;\colon\; \mathscr{V} \to \sigma^\ast\mathscr{V}

such that

g(−,−)=⟨τ(−),−⟩. g(-,-) \;=\; \langle \tau(-) , - \rangle \,.

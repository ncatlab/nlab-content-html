Idea

The notion of enriched groupoids is the generalization to enriched category theory of the notion of groupoids from plain category theory.

Definition

Let (𝒱,×,*)(\mathcal{V}, \times, \ast) be a cartesian monoidal category (serving as an cosmos for enrichment), so that, in particular,

  • the tensor unit is a terminal object *\ast,

  • for each object v∈𝒱v \in \mathcal{V} there is a diagonal morphism

    Δ v:v→v×v. \Delta_v \,\colon\, v \to v \times v \,.

A 𝒱\mathcal{V}-enriched groupoid is a 𝒱\mathcal{V}-enriched category 𝒞\mathcal{C} equipped for X,Y∈Obj(𝒞)X, Y \,\in\, Obj(\mathcal{C}) with morphisms (in 𝒱\mathcal{V}) of the form

inv X,Y:𝒞(X,Y)⟶𝒞(Y,X) inv_{X,Y} \;\colon\; \mathcal{C}(X,Y) \longrightarrow \mathcal{C}(Y,X)

which behave like assigning inverse morphisms in that the composition morphisms of 𝒞\mathcal{C}

comp X,Y,Z:𝒞(X,Y)⊗𝒞(Y,Z)⟶𝒞(Y,Z) comp_{X, Y, Z} \;\colon\; \mathcal{C}(X,Y) \otimes \mathcal{C}(Y,Z) \longrightarrow \mathcal{C}(Y,Z)

satisfy

comp Y,X,Y∘(inv X,Y×id 𝒞(X,Y))∘Δ 𝒞(X,Y)=𝒞(X,Y)⟶*⟶id Y𝒞(Y,Y) comp_{Y,X,Y} \circ \big( inv_{X,Y} \times id_{\mathcal{C}(X,Y)} \big) \circ \Delta_{\mathcal{C}(X,Y)} \;\;=\;\; \mathcal{C}(X,Y) \overset{}{\longrightarrow} \ast \overset{id_Y}{\longrightarrow} \mathcal{C}(Y,Y)

and

comp X,Y,X∘(id 𝒞(X,Y)×inv X,Y)∘Δ 𝒞(X,Y)=𝒞(X,Y)⟶*⟶id Y𝒞(X,X). comp_{X,Y,X} \circ \big( id_{\mathcal{C}(X,Y)} \times inv_{X,Y} \big) \circ \Delta_{\mathcal{C}(X,Y)} \;\;=\;\; \mathcal{C}(X,Y) \overset{}{\longrightarrow} \ast \overset{id_Y}{\longrightarrow} \mathcal{C}(X,X) \,.

Examples

Example

A Set-enriched groupoid is an ordinary groupoid.

Example

The sSet-enriched groupoids are traditionally misnamed simplicial groupoids (following Dwyer & Kan (1984) and similar abuse for simplicial category), see there for more. More pedantically, sSetsSet-enriched groupoids are only those sSet-internal groupoids whose set of objects is constant, cf. Exp. below.

Moreover, fundamental ∞ \infty -groupoids incarnated as sSet-enriched groupoids aka “Dwyer-Kan simplicial groupoids” are (mis-)named Dwyer-Kan loop groupoids.

Example

A 𝒱\mathcal{V}-enriched groupoid with a single object is equivalently the delooping groupoid of a group object internal to 𝒱\mathcal{V}.

For instance, a one-object sSet-groupoid (Exp. ) is the delooping groupoid of a simplicial group.

Example

An internal groupoid in 𝒱\mathcal{V} is equivalently a 𝒱\mathcal{V}-enriched groupoid if its 𝒱\mathcal{V}-object of objects is in the image of the coproduct-preserving functor Set→𝒱Set \to \mathcal{V} (using here the assumption that 𝒱\mathcal{V} is a Bénabou cosmos).

References

The notion of enriched groupoids is folklore, originating in the special case of enrichment over sSet, where it is traditionally discussed, going back to Dwyer & Kan (1980), (1984), in the guise of simplicial objects in Grpd with discrete simplicial set of objects and then referred to (inaccurately) as simplicial groupoids (see there for more).

Later

  • John F. Jardine, §9.3 in: Local homotopy theory, Springer Monographs in Mathematics (2015) [doi:10.1007/978-1-4939-2300-7]

switches to the terminology of enriched groupoids over simplicial sets (but still does not give the definition of enriched groupoids).

References that make the general definition of enriched groupoids explicit:

  • Jacopo Emmenegger, Fabio Pasquali, Giuseppe Rosolini, §3 in: Elementary fibrations of enriched groupoids, Mathematical Structures in Computer Science 31 9 (2021) 958-978 [doi:10.1017/S096012952100030X]

    (discussed as categorical semantics for homotopy type theory)

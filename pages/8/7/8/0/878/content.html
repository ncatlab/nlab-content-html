Definition

A (binary) relation ∼\sim on a set AA is symmetric if any two elements that are related in one order are also related in the other order:

∀(x,y:A),x∼y⇒y∼x\forall (x, y: A),\; x \sim y \;\Rightarrow\; y \sim x

In the language of the 22-poset-with-duals Rel of sets and relations, a relation R:A→AR: A \to A is symmetric if it is contained in its reverse:

R⊆R opR \subseteq R^{op}

In that case, this containment is in fact an equality.

Relation to graphs

A set with a symmetric relation is the same as a loop digraph (V,E,s:E→V,t:E→V)(V, E, s:E \to V, t:E \to V) with a function sym:E→Esym:E \to E such that

  • for every f∈Ef \in E, s(f)= Vt(sym(f))s(f) =_V t(sym(f))
  • for every f∈Ef \in E, t(f)= Vs(sym(f))t(f) =_V s(sym(f))
  • for every f∈Ef \in E, sym(sym(f))= Efsym(sym(f)) =_E f

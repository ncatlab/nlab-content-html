Idea

The orthosymplectic supergroup OSp(N|2p)OSp(N|2p) is the sub-supergroup of the general linear supergroup GL(N|2p)GL(N|2p) on those elements which preserve the canonical graded-symmetric bilinear form on ‚Ñù N|2p\mathbb{R}^{N|2p}, i.e. the form which is the canonical symmetric bilinear form on the even elements ‚Ñù N\mathbb{R}^{N}, is the canonical symplectic form on the odd elements in ‚Ñù 0|2p\mathbb{R}^{0|2p} and is zero on mixed pairs of elements.

The corresponding super Lie algebras are called the orthosymplectic Lie algebras ùî¨ùî∞ùî≠(N|2p)\mathfrak{osp}(N|2p). Over a field of characteristic zero these constitute the infinite BB- and DD-series in the classification of simple super Lie algebras. They are closely related to superconformal symmetry (e.g. D‚ÄôAuria-Ferrara-Lledo-Varadarajan 00), see at supersymmetry ‚Äì Classification ‚Äì Superconformal symmetry.

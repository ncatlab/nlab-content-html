Idea of a situs

A situs is a notion of generalised topological space, and defined as a simplicial object of the category of filters on sets or, equivalently, the category of finitely additive measures taking values 0 and 1 only, i.e. a simplicial set equipped, for each n‚â•0n\geq 0, with a filter, equiv. such (not quite) a measure, on the set of nn-simplices such that under any face or degeneration map the preimage of a large set is large. We denote the category of situses by s·ãã. Most of what we say below would also apply to the category sMsM of simplicial sets with finitely additive measures obtained by dropping the restriction that the measure takes only two values. With appropriate definitions, s·ãã is a full subcategory of sMsM.

Intuitively, these filters (measures) are viewed as additional structure of topological nature on a simplicial set (‚Äúthe situs structure on a simplicial set‚Äù) giving a precise meaning to the phrase ‚Äúa simplex is sufficiently small‚Äù: by definition, we say ‚Äúa property holds for all small enough nn-simplices‚Äù iff it holds ‚Äúalmost everywhere‚Äù according to the filter (measure), i.e. on a set in the filter, or, resp., on a set of full measure. We refer to sets in the filter, resp., sets of full measure, as neighbourhoods. In particular, the situs structure ‚Äúon a set‚Äù (i.e. on the simplicial set represented by the set) allows one to talk about an n-tuple of points being ‚Äúsufficiently near each other‚Äù for n‚â•2n\ge 2. This extends to simplicial language the standard intuition of topology available for pairs of points (n=2): given a topological structure on a set, the precise meaning of the phrase ‚Äúa property P x 0(x)P_{x_0}(x) holds for all points xx sufficiently close to a given point x 0x_0‚Äù is that the property holds on a neighbourhood of x 0x_0; intuitively, the pair (x 0,x)(x_0,x) with xx near x 0x_0 is thought of as small (a small simplex) because xx approximates x 0x_0 up to a small error.

Situses generalise metric and topological spaces, filters, and simplicial sets, and the concept is designed to be flexible enough to formulate categorically a number of standard basic elementary definitions in various fields, e.g. in analysis, limit, (uniform) continuity and convergence, equicontinuity of sequences of functions; in algebraic topology, being locally trivial and geometric realisation; in geometry, quasi-isomorphism; in model theory, stability and simplicity and several Shelah‚Äôs dividing lines, e.g. NIP, NOP, NSOP, NSOP iNSOP_i, NTP iNTP_i, NATPNATP, NFCPNFCP, of a theory.

No homotopy theory for situses has been developed, although the naive definition of an interval object (namely, the simplicial set represented by the linear order [0,1][0,1] equipped with some situs structure based on the metric/topology) leads to a directed (not symmetric) notion of homotopy, and in s·ãã there is a diagram chasing reformulation of local triviality (becoming trivial after a certain base-change/pullback).

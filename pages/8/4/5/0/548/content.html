Idea

The notion of 2-group is a vertical categorification of the notion of group.

It is the special case of an n-group for n=2n=2, equivalently an ∞-group which is 1-truncated. Under the looping and delooping-equivalence, 2-groups are equivalent to pointed connected homotopy 2-types.

Somewhat more precisely, a 22-group is a group object in the (2,1)-category of groupoids. Equivalently, it is a monoidal groupoid in which the tensor product with any object has an inverse up to isomorphism. Also equivalently, by the looping and delooping-equivalence, it is a pointed 2-groupoid with a single equivalence class of objects.

Like other notions of higher category theory, 22-groups come in weak and strict forms, depending on how you interpret the above.

Strict 22-groups

The earliest version studied is that of strict 2-groups.

A strict 22-group consists of:

  • a collection of group homomorphisms of the form

    C 1→s,tC 0→iC 1 C_1 \stackrel{s,t}{\to} C_0 \stackrel{i}{\to} C_1

    such that the composites s⋅is\cdot i and t⋅it\cdot i are the identity morphisms on C 0C_0, and such that, writing C 1× t,sC 1C_1 \times_{t,s} C_1 for the pullback,

    C 1× t,sC 1 → C 1 ↓ ↓ t C 1 →s C 0 \array{ C_1 \times_{t,s} C_1 &\to& C_1 \\ \downarrow && \downarrow^{t} \\ C_1 &\stackrel{s}{\to}& C_0 }

    there is, in addition, a homomorphism

    C 1× t,sC 1→compC 1 C_1 \times_{t,s} C_1 \stackrel{comp}{\to} C_1

    “respecting ss and tt”;

  • such that the composition compcomp is associative and unital with respect to ii “in the obvious way”.

See strict 2-group for further discussion and examples.

Weak 22-groups

A weak 22-group, or simply 22-group, is a (weak) monoidal category where every morphism is invertible and such that:

  • given any object xx, there exists an object x −1x^{-1} such that the monoidal products x⊗x −1x \otimes x^{-1} and x −1⊗xx^{-1} \otimes x are each isomorphic to the monoidal unit 11.

A coherent 22-group is a monoidal category where every morphism is invertible and equipped with:

  • for each object xx a specific object x −1x^{-1} and specific isomorphisms from x⊗x −1x \otimes x^{-1} and x −1⊗xx^{-1} \otimes x to 11 which form an adjoint equivalence.

A theorem in HDA V (see references) shows that every weak 22-group may be made coherent. For purposes of internalization, one probably wants to use the coherent version.

Definition

The (2,1)-category 2Grp2Grp of 2-groups is equivalently

  • the full sub-(2,1)-category of that of monoidal categories and strong monoidal functors on those that are groupoids and whose tensor product has weak inverses for each object;

  • the full sub-(∞,1)-category of that of ∞-groups on the 1-truncated objects;

  • the full sub-(∞,1)-category of that of group objects in ∞Grpd on the 1-truncated objects;

    ∞Grpd 1≤•≤2 */↪∞Grpd */ \infty Grpd_{1 \leq \bullet \leq 2}^{*/} \hookrightarrow \infty Grpd^{*/}

Properties

Presentation by crossed modules

By the discussion there, every ∞-group has a presentation by a simplicial group. More precisely, the (∞,1)-category, ∞Grp\infty Grp, is presented by the model structure on simplicial groups (for instance under simplicial localization)

∞Grpd≃L WGrp Δ op. \infty Grpd \simeq L_W Grp^{\Delta^{op}} \,.

Moreover, if G∈Grp Δ opG \in Grp^{\Delta^{op}} is an n-group, then it is equivalent to a n-coskeletal simplicial group. For n=2n = 2 one finds that these are naturally identified with crossed modules of groups (see there for more details).

In conclusion, this means that

Proposition

The (2,1)-category 2Grp2Grp of 2-groups is equivalent to the simplicial localization of the category with weak equivalences whose

  • objects are crossed modules

  • morphisms are homomorphisms of crossed modules;

  • weak equivalences are those morphisms of crossed modules which correspond to weak homotopy equivalences of the corresponding simplicial groups.

A straightforward analysis shows that

Proposition

For (G 1→δG 0,G 0→αAut(G 1))(G_1 \stackrel{\delta}{\to} G_0, G_0 \stackrel{\alpha}{\to} Aut(G_1)) a crossed module, the homotopy groups of the corresponding 2-group/simplicial group are

  • π 0=G 0/im(δ)\pi_0 = G_0 / im(\delta) (the quotient of G 0G_0 by the image of δ\delta, which is necessarily a normal subgroup of G 0G_0);

  • π 1=ker(δ)\pi_1 = ker(\delta) (the kernel of δ\delta).

Accordingly, a weak equivalence of crossed modules f:G→Hf : G \to H is a morphism of crossed modules which induces an isomorphism of kernel and cokernel of δ G\delta_G with that of δ H\delta_H.

Similar statements hold for 2-groups with extra structure. For instance the (2,1)(2,1)-category Smooth2GrpSmooth2Grp of smooth 2-groups is equivalent to the simplicial localization of the category whose

  • objects are sheaves of crossed modules on CartSp smooth{}_{smooth};

  • weak equivalences are those morphisms of sheaves of crossed modules which on every stalk induce weak equivalences of crossed modules as above.

(See the discussion at Smooth∞Grpd for more on this.)

Equivalences of 2-groups

We discuss some weak equivalences in the category with weak equivalences of crossed modules and crossed module homomorphisms, which presents 2Grp2Grp by the discussion above.

From inclusions of normal subgroups

Let GG be a group and N↪GN \hookrightarrow G the inclusion of a normal subgroup. Equipped with the canonical action of GG on NN by conjugation, this inclusion constitutes a crossed module. There is a canonical morphism of crossed modules from (N↪G)(N \hookrightarrow G) to (1→G/N)(1 \to G/N), hence to the ordinary quotient group, regarded as a crossed module.

Observation

The morphism (N↪G)→G/N(N \hookrightarrow G) \to G/N is a weak equivalence of crossed modules, prop. . Accordingly, it presents an equivalence of 2-groups.

Proof

The canonical morphism in question is given by the commuting diagram of groups

N →f 1 1 ↓ ↓ G →f 0 G/N. \array{ N &\stackrel{f_1}{\to}& 1 \\ \downarrow && \downarrow \\ G &\stackrel{f_0}{\to}& G/N } \,.

By prop. we need to check that this induces an isomorphism on the kernel and cokernel of the vertical morphisms.

The kernel of the left vertical morphism is the trivial group, because N↪GN \hookrightarrow G is an inclusion, by definition. Clearly also the kernel of the right vertical morphisms is the trivial group. Hence f 1f_1 restricted to the kernels is the unique morphism from the trivial group to itself, hence is an isomrphism.

Moreover, the cokernel of the left vertical morphism is of course the quotient G/NG/N and f 0f_0, being the quotient map, is manifestly an isomorphism on cokernels.

This class of weak equivalence plays an important role as constituting 2-anafunctors that exhibit long fiber sequence extensions of short exact sequences of central extensions of groups.

Observation

Let A→G^A \to \hat G be the inclusion of a central subgroup, exhibiting a central extension A→G^→GA \to \hat G \to G with G:=G^/AG := \hat G/A. Then this short exact sequence of groups extends to a long fiber sequence of 2-groups

A→G^→G→BA→BG^→BG→B 2A, A \to \hat G \to G \to \mathbf{B}A \to \mathbf{B}\hat G \to \mathbf{B}G \to \mathbf{B}^2 A \,,

where BA\mathbf{B}A denotes the 2-group given by the crossed module (A→1)(A \to 1), and similarly for the other cases.

Here the connecting homomorphism G→BAG \to \mathbf{B}A is presented in the category of crossed modules by a zig-zag / anafunctor whose left leg is the above weak equivalence:

(1→G)←≃(A→G^)→(A→1). (1 \to G) \stackrel{\simeq}{\leftarrow} (A \to \hat G) \to (A \to 1) \,.
Example

For smooth 2-groups, useful examples of the above are smooth refinements of various universal characteristic classes:

  • the second Stiefel-Whitney class

    w 2:BSO→B 2ℤ 2 w_2 : \mathbf{B}SO \to \mathbf{B}^2\mathbb{Z}_2

    is induced this way from the central extension ℤ 2→Spin→SO\mathbb{Z}_2 \to Spin \to SO of the special orthogonal group by the spin group;

  • the first Chern class

    c 1:BU(1)→B 2ℤ c_1 : \mathbf{B}U(1) \to \mathbf{B}^2 \mathbb{Z}

    induced from the central extension ℤ→ℝ→U(1)\mathbb{Z} \to \mathbb{R} \to U(1).

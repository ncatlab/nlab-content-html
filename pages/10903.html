Idea

An indexed monoidal (‚àû,1)(\infty,1)-category is the (‚àû,1)-categorical version of an indexed monoidal category. That is, it consists of a ‚Äúbase‚Äù (‚àû,1)(\infty,1)-category ùíû\mathcal{C} together with, for each X‚ààùíûX\in \mathcal{C}, a monoidal (‚àû,1)-category Mod(X)Mod(X) varying functorially with XX. In one of the fundamental examples, ùíû\mathcal{C} is the (‚àû,1)(\infty,1)-category of ‚àû-groupoids (‚Äúspaces‚Äù), while Mod(X)Mod(X) is that of parametrized spectra.

Indexed monoidal (‚àû,1)(\infty,1)-categories are conjectured to be the semantics of linear homotopy type theory.

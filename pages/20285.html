topology - countability axioms

Properties

  • metrisable: the topology is induced by a metric.

  • second-countable: there is a countable base of the topology.

  • \sigma-locally discrete base: the topology of XX is generated by a \sigma -locally discrete base.

  • \sigma-locally finite base: the topology of XX is generated by a countably locally finite base.

  • separable: there is a countable dense subset.

  • Lindelf: every open cover has a countable sub-cover.

  • weakly Lindelf: every open cover has a countable subcollection the union of which is dense.

  • countable choice: the natural numbers is a projective object in Set.

  • metacompact: every open cover has a point-finite open refinement.

  • first-countable: every point has a countable neighborhood base

  • Frechet-Uryson space: the closure of a set AA consists precisely of all limit points of sequences in AA

  • sequential topological space: a set AA is closed if it contains all limit points of sequences in AA

  • countably tight: for each subset AA and each point xAx\in \overline A there is a countable subset DAD\subseteq A such that xDx\in \overline D.

Implications

  • a metric space has a \sigma -locally discrete base

  • Nagata-Smirnov metrization theorem

  • a second-countable space has a \sigma -locally finite base: take the the collection of singeltons of all elements of countable cover of XX.

  • second-countable spaces are separable: use the axiom of countable choice to choose a point in each set of countable cover.

  • second-countable spaces are Lindelf.

  • weakly Lindelf spaces with countably locally finite base are second countable.

  • separable metacompact spaces are Lindelf.

  • separable spaces are weakly Lindelf: given a countable dense subset and an open cover choose for each point of the subset an open from the cover.

  • Lindelf spaces are trivially also weakly Lindelf.

  • a space with a \sigma-locally finite base is first countable: obviously, every point is contained in at most countably many sets of a \sigma-locally finite base.

  • a first-countable space is obviously Frchet-Urysohn.

  • a Frchet-Uryson space is obviously sequential.

  • a sequential space is obviously countably tight.

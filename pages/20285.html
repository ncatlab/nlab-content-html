second-countable: there is a countable base of the topology.A second-countable space has a \sigma -locally finite base: take the the collection of singeltons of all elements of countable cover of XX.
\sigma-locally finite base, i.e. XX has a countably locally finite base, e.g. a metrisable topological space by Nagata-Smirnov metrization theorem.second-countable spaces are separable: choose a point in each set of countable cover.
separable: there is a countable dense subset.second-countable spaces are Lindelf
Lindelf: every open cover has a countable sub-cover.weakly Lindelf spaces with countably locally finite base are second countable
weakly Lindelf: every open cover has a countable subcollection the union of which is dense.separable metacompact spaces are Lindelf
countable choice: the natural numbers is a projective object in Set.separable space are Lindelf: given a countable dense subset and an open cover choose for each point of the subset an open from the cover.
metacompact: every open cover has a point-finite open refinement.Lindelf spaces are trivially also weakly Lindelf.

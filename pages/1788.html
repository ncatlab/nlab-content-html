Proposition

Let

  • H\mathbf{H} be an ∞ \infty -topos;

  • G∈Groups(H)G \,\in\, Groups(\mathbf{H}) a group object ( ∞ \infty -group);

  • G↷X∈AAct(H)G \curvearrowright X \,\in\, A Act(\mathbf{H}) an ∞ \infty -action of GG on XX.

If this is an ∞\infty-free action in the sense that all its higher shear maps are (-1)-truncated

then the homotopy quotient is punctually 0-truncated in that every morphism out of a pointed connected object BK\mathbf{B}K with KK inhabited factors through the point:

Proof

Given a morphism BK→X⫽G\mathbf{B}K \to X \sslash G, regard it as a morphism over the terminal object *\ast and then form the following diagram of atlases and their Cech nerves over it:

By the assumptions on KK and on G↷XG \curvearrowright X all the upper horizontal squares have a (-1)-connected morphism on the left and a (-1)-truncated morphism on the right. Since connected/truncated morphisms for (infinity,1)-categories of (infinity,1)-presheaves are detected objectwise, this means that the entire square diagram of Cech nerve simplicial objects has a (-1)-connected morphism on the left and a (-1)-truncated morphism in the right. Therefore, the (n-connected, n-truncated) factorization system there exist compatible dashed lifts filling all the upper squares. But then passaged back to the (infinity,1)-colimits and using that groupoid objects in an ∞ \infty -topos are effective?, shows that there is a dashed lift in the bottom square. This is the claimed factorization through the point.

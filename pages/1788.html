Prop. Let 𝕋\mathbb{T} be of presheaf type. Then Set[𝕋]≃[𝕋-Mod fp(Set),Set]Set[\mathbb{T}]\simeq [\mathbb{T}\text{-}Mod_{fp}(Set),Set].

Proof. By assumption Set[𝕋]≃[𝒞,Set]Set[\mathbb{T}]\simeq [\mathcal{C}, Set]. Since [𝒞,Set]≃[𝒞^,Set][\mathcal{C},Set]\simeq [\hat{\mathcal{C}}, Set] (by Elephant, p.10) we can assume that 𝒞\mathcal{C} is Cauchy complete.

We have:

  • Ind-𝒞≃Flat(𝒞 op,Set)Ind\text{-}\mathcal{C}\simeq Flat(\mathcal{C}^{op}, Set) (by Elephant, p.723, or Caramello, p.198)

  • (Ind-𝒞) fp≃𝒞(Ind\text{-}\mathcal{C})_{fp}\simeq \mathcal{C} (by Elephant 4.2.2.(iii), p.724)

  • 𝕋-Mod(Set)≃Flat(𝒞 op,Set)\mathbb{T}\text{-}Mod(Set)\simeq Flat(\mathcal{C}^{op}, Set) (from Diaconescu’s theorem).

Whence (Ind-𝒞) fp≃(𝕋-Mod(Set)) fp=𝕋-Mod fp(Set)≃𝒞(Ind\text{-}\mathcal{C})_{fp}\simeq (\mathbb{T}\text{-}Mod (Set))_{fp} =\mathbb{T}\text{-}Mod_{fp}(Set)\simeq \mathcal{C} and, accordingly, [𝒞,Set]]≃[𝕋-Mod fp(Set),Set][\mathcal{C},Set]]\simeq [\mathbb{T}\text{-}Mod_{fp}(Set),Set]. ▪\qed

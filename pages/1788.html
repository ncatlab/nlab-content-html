Remark

The category [C,D][\mathbf{C},\mathbf{D}] is locally small if C\mathbf{C} is small and D\mathbf{D} locally small, in which case we shall often denote it by D C\mathbf{D}^{\mathbf{C}}. The category D C\mathbf{D}^{\mathbf{C}} is small, if additionally D\mathbf{D} is small.

Examples

  • Recall that a [presheaf] on a small category CC is defined to be a [contravariant functor] C→SetC\to \mathbf{Set}, or equivalently a covariant functor C o→SetC^o\to \mathbf{Set}. If XX and YY are presheaves on CC, then a map X→YX\to Y is defined to be a natural transformation X→YX\to Y. The category of presheaves [C o,Set][C^o,\mathbf{Set}] is locally small and we shall denote it by P(C)\mathbf{P}(C).

  • Recall that a [simplicial set] is defined to be a presheaf on the [simplicial category] Δ\Delta. We shall denote the category of simplicial sets P(Δ o)\mathbf{P}(\Delta^o) by SSet\mathbf{SSet}.

  • If KVectK\mathbf{Vect} is the category of KK-vector spaces over a field KK and GG is a group, then the functor category [BG,KVect][\mathbf{B}G,K\mathbf{Vect}] is the category of a KK-linear representations of GG.

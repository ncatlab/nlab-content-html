Lemma

For N∈ℕN \in \mathbb{N} we have that the exponential of NN to the number of cycles of a permutation is, as a class function, equal to the sum of irreducible characters weighted by the number of corresponding semistandard Young tableaux:

N |Cycles(−)|=∑T∈ssYT n(N)χ |T|(−), N^{\left\vert Cycles(-) \right\vert} \;=\; \underset{ T \in ssYT_n(N) }{\sum} \chi^{ \left\vert T \right\vert }(-) \,,

where on the right

  • ssYT n(N)ssYT_n(N) is the set of semistandard Young tableaux TT

    • with nn boxes, and

    • with last (largest) entry NN;

  • |T|∈Part(n)\left\vert T\right\vert \in Part(n) denotes the underlying partition;

  • χ (λ)\chi^{(\lambda)} is the λ\lambdath irreducible character.

This is the statement of Gnedin, Gorin & Kerov 11, Prop. 2.4 (with Prop. 2.1).

Lemma

For e β=N∈{1,2,⋯,n}e^\beta = N \in \{1, 2, \cdots, n\} all the eigenvalues of the Cayley distance kernel e −β⋅d Ce^{- \beta \cdot d_C} on Sym(n)Sym(n) are non-negative.

Proof

We observe that the eigenvalues at these particular values of β\beta appear, up to a positive multiple, as coefficients of monomials in Schur polynomials, and all such coefficients are non-negative integers (this Prop.).

Concretely, for λ\lambda any partition of nn, with corresponding Schur polynomial s λ\s_\lambda and irreducible Specht character χ (λ)\chi^{(\lambda)}, consider the following computation:

s λ(x,x,⋯,x⏟Nargs,0,0,⋯,0⏟n−Nargs) =1n!∑σ∈Sym(n)χ (λ)(σ)⋅(Nx l 1)(Nx l 2)⋯(Nx l |Cycles(σ)|) =(1n!∑σ∈Sym(n)χ (λ)(σ)⋅N |Cycles(σ)|)⋅x n =(χ (λ)(1)n!e ln(N)⋅n⋅EV λ(e −ln(N)⋅d C))⏟∈ℕ⋅x n \begin{aligned} s_{\lambda} \big( \underset{ N \; args }{ \underbrace{ x, x, \cdots, x } } , \underset{ n - N \; args }{ \underbrace{ 0, 0, \cdots, 0 } } \big) & \;=\; \frac{1}{n!} \underset{ \sigma \in Sym(n) }{\sum} \chi^{(\lambda)}(\sigma) \cdot \big( N x^{l_1} \big) \big( N x^{l_2} \big) \cdots \big( N x^{l_{\left\vert Cycles(\sigma)\right\vert}} \big) \\ & \;=\; \left( \frac{1}{n!} \underset{ \sigma \in Sym(n) }{\sum} \chi^{(\lambda)}(\sigma) \cdot N^{ \left\vert Cycles(\sigma) \right\vert } \right) \cdot x^n \\ & \;=\;\; \underset{ \in \, \mathbb{N} }{ \underbrace{ \left( \tfrac {\chi^{(\lambda)}(1)} {n!} e^{ln(N) \cdot n} \cdot EV_\lambda (e^{- ln(N) \cdot d_C}) \right) } } \cdot x^n \end{aligned}

Here we used:

  1. the Frobenius formula for Schur functions (this Prop.);

  2. some evident re-arrangements;

  3. the character formula for the eigenvalues (this Prop).

Idea

Cyclic objects in a category CC are the contravariant functors Œõ op‚ÜíC\Lambda^{\mathrm{op}}\to C, cocyclic objects are the covariant functors Œõ‚ÜíC\Lambda\to C. If AA is an abelian category then the category of AA-presheaves on Œõ\Lambda is usually called (Connes's) category of cyclic modules in AA.

Properties

General

We reiterate the development in Section \ref{structure} in summary form:

Theorem

  1. Aut Œõ([n])=Z/(n+1)Z\Aut_\Lambda([n]) = \mathbf{Z}/(n+1)\mathbf{Z}

  2. Œõ([n],[m])=Œî([n],[m])√óZ/(n+1)Z\Lambda([n],[m]) = \Delta([n],[m])\times \mathbf{Z}/(n+1)\mathbf{Z} (as a set)

  3. Any morphism ff in Œõ([n],[m])\Lambda([n],[m]) can be uniquely written as a composition f=œï‚àògf = \phi\circ g where œï‚ààŒî([n],[m])\phi\in\Delta([n],[m]) and g‚ààAut Œõ([n])g\in\Aut_\Lambda([n]).

The generalizations of this theorem are the starting point of the theory of skew-simplicial sets of Krasausukas or equivalently crossed simplicial groups of Loday and Fiedorowicz.

The cyclic category is a generalized Reedy category, as explained here.

Generalized Reedy model structure

The cycle category is a generalized Reedy category. Hence ‚Äúcyclic spaces‚Äù carry a generalized Reedy model structure.

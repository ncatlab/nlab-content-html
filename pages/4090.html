Idea

The generalization of the notion of flat functor from category theory to (‚àû,1)-category theory.

Definition

As for 1-categorical flat functors, there is a general definition of flat functors that restricts, in the case when finite limits exist, to the condition that these are preserved.

Definition

For Œ∫\kappa a regular cardinal, an (‚àû,1)-functor F:C‚ÜíDF : C \to D is Œ∫\kappa-flat, if, when modeled as a morphism of quasicategories, for any left Kan fibration D‚Ä≤‚ÜíDD' \to D with D‚Ä≤D' a Œ∫\kappa-cofiltered (‚àû,1)-category, the pullback C‚Ä≤:=C√ó DD‚Ä≤C' := C \times_D D' (in sSet) is also Œ∫\kappa-cofiltered.

If Œ∫=œâ\kappa = \omega then we just say FF is flat.

The dual of this is HTT, def. 5.3.2.1, under the name ‚ÄúŒ∫\kappa-right exact‚Äù. But in 1-category theory, the terminology ‚Äúleft/right exact‚Äù is almost universally reserved for the case when finite limits/colimits do exist, so we continue that tradition in the ‚àû\infty-case. We do have:

Proposition

If CC has Œ∫\kappa-small limits, then FF is Œ∫\kappa-flat precisely if it preserves these Œ∫\kappa-small limits.

In particular, if CC has all finite limits, then FF is flat precisely if it preserves these.

The dual of this is HTT, prop. 5.3.2.9.

Properties

Proposition

  1. Œ∫\kappa-flat (‚àû,1)(\infty,1)-functors are closed under composition.

  2. Every (‚àû,1)-equivalence is Œ∫\kappa-flat.

  3. An (‚àû,1)(\infty,1)-functor equivalent (in the (‚àû,1)-category of (‚àû,1)-functors) to a Œ∫\kappa-flat one is itself Œ∫\kappa-flat.

This is HTT, prop. 5.3.2.4.

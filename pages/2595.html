Idea

Since a topos is a cartesian monoidal category, the notion of a unital ring and commutative unital ring can be defined internal to it.

A ringed topos (X,ğ’ª X)(X,\mathcal{O}_{X}) is a topos XX equipped with a choice of ring object ğ’ª\mathcal{O}. If XX is a sheaf topos over a site CC then ğ’ª X\mathcal{O}_X is a sheaf of rings on CC: a structure sheaf.

The notion of ringed topos makes sense for the theory of rings replaced by any Lawvere theory. Moreover, it makes sense for higher toposes such as (âˆ,1)-toposes. This is described at structured (âˆ,1)-topos.

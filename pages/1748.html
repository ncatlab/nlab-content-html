Idea

In many categories CC in which one does homotopy theory, there is a notion of homotopy between morphisms, which is closely related to the 2-morphisms in higher category theory: a homotopy between two morphisms is a way in which they are equivalent.

If we regard such a category as a presentation of an (‚àû,1)(\infty,1)-category, then homotopies f‚àºgf\sim g present the 2-cells f‚áígf\Rightarrow g in the resulting (‚àû,1)(\infty,1)-category.

In (co-)fibration categories

Clearly the concepf of left homotopy in def. only needs part of the model category axioms and thus makes sense more generally in suitable cofibration categories. Dually, the concepf of path ojects in def. makes sense more generally in suitable fibration categories such as categories of fibrant objects in the sense of Brown.

Likewise if there is a cylinder functor, one gets functorially defined cylinder objects, etc.

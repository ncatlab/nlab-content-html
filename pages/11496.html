Idea

Over the complex numbers an elliptic curve (hence a complex torus) may be, and often is, presented as a quotient of the complex plane by a framed lattice, determined by a point œÑ\tau in the upper half plane ùî•\mathfrak{h}. But indeed this data determines a framed elliptic curve, namely the underlying curve Œ£\Sigma together with the ‚Äúedges along which it is glued‚Äù by this construction. The concept of level structure on an elliptic curve is a structure weaker than that of a framing which analogously gives a finite covering. Instead of considering cycles in integral homology, a level nn-structure for natural number BB is given by cycles in homology with coefficients just in the cyclic group ‚Ñ§/n‚Ñ§\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z} (e.g. Hain 08, def. 4.6).

On such level-nn data now acts instead just the group SL 2(‚Ñ§/n‚Ñ§)SL_2(\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z}). The kernel of the projection maps is called the level nn-subgroup (an example of a congruence subgroup)

Properties

Relation to spin structures on elliptic curves

For elliptic curves over the complex numbers (complex oriented pointed tori) the congruence subgroup Œì 0(2)\Gamma_0(2) has the interpretation as being precisely the subgroup of the modular group which preserves one of the ‚ÄúNS-R‚Äù spin structures.

In detail, the elliptic curve Œ£\Sigma, being framed has a canonical spin structure given by the trivial double cover. The space of all spin structures is a torsor over H 1(Œ£,‚Ñ§/2‚Ñ§)‚âÉ[œÄ 1(œÉ),‚Ñ§/2‚Ñ§]‚âÉ[‚Ñ§√ó‚Ñ§,‚Ñ§/2‚Ñ§]‚âÉ(‚Ñ§/2‚Ñ§) 2H^1(\Sigma, \mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}) \simeq [\pi_1(\sigma), \mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}] \simeq [\mathbb{Z} \times \mathbb{Z}, \mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}] \simeq (\mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z})^2. In terms of this action the canonical one is labeled (0,0)(0,0) and then there are three more, labeled (1,0)(1,0), (0,1)(0,1) and (1,1)(1,1). The modular group acts on these via the quotient map p 2:SL 2(‚Ñ§)‚ÜíSL 2(‚Ñ§/2‚Ñ§)p_2 \;\colon\; SL_2(\mathbb{Z}) \to SL_{2}(\mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}). Hence it preserves (0,0)(0,0) and mixes the other three spin structures. Precisely Œì 0(2)\Gamma_0(2) preserves (1,0)(1,0) (and an isomorphic subgroup of course preserves (0,1)(0,1)). The principal congruence subgroup Œì(2)\Gamma(2) is the one which preserves all four spin structures jointly.

In terms of type II string theory the spin structure (1,0)(1,0) is called the ‚ÄúNS-R boundary condition‚Äù for the spinors. The partition function of the type II superstring ‚Äúin the NS-R sector‚Äù is therefore (at best, indeed it is, being the universal Ochanine elliptic genus) a modular form not for the full modular group, but for Œì 0(2)\Gamma_0(2) (Witten 87a, below (13)). For more on this see at Witten genus ‚Äì Modularity ‚Äì For the type II string. The homotopy-theoretic refinement of this involves tmf0(2), see at spin orientation of Ochanine elliptic cohomology.

Topological modular forms with level structure

The construction of topological modular forms (tmf) may be generalized to curves with level structure (Mahowald-Rezk 09). A systematic kind of ‚Äúmodular equivariant elliptic cohomology‚Äù in this sense is discussed in (Hill-Lawson 13).

Idea

A persistent object of a category CC is a functor from a poset, often a product of linear orders, to CC. When the indexing poset is the poset of real numbers or, more generally, a product of copies of the poset of real numbers, the collection of persistent objects of a fixed category CC admits a distance called the interleaving distance, which, informally, measures how isomorphic any two persistent objects are.

Definitions

Let PP be a partially ordered set, seen as a category, and let CC be a category. A PP-persistent object of CC is a functor P→CP \to C. The category of PP-persistent objects of CC is the functor category C PC^P.

Let (ℝ,≤)(\mathbb{R}, \leq) be the poset of real numbers with its standard order, and let CC be a fixed category. Let X:ℝ→CX : \mathbb{R} \to C be a persistent object of CC, and let ϵ≥0∈ℝ\epsilon \geq 0 \in \mathbb{R}. The ϵ\epsilon-shift of XX is the persistent object X[ϵ]:ℝ→CX[\epsilon] : \mathbb{R} \to C such that, for all r∈ℝr \in \mathbb{R} we have X[ϵ](r)=X(r+ϵ)X[\epsilon](r) = X(r+\epsilon), and for all r≤s∈ℝr \leq s \in \mathbb{R}, the structure morphism X[ϵ](r)→X[ϵ](s)X[\epsilon](r) \to X[\epsilon](s) is equal to the structure morphism X(r+ϵ)→X(s+ϵ)X(r+\epsilon) \to X(s+\epsilon). The ϵ\epsilon-shift construction gives a functor (−)[ϵ]:C ℝ→C ℝ(-)[\epsilon] : C^\mathbb{R} \to C^\mathbb{R}. Note that the structure morphisms of XX give a natural transformation η ϵ X:X→X[ϵ]\eta^X_\epsilon : X \to X[\epsilon].

An ϵ\epsilon-interleaving between XX and YY consists of natural transformations f:X→Y[ϵ]f : X \to Y[\epsilon] and g:Y→X[ϵ]g : Y \to X[\epsilon] such that g[ϵ]∘f=η 2ϵ Xg[\epsilon] \circ f = \eta^X_{2\epsilon} and f[ϵ]∘g=η 2ϵ Yf[\epsilon] \circ g = \eta^Y_{2\epsilon}.

As an example, note that a 00-interleaving is precisely an isomorphism in the category C ℝC^\mathbb{R}.

The interleaving distance between persistent objects X,Y∈C ℝX, Y \in C^{\mathbb{R}} is

d I(X,Y)=inf({ϵ≥0:XandYareϵ-interleaved}∪{∞}). d_I(X,Y) = inf\; \big( \big\{\epsilon \geq 0 \;:\; X \;\text{and}\; Y\;\text{are}\; \epsilon\text{-interleaved}\;\big\} \cup \{ \infty \} \big).

Examples

Let (ℝ,≤)(\mathbb{R}, \leq) denote the poset of real numbers with its standard order, and let Vect kVect_k denote the category of vector spaces over a fixed field kk. The ℝ\mathbb{R}-persistent objects of Vect kVect_k are known as persistence modules, and are a central object of study in topological data analysis.

Let n≥2n \geq 2 be a natural number. Persistent objects of the form ℝ n→Vect k\mathbb{R}^n \to Vect_k are known as multiparameter persistence modules.

Let ℝ ≥0\mathbb{R}_{\geq 0} denote the poset of non-negative real numbers with its standard order, and let SetSet denote the category of sets. The ℝ ≥0\mathbb{R}_{\geq 0}-persistent objects of SetSet are known as persistent sets.

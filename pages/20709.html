Idea

By rational cohomology one usually means ordinary cohomology with rational number coefficients, denoted H •(−,ℚ)H^\bullet\big(-, \mathbb{Q}\big).

Hence, with the pertinent conditions on the domain space XX satisfied, its rational cohomology H •(−,ℚ)H^\bullet\big(-, \mathbb{Q}\big) is what is computed by the Cech cohomology or singular cohomology or sheaf cohomology of XX with coefficients in ℚ\mathbb{Q}.

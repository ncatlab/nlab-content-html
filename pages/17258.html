Idea

Kinship is the collection of relations, rules and behaviors concerning alliance and fictive or real consanguinity that occur in the social organisation of the biological reproduction of individuals. Kinship relations play a significant role for individuals as well as for society as a whole.

In particular in pre-industrial societies, systems of marriage and kinship are often cast into rigid and intricate patterns that are a preferred playground for applications of mathematics to anthropology ever since AndrÃ© Weil contributed an algebraic appendix to Claude LÃ©vi-Strauss' groundbreaking Les structures Ã©lÃ©mentaires de la parentÃ©.

Idea

There exist various model category structures which present the homotopy theory of L-∞ algebras.

By definition L-∞ algebras are the ∞-algebras in the category of chain complexes over the Lie operad. As such they carry a model structure on algebras over an operad. There is a strictification which leads equivalently to a model structure on dg-Lie algebras.

A more geometric way is to think of L-∞ algebras as being the tangent spaces to connected smooth ∞-groupoids, hence to the delooping/moduli ∞-stacks BG\mathbf{B}G of smooth ∞-groups, hence as the first order infinitesimal neighbourhood

B𝔤↪BG \mathbf{B}\mathfrak{g} \hookrightarrow \mathbf{B}G

of the essentially unique point *→BG* \to \mathbf{B}G (see at Lie differentiation). Since this is equivalently the first order neighbourhood of the formal neighbourhood (the jets) and the formal neighbourhood can be described purely in terms of associative algebra/coalgebra (see at smooth algebra) many models for L ∞L_\infty-algebras are formulated in terms of such data.

In particular, one succinct way to present L-∞ algebras (as discussed there) is as dg-coalgebras:

Proposition/Definition

An L-∞ algebra (𝔤,[−],[−,−],[−,−,−],⋯)(\mathfrak{g}, [-], [-,-], [-,-,-], \cdots) structure on a graded vector space 𝔤\mathfrak{g} is equivalently a dg-coalgebra structure on the graded-commutative cofree coalgebra over 𝔤\mathfrak{g}.

Conversely, the category of L-∞ algebras (and general “weak” morphisms between them) is the full subcategory of that of counital cocommutative dg-coalgebras on those whose underlying bare graded-commutative coalgebra (forgetting the codifferential) is free

L ∞Alg↪dgCoCAlg. L_\infty Alg \hookrightarrow dgCoCAlg \,.

Accordingly it is of interest to have also model structure on dg-coalgebras or dually (on pro-objects of) dg-algebras which presents L ∞L_\infty-algebras. Prop. below identifies the category L ∞AlgL_\infty Alg as the (full sub-)category of fibrant objects inside such a model structure of “differential graded formal spaces”, which in turn is related by a zig-zag of Quillen equivalence to various other models

Remark/Warning

So we write “L ∞AlgL_\infty Alg” here for the 1-category of L ∞L_\infty-algebras and general morphisms between them, since this is an entry on model category presentations. If we want to refer to the (∞,1)-category of L ∞L_\infty-algebras we here write explicitly “L W(L ∞Alg)L_W( L_\infty Alg)”, referring to the simplicial localization of this 1-category.

Remark/Warning

All gradings in the following are ℤ\mathbb{Z}-gradings, unless explicitly stated otherwise. In terms of the underlying geometry this means that we are dealing with derived geometry (see below the section Simplicial sheaves over comsimplicial formal spaces for details): the algebra elements in positive degree correspond to categorical/simplicial/∞-groupoid/∞-stack-degree, and those in negative degree to the cosimplicial degree of the derived site of cosimplicial formal spaces.

Technically this affects for instance the nature of fibrations: for instance the model structure on dg-Lie algebras below is transferred from a model structure on chain complexes. For unbounded chain complexes this is the “Categorical projective class structure” whose fibrations are the chain maps that are surjective in every degree. This appears for instance in prop. and prop. below.

On the other hand, if one considered chain complexes in non-negative degree (for tangent complexes in “higher but non-derived geometry”), then one would use the Projective structure on chain complexes in non-negative degree. This has as fibrations precisely the chain maps that are surjective in every positive degree. This case is (currently) not discussed in the following.

Remark/Warning

Some of the model structures below are on the category of L ∞L_\infty-algebras with “strict” morphisms between them, namely for those morphisms which are morphisms of algebras over an operad for an L ∞L_\infty-algebra regarded as an algebra over a cofibrant resolution of the Lie operad. We write

L ∞Alg str→L ∞Alg L_\infty Alg_{str} \to L_\infty Alg

for the wide subcategory on the strict L ∞L_\infty-morphisms.

Definition as algebras over an operad

As their name indicates, L-∞ algebras are the homotopy algebras over the Lie operad (in a category of chain complexes). As such, the general theory of model structures on algebras over an operad provides a model category structure on the category of L ∞L_\infty-algebras.

This we discuss here. But there is also a natural identification of L ∞L_\infty-algebras with infinitesimal derived ∞-stacks. For expressing this a host of other, Quillen equivalent model structures are available. These we discuss below in Definitions as formal/infinitesimal ∞-stacks.

By the general discussion at model structure on dg-algebras over an operad, if kk is a field which contains the field of rational numbers, then for every symmetric operad (uncolored) 𝒪\mathcal{O} in the category of chain complexes (unbounded) Ch •(k)Ch_\bullet(k), the free-forgetful adjunction

Alg(𝒪)→U←FCh •(k) Alg(\mathcal{O}) \stackrel{\overset{F}{\leftarrow}}{\underset{U}{\to}} Ch_\bullet(k)

between the algebras over an operad and the underlying chain complexes induces a transferred model structure from the projective unbounded model structure on chain complexes where hence on both sides

  • the weak equivalences are the morphisms that are quasi-isomorphisms on the (underlying) chain complexes;

  • the fibrations are the morphisms that are degreewise surjections on the (underlying) chain complexes.

Hence in particular (F⊢U)(F \vdash U) is Quillen adjunction between these model structures.

(Hinich97, theorem 4.1.1)

So this is in particular true for 𝒪=Lie^\mathcal{O} = \widehat Lie the standard cofibrant resolution of the Lie operad. In this case Alg(Lie^)≃L ∞Alg strAlg(\widehat Lie) \simeq L_\infty Alg_{str} is the category of (unbounded) L ∞L_\infty-algebras (with strict L ∞L_\infty-maps between them as in remark above) and hence is equipped with a transferred model structure this way

L ∞Alg str(k)→U←FCh •(k). L_\infty Alg_{str}(k) \stackrel{\overset{F}{\leftarrow}}{\underset{U}{\to}} Ch_\bullet(k) \,.

Moreover, by the rectification result discussed at model structure on dg-algebras over an operad, the resolution map Lie^→≃Lie\widehat Lie \stackrel{\simeq}{\to} Lie induces a Quillen equivalence

L ∞Alg str(k)→≃dgLieAlg(k) L_\infty Alg_{str}(k) \stackrel{\simeq}{\to} dgLieAlg(k)

with the model structure on dg-Lie algebras, similarly transferred from the model structure on chain complexes.

On dg-Lie algebras

Let kk be a field of characteristic 0.

Definition

Write dgLieAlg k∈CatdgLieAlg_k \in Cat for the category of dg-Lie algebras over kk.

Proposition

The category dgLieAlg kdgLieAlg_k carries a cofibrantly generated model category structure in which

  • fibrations are the degreewise surjective maps;

  • weak equivalences are the quasi-isomorphisms

on the underlying chain complexes. This is the transferred model structure of the corresponding model structure on chain complexes along the forgetful functor to the category of chain complexes.

(Pridham, lemma 3.24)

We call this the model structure on dg-Lie algebras.

Definition

Write

𝒞:dgLieAlg k→dgCoCAlg k \mathcal{C} \;\colon\; dgLieAlg_k \to dgCoCAlg_k

for the functor which sends a dg-Lie algebra (𝔤,d,[−,−])(\mathfrak{g},d,[-,-]) to the dg-coalgebra whose underlying coalgebra is free on the underlying graded vector space 𝔤\mathfrak{g} and whose coderivation is given by

δ:v 1↦dv 1 \delta \colon v_1 \mapsto d v_1
δ:(v 1,v 2)↦[v 1,v 2] \delta \colon (v_1, v_2) \mapsto [v_1, v_2]

and then extended as a coderivation.

(The Chevalley-Eilenberg dg-coalgebra.)

Proposition

The functor from def. has a left adjoint

ℒ:dgCoCAlg k→dgLieAlg k. \mathcal{L} \;\colon\; dgCoCAlg_k \to dgLieAlg_k \,.

(Quillen, App. B6) (Hinich98, 1.2.1, 2.2.5) See also (Pridham, def. 3.23).

Remark

If one thinks of a dg-coalgebra as presenting a a derived formal space, as discuss below then its image under ℒ\mathcal{L}, prop. , may be thought of as its tangent dg-Lie algebra. Therefore ℒ\mathcal{L} is also called the tangent Lie algebra functor.

(Hinich98, 1.2.1)

On dg-coalgebras

Let kk be a field of characteristic 0.

Definition

Write dgCoCAlg k∈CatdgCoCAlg_k \in Cat for the category of co-commutative counital ℤ\mathbb{Z}-graded dg-coalgebras over kk.

Proposition

There exists a model category structure on dgCoCAlg kdgCoCAlg_k for which

  • the cofibrations are the morphisms that are degreewise injections;

  • the weak equivalences are the morphisms ff whose corresponding morphisms of dg-Lie algebras ℒ(f)\mathcal{L}(f), prop. , is a quasi-isomorphism on the underlying chain complexes.

(Hinich98, theorem 3.1) See also (Pridham, lemma 3.25).

We call this the model structure on dg-coalgebras.

Remark/Warning

Beware that the class of weak equivalences in prop. is not that of quasi-isomorphisms on the chain complexes underlying the dg-coalgebras.

But they form a sub-class:

Proposition

The Chevalley-Eilenberg functor

𝒞:dgLieAlg k→dgCoCAlg k \mathcal{C} \;\colon\; dgLieAlg_k \to dgCoCAlg_k

from def. sends quasi-isomorphisms on the chain complexes underlying dg-Lie algebras to quasi-isomorphisms on the chain complexes underlying their Chevalley-Eilenberg dg-coalgebras.

(Lurie, prop. 2.2.6)

Proposition

The pair of adjoint functors

(ℒ⊣𝒞):dgLie k→𝒞←ℒdgCoCAlg k (\mathcal{L} \dashv \mathcal{C}) \;\colon\; dgLie_k \stackrel{\overset{\mathcal{L}}{\leftarrow}}{\underset{\mathcal{C}}{\to}} dgCoCAlg_k

from prop. constitutes a Quillen equivalence between the model structure on dg-Lie algebras, prop. , and the model structure on dg-coalgebras, prop. .

(Hinich98, theorem 3.2)

Remark

Since every object in dgCoCAlg kdgCoCAlg_k is a cofibrant object and every object in dgLie kdgLie_k is a fibrant object, the composite

𝒞ℒ:dgCoCAlg k→dgCocAlg k \mathcal{C}\mathcal{L} \colon dgCoCAlg_k \to dgCocAlg_k

is already its derived functor and the unit

𝔤→𝒞ℒ𝔤 \mathfrak{g} \to \mathcal{C}\mathcal{L}\mathfrak{g}

is a weak equivalence that exhibits 𝒞ℒ𝔤\mathcal{C}\mathcal{L}\mathfrak{g} as a fibrant resolution and moreover, if 𝔤\mathfrak{g} was already fibrant, hence by prop. below an L-∞ algebra, as a strictification of 𝔤\mathfrak{g}: because a dg-Lie algebra is an L ∞L_\infty-algebra in which the Lie bracket satisfies its Jacobi identity strictly (not just up to a homotopy measured by the trinary bracket) and in which the “Jacobiator identity” holds strictly, etc.

Properties

General

We discuss some further properties of the above model category structures.

Proposition

The model category dgFormalSpacedgFormalSpace, def. , is a right proper model category.

This observation has been communicated privately by Jonathan Pridham

Proof

We need to show that the pullback of a weak equivalence ww along a fibration ff is again a weak equivalence. If ww is a fibration, this is automatic, so by factorisation we reduce to the case where ww is a cofibration. Now, every trivial cofibration is SpfSpf of a composition of acyclic small extensions, so we may take ww to be SpfSpf of an acyclic small extension A→BA \to B with kernel II. Then ff is SpfSpf of a quasi-free map A→RA \to R, so the pullback is SpfSpf of R→R/IRR \to R/IR, and IR=I⊗^ ARIR =I\hat{\otimes}_A R, so R→R/IRR \to R/IR is also an acyclic small extension.

Homotopies and derived hom spaces

In any model category we have a notion of homotopy between 1-morphisms. In any category of fibrant objects we still have a notion of right homotopy, given by maps into a path space object. So all of the above model category/fibrant object category structures yield models for homotopies between morphisms of L ∞L_\infty-algebras.

A discussion of path space objects of and hence of right homotopies between L ∞L_\infty-algebras (in the category of def. ) is for instance in (Dolgushev 07, section 5).

More generally, a description of the full derived hom space between two L ∞L_\infty-algebras is obtained via remark from the description of derived hom-spaces in categories of fibrant objects.

Homotopy fiber products

Recognizing homotopy fiber products in any of the model structure above can be a bit subtle. A recognition principle of homotopy fibers over abelian L ∞L_\infty-algebras , hence useful for discussion of ∞-Lie algebra extensions), is described in (Fiorenza-Rogers-Schreiber 13, theorem 3.1.13).

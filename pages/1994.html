Alex asks: Is there any relation between a profunctor and a Crossed Profunctor? Just as one could look at a crossed module as a module with ‚Äútwisted multiplication‚Äù (according to the crossed module page), is there some analogous ‚Äútwisting‚Äù of a profunctor to obtain a crossed profunctor?

Beppe says: no. Actually the name ‚Äúcrossed profunctor‚Äù was not a happy choice. A crossed profunctor is just the normalized version of a profunctor, as a crossed module is the normalization of a groupoid. This extends to a strong biequivalence between the bicategory of crossed modules, crossed profunctors and their morphisms (in Grp) and that of crossed modules, profunctors and their morphisms.

This result holds also in intrinsic settings, say when we consider internal categories in a semi-abelian category.

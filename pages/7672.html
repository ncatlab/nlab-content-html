Idea

The JJ-homomorphism is traditionally a family of group homomorphisms

J i:œÄ i(O(n))‚ü∂œÄ n+i(S n) J_i \;\colon\; \pi_i(O(n)) \longrightarrow \pi_{n+i}(S^n)

from the homotopy groups of (the topological space underlying) the orthogonal group to the homotopy groups of spheres. This refines to a morphism of ‚àû-groups

J:O‚ü∂GL 1(ùïä) J \;\colon\; O \longrightarrow GL_1(\mathbb{S})

from the stable orthogonal group (regarded as a group object in L wheTop‚âÉL_{whe} Top \simeq ‚àûGrpd) to the ‚àû-group of units of the sphere spectrum, regarded as an E-‚àû ring spectrum.

By postcomposition, the delooping of the J-homomorphism

BJ:BO‚ÜíBGL 1(ùïä) B J \;\colon\; B O \to B GL_1(\mathbb{S})

sends real vector bundles to sphere bundles, namely to (‚àû,1)-line bundles with typical fiber the sphere spectrum ùïä\mathbb{S}. See also at Thom space for more on this.

The description of the image of the JJ-homomorphism in the stable homotopy groups of spheres was an important precursor to the development of chromatic homotopy theory, which is used to explain the periodicities seen in the image of the J-homomorphism (see also Lurie 10, remark 8). See also at periodicity theorem.

Definition

A Poisson algebra is

  • a module AA over some field or other commutative ring kk,

  • equipped with the structure ‚ãÖ:A‚äó kA‚ÜíA{\cdot}\colon A \otimes_k A \to A of a commutative associative algebra;

  • and equipped with the bracket [‚àí,‚àí]:A‚äó kA‚ÜíA[-,-]\colon A \otimes_k A \to A of a Lie algebra;

  • such that for every a‚ààAa \in A we have that [a,‚àí]:A‚ÜíA[a,-]\colon A \to A is a derivation of (A,‚ãÖ)(A,\cdot).

The definition makes sense, but is not standardly used, also for the more general case when the product ‚ãÖ\cdot is not necessarily commutative (it is often however taken in the sense of commutative internally to a symmetric monoidal category, say of chain complexes, graded vector spaces or supervector spaces).

Poisson algebras form a category Poiss.

This fits into a central extension of Lie algebras

This is also called the Kostant-Souriau central extension (see Kostant 1970).

Related concepts

  • A polynomial Poisson algebra is one whose underlying commutative algebra is a polynomial algebra.

  • A Poisson manifold is a smooth manifold whose associatve algebra of smooth functions with values in the real line ‚Ñù\mathbb{R} that is equipped with the structure of a Poisson algebra (over ‚Ñù\mathbb{R}).

  • A Poisson n-algebra is a Poisson algebra in chain complexes with a bracket of degree 1‚àín1-n.

    • A Gerstenhaber algebra is a Poisson 2-algebra.

  • A Coisson algebra is essentially a Poisson algebra internal to D-modules.

  • A Jordan‚ÄìLie algebra is a non-associative (quantum) Poisson algebra.

  • The quantomorphism group is the Lie group that integrates the Poisson Lie bracket. Over a symplectic vector space this contains notably the corresponding Heisenberg group.

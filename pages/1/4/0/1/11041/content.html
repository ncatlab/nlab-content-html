Idea

In quantum physics and in particular in the context of quantum computing a linear map between spaces of linear operators (hence an ‚Äúoperator on operators‚Äù) is sometimes called a superoperator.

Notably density matrices representing quantum states are given by linear operators and a quantum operation is a superoperator that preserves the defining properties of density matrices. (Beware that sometimes different or no distinctions between ‚Äúquantum operation‚Äù and ‚Äúsuperoperator‚Äù are made.)

Idea

In solid state physics, many or all anyon-species of (potential) practical interest (such as for topological quantum computation) are thought to be characterized by affine Lie algebras ùî§^ k\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}^k (at some level kk), in that their wavefunctions are, essentially, g^\widehat{g}-conformal blocks and their braiding is described by GG-Chern-Simons theory at level kk (possibly fractional, see at logarithmic CFT here).

If here ùî§=\mathfrak{g} = ùî∞ùî≤ ( 2 ) \mathfrak{su}(2) , then one also speaks of ‚ÄúSU(2)-anyons‚Äù (with varying conventions on capitalization, etc.). With ‚ÄúMajorana anyons‚Äù (k=2k = 2) and ‚ÄúFibonacci anyons‚Äù (k=3k = 3) this class subsumes most or all anyon species which seem to have a realistic chance of existing in nature.

Notably Majorana anyons (in the guise of ‚ÄúMajorana zero modes‚Äù in super/semi-conducting nanowires) are (or were until recently, see arXiv:2106.11840v4, p. 3) at the focus of attention of an intense effort to finally provide a practical proof of principle for the old idea of topological quantum computation (following the plan laid out in Das Sarma, Freedman & Nayak 15). After initial claims had to be retracted in 2021 [doi:10.1038/s41586-021-03373-x, doi:10.5281/zenodo.4587841, doi:10.5281/zenodo.4545812, TU Delft press release] and again in 2022 [doi:10.1038/s41586-022-04704-2] (further claims are under criticism, see e.g. doi:10.5281/zenodo.6344447, doi:10.5281/zenodo.6325378 and the list here) there is a new claim of detection by Nayak 22 & MicrosoftQuantum 22, but see Frolov & Mourik 22a, 22b and Frolov 22.

In any case, Majorana anyons are known not to be universal (not all quantum gates may be approximated with Majorana braiding). The simplest universal ùî∞ùî≤(2)\mathfrak{su}(2)-anyon species are the Fibonacci anyons at level k=3k = 3 (e.g. Simeon 2021).

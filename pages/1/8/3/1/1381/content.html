Idea

A symmetric monoidal (‚àû,1)(\infty,1)-category is

  • an (‚àû,1)-category

  • which is ‚Äú‚àû\infty-tuply monoidal‚Äù, or ‚Äústably monoidal‚Äù.

This means that it is

  • a monoidal (‚àû,1)-category;

  • for which the tensor product is commutative up to infinite coherent homotopy.

This can be understood as a special case of an (‚àû,1)-operad (‚Ä¶to be expanded on‚Ä¶)

Equivalently, a symmetric monoidal (‚àû,1)(\infty,1)-category is a commutative algebra in an (infinity,1)-category in the (infinity,1)-category of (infinity,1)-categories.

Just as many ordinary (‚àû,1)(\infty,1)-categories (particularly, all of those that are locally presentable) can be presented by model categories, many symmetric monoidal (‚àû,1)(\infty,1)-categories can be presented by symmetric monoidal model categories. See for instance NikolausSagave15.

Idea

In an associated bundle with connection the covariant derivative of a section is a measure for how that section fails to be constant with respect to the connection.

Definition

In the context of connections on ‚àû\infty-groupoid principal bundles

We give here a definition of covariant derivatives that is natural in the general context of ‚àû-Chern-Weil theory in that it applies to connections on ‚àû-bundles.

We start by describing this just for ordinary connections on a bundle and demonstrate how this general abstract definition reproduces the traditional definitions found in the literature.

The central statement is: a covariant derivative ‚àáœÉ\nabla \sigma of a section may be identified with the 1-form curvature-component of a Lie algebroid-valued connection, and the curvature equation

‚àá‚àáœÉ=F ‚àáœÉ \nabla \nabla \sigma = F_\nabla \sigma

is the Bianchi identity on its curvature 1-form.

Definition of covariant derivative

Observation

Given a connection ‚àá\nabla on the GG-principal bundle with cocycle gg, there is a unique connection ‚àáœÉ\nabla \sigma on the V//GV//G-groupoid principal bundle that corresponds to a section œÉ\sigma by the above proposition.

Definition

The covariant derivative of a section œÉ\sigma is the 1-form component F ‚àáœÉ 1F_{\nabla \sigma}^1 of the curvature of this groupoid-bundle connection.

This 1-form curvature is literally the measure for the non-flatness of the section . Whereas the 2-form curvature is a measure for the non-flatness of the connection.

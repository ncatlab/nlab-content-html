Idea

A rigid (monoidal) category, also called an autonomous (monoidal) category is a kind of category with duals. In particular, a rigid monoidal category is a category in which every object AA has a dual object A *A^*. Depending on whether these duals exist on the right or the left we call the category left or right rigid. Sometimes rigid refers to categories with duals on both sides.

Definition

In components

A monoidal category is right rigid (or right autonomous) if every object has a right dual. Similarly, a monoidal category is left rigid (or left autonomous) if every object has a left dual. A monoidal category is autonomous (sometimes called rigid) if duals exist on both sides.

Conventions differ regarding which type of duals are which. One convention is as follows: a right dual of an object AA in a monoidal category 𝒞\mathscr{C} is an object A *A^* equipped with evaluation/co-evaluation maps

ev A:A⊗A *→1,coev A:1→A *⊗A \text{ev}_{A} \,\colon\, A\otimes A^*\to 1,\,\, \text{coev}_{A}:1\to A^*\otimes A

where 11 is the tensor unit, subject the the triangle identities (snake diagrams)

(ev A⊗id A)∘(id A⊗coev A)=id A,(\text{ev}_A\otimes \text{id}_A)\circ (\text{id}_A\otimes \text{coev}_{A})=\text{id}_{A},
(id A *⊗ev A)∘(coev A⊗id A *)=id A *.(\text{id}_{A^*}\otimes \text{ev}_{A})\circ ( \text{coev}_{A}\otimes \text{id}_{A^*})=\text{id}_{A^*}.

A left dual is an object A *A^* paired with morphisms ev A:A *⊗A→1\text{ev}_A:A^*\otimes A\to 1 and coev A:1→A⊗A *\text{coev}_A:1\to A\otimes A^* satisfying the dual conditions.

Remarks

Note that this definition only asserts the existence of the dual objects. It does not assert that specific duals have been chosen. However, the choice of duals is unique up to unique isomorphism, justifying reference to ‘the’ dual of an object; in fact, this extends to a contravariant anafunctor *:M→M{}^*\colon M \to M. (Using the axiom of choice to pick duals for every object at once, we can make this into a strict functor.) This functor is defined explicitly by the formula

f *:A *→coev B⊗id A *B *⊗B⊗A *→id B *⊗f⊗id A *B *⊗A⊗A *→id B *⊗ev AB * f^* \,\colon\, A^*\xrightarrow{\text{coev}_B\otimes \text{id}_{A^*}} B^*\otimes B\otimes A^*\xrightarrow{\text{id}_{B^*}\otimes f\otimes \id_{A^*}} B^*\otimes A\otimes A^* \xrightarrow{\text{id}_{B^*}\otimes \text{ev}_A} B^*

This formula is clearer in its string diagram-form

Additionally, the above definition does not assert that the right dual of an object is isomorphic to its left dual: this need not be the case in general, though it is true in a braided monoidal category, and thus automatically also in a symmetric monoidal category (this last fact can be considered an algebraic form of the “Whitney trick” for knots; see this MO discussion).

Left duals and right duals are also isomorphic in a semisimple category. Note that a rigid monoidal category which is also symmetric is sometimes called compact closed, or simply “compact”.

In practice, algebraic geometers are the most frequent users of the term ‘rigid’, and they focus on the symmetric monoidal case, so they ignore the difference between right and left duals.

Examples

Finite-dimensional vector spaces

The canonical example of a rigid category is the category FinDimVect k FinDimVect_k of finite dimensional vector spaces over a field kk. Namely, given a finite-dimensional vector space VV, its dual vector space inthe sense of linear algebra, V *≔Hom k(V,k)V^* \coloneqq\text{Hom}_k(V,k), constitutes its dual object in the category FinDimVect. Explicitely we have the following:

Proposition

Let kk be a field. For every V∈FinDimVect kV\in FinDimVect_k, define the maps

ev V:V⊗V *→k\text{ev}_V: V\otimes V^*\to k
(v,φ)↦φ(v)(v,\varphi)\mapsto \varphi(v)

and

coev V:k→V *⊗V\text{coev}_V \colon k\to V^*\otimes V
x↦x⋅∑ i∈Iφ i⊗v ix\mapsto x\cdot \sum_{i\in I}\varphi_i\otimes v_i

where {v i} i∈I\{v_i\}_{i\in I} is a basis for VV, and φ i\varphi_i is a dual basis. That is, φ i\varphi_i is defined by

φ i(v j)={1 i=j 0 otherwise.\varphi_i(v_j)= \begin{cases} 1 & i=j\\ 0 & \text{otherwise}. \end{cases}

These formulas give well defined Vec k\text{Vec}_k-morphisms. Moreover, the satisfy the triangle identities and hence give Vec k\text{Vec}_k the structure of a (right) rigid category.

Proof

To begin, we verify that the definition of coev\text{coev} is independent of basis. Suppose {v˜ i} i∈I\{\tilde{v}_i\}_{i\in I} is a different choice of basis. There is a change of basis matrix c=(c i,j) (i,j)∈I×Ic=(c_{i,j})_{(i,j)\in I\times I} between them. It is straightforward to see that the dual bases are related by the change of basis matrix c −1c^{-1}. Expanding, we thus find that

=∑ j,k(∑ ic i,j(c −1) k,i)⋅v i⊗φ i.=\sum_{j,k} \left(\sum_{i}c_{i,j}(c^{-1})_{k,i}\right)\cdot v_i\otimes \varphi_i.

The definition of the inverse matrix says that ∑ ic i,j(c −1) k,i={1 j=k 0 otherwise\sum_{i}c_{i,j}(c^{-1})_{k,i}=\begin{cases}1 & j=k \\ 0 &\text{otherwise}\end{cases}. Hence, we conclude that

∑ iv˜ i⊗φ˜ i=∑ iv i⊗φ i,\sum_{i}\tilde{v}_i\otimes \tilde{\varphi}_i=\sum_{i}v_i\otimes \varphi_i,

so coev V\coev_{V} is well defined as desired. We now show that the rigidity diagrams commute. Going around the square, we find that this is equivalent to the condition that 1⊗w=∑ iφ i(w)⊗v i1\otimes w=\sum_{i}\varphi_i(w)\otimes v_i for all w∈Vw\in V. This follows from choosing a basis such that v i=wv_i=w for some ii. Then, φ i(w)⊗v i=w\varphi_i(w)\otimes v_i=w and all the other terms are zero, making the equality obvious.

Tannaka duality

The statement of Tannaka duality for associative algebras says that rigid monoidal categories equipped with a fiber functor are categories of modules over a Hopf algebra.

Free rigid monoidal categories

The inclusion of the full sub-2-category of rigid monoidal categories into the 2-category MonCat of monoidal categories a left adjoint 2-functor LL, making it a reflective sub-2-category with reflector a free construction of rigid monoidal structure from any monoidal category.

Furthermore, the unit of the adjunction is a strong monoidal fully faithful functor.

This means thatany monoidal category CC admits a fully faithful strong monoidal functor C→L(C)C\to L(C) into a rigid monoidal category.

This is Delpeuch (2020), Thm. 1 & 2.

Idea

In the context of factorization systems such as they appear notably in enriched model category one frequently needs to handle iterated lifting problems. In the appendix of (Joyal–Tierney, 06) a symbolic calculus is introduced to facilitate these computations.

A central point of it is to have the statement of prop. below be easily expressible in terms of “division on both sides”-operations.

The calculus

Lifting

Let ℰ\mathcal{E} be a category (locally small).

Notation

For f,g∈Mor(ℰ)f, g \in Mor(\mathcal{E}), write

f⧄g f \,&solb;\, g

if ff has the left lifting property against gg, or equivalently if gg has the right lifting property against ff.

For S∈ℰS \in \mathcal{E} an object, write

f⧄S f \,&solb;\, S

to indicate that for the morphism f:X→Yf : X \to Y the induced hom set morphism

ℰ(f,S):ℰ(Y,S)→ℰ(X,S) \mathcal{E}(f, S) : \mathcal{E}(Y,S) \to \mathcal{E}(X,S)

is surjective, dually for

S⧄f. S \,&solb;\, f \,.

In the case that ℰ\mathcal{E} has a terminal object ** we have equivalently

f⧄S⇔f⧄(S→*) f \,&solb;\, S \;\;\Leftrightarrow\;\; f \,&solb;\, (S \to *)

and if ℰ\mathcal{E} has an initial object ∅\emptyset we have equivalently

S⧄f⇔(∅→S)⧄f. S \,&solb;\, f \;\;\Leftrightarrow \;\; (\emptyset \to S) \,&solb;\, f \,.

Accordingly, for Q⊂Mor(ℰ)Q \subset Mor(\mathcal{E}) write ⧄Q{}^{&solb;}Q and Q ⧄Q^{&solb;} for the class of morphisms with left or right lifting property against all elements of QQ, respectively.

Proposition

If (L⊣R):ℰ→ℱ(L \dashv R) : \mathcal{E} \to \mathcal{F} is a pair of adjoint functors, then

f⧄R(g)⇔L(f)⧄g f \,&solb;\, R(g) \;\; \Leftrightarrow \;\; L(f) \,&solb;\, g
Definition

A pair of classes of morphisms (L,R)(L,R) in ℰ\mathcal{E} is a weak factorization system precisely if

  1. every morphism in ℰ\mathcal{E} factors as the composition of a morphism in LL followed by a morphism in RR;

  2. R=L ⧄R = L^{&solb;} and L= ⧄RL = {}^{&solb;} R.

Tensoring

Let ℰ 1\mathcal{E}_1, ℰ 2\mathcal{E}_2, ℰ 3\mathcal{E}_3 be three categories.

Definition

A functor

⊗:ℰ 1×ℰ 2→ℰ 3 \otimes : \mathcal{E}_1 \times \mathcal{E}_2 \to \mathcal{E}_3

  1. is called divisible on the left if for every A∈ℰ 1A \in \mathcal{E}_1 the functor A⊗(−)A \otimes (-) has a right adjoint, to be denoted

    A\(−):ℰ 3→ℰ 2; A \backslash (-) : \mathcal{E}_3 \to \mathcal{E}_2 \,;

  2. is called divisible on the right if for every A∈ℰ 2A \in \mathcal{E}_2 the functor (−)⊗A(-) \otimes A has a right adjoint, to be denoted

    (−)/A:ℰ 3→ℰ 1; (-)/ A : \mathcal{E}_3 \to \mathcal{E}_1 \,;
Proposition

If ⊗\otimes is divisble on both sides, then there are natural isomorphisms between the collections of morphisms

A⊗B→X A \otimes B \to X

and

B→A\X B \to A\backslash X

and

A→X/B. A \to X / B \,.
Proposition

For every f∈Mor(ℰ 1)f \in Mor(\mathcal{E}_1), g∈Mor(ℰ 2)g \in Mor(\mathcal{E}_2) and X∈ℰ 3X \in \mathcal{E}_3 we have

f⧄(X/g)⇔g⧄(f\X). f \,&solb;\, (X/g) \;\; \Leftrightarrow \;\; g \,&solb;\, (f \backslash X) \,.
Example

If ℰ\mathcal{E} is a closed symmetric monoidal category, then its tensor product functor ⊗:ℰ×ℰ→ℰ\otimes : \mathcal{E} \times \mathcal{E} \to \mathcal{E} is divisible on both sides, the two divisions coincide and are given by the internal hom [−,−]:ℰ op×ℰ→ℰ[-,-] : \mathcal{E}^{op} \times \mathcal{E} \to \mathcal{E}

X/A≃[A,X]≃A\X. X/A \simeq [A,X] \simeq A\backslash X \,.

Pushout-tensoring

Let now ℰ 3\mathcal{E}_3 have finite colimits and let ⊗:ℰ 1×ℰ 2→ℰ 3\otimes : \mathcal{E}_1 \times \mathcal{E}_2 \to \mathcal{E}_3 be a functor.

Definition

for f:A→Bf : A \to B in ℰ 1\mathcal{E}_1 and g:X→Yg : X \to Y in ℰ 2\mathcal{E}_2, write

A⊗Y⨿A⊗XB⊗X⟶B⊗Y A \otimes Y \overset {A \otimes X} {\amalg} B \otimes X \longrightarrow B \otimes Y

for the induced pushout-product morphism, the canonical morphism out of the pushout induced from the commutativity of the diagram

A⊗X → B⊗X ↓ ↓ A⊗Y → B⊗Y. \array{ A \otimes X &\to& B \otimes X \\ \downarrow && \downarrow \\ A \otimes Y &\to& B \otimes Y } \,.
Proposition

The pushout-product extends to a functor

⊗¯:ℰ 1 I×ℰ 2 I→ℰ 3 I, \bar \otimes : \mathcal{E}_1^I \times \mathcal{E}_2^I \to \mathcal{E}_3^I \,,

where C IC^I denotes the arrow category of CC.

Proposition

If in the above situation ℰ 1\mathcal{E}_1 and ℰ 2\mathcal{E}_2 have finite limits and ⊗\otimes is divisble on both sides, def. , then also ⊗¯\bar{\otimes} is divisible on both sides:

  1. for f:A→Bf : A \to B in ℰ 1\mathcal{E}_1 and g:X→Yg : X \to Y in ℰ 3\mathcal{E}_3, the left quotient is

    f\¯g:B\X→B\Y× A\YA\X; f \bar \backslash g \;\colon\; B \backslash X \to B \backslash Y \times_{A \backslash Y} A \backslash X \,;

  2. for f:S→Tf : S \to T in ℰ 2\mathcal{E}_2 and g:X→Yg : X \to Y in ℰ 3\mathcal{E}_3, the right quotient is

    g/¯f:X/T→Y/T× Y/SX/S; g \bar / f \;\colon\; X / T \to Y / T \times_{Y / S} X / S \,;

A key statement now is the following, characterizing the right lifting property again pushout product morphisms:

Proposition

In the above situation, let ℰ 1\mathcal{E}_1, ℰ 2\mathcal{E}_2, ℰ 3\mathcal{E}_3 have all finite limits and colimits. For all u∈Mor(ℰ 1)u \in Mor(\mathcal{E}_1), v∈Mor(ℰ 2)v \in Mor(\mathcal{E}_2), f∈Mor(ℰ 3)f \in Mor(\mathcal{E}_3) we have

(u⊗¯v)⧄f⇔u⧄f/¯v⇔v⧄u\¯f. (u \bar \otimes v) \,&solb;\, f \;\; \Leftrightarrow \;\; u \,&solb;\, f \bar /v \;\; \Leftrightarrow \;\; v \,&solb;\, u \bar \backslash f \,.

Applications

Reedy theory

Let ℰ\mathcal{E} be a model category. Write Δ\Delta for the simplex category and sSet for the category of simplicial sets. In the Reedy model structure on the presheaf category [Δ op,ℰ][\Delta^{op}, \mathcal{E}] the following constructions are central.

Definition

Write

□:sSet×ℰ→[Δ op,ℰ] \Box : sSet \times \mathcal{E} \to [\Delta^{op}, \mathcal{E}]

for the functor given by

(S□X):n↦S n⋅X. (S \Box X) : n \mapsto S_n \cdot X \,.

Write

⊗:[Δ,Set]×[Δ op,ℰ]→ℰ \otimes : [\Delta, Set] \times [\Delta^{op}, \mathcal{E}] \to\mathcal{E}

for the functor given by the coend

S⊗X=∫ n∈ΔS n⋅X n. S \otimes X = \int^{n \in \Delta} S_n \cdot X_n \,.

(Here on the right we have the canonical tensoring of ℰ\mathcal{E} over Set, where S n⋅X≃∐ s∈S nXS_n \cdot X \simeq \coprod_{s \in S_n} X.)

Proposition

The functor □\Box is divisible on both sides.

Let X∈[Δ op,sSet]X \in [\Delta^{op}, sSet]. Then

  • the object ∂Δ[n]\X\partial \Delta[n] \backslash X is the matching object of XX at stage nn;

  • the morphism (∂Δ[n]↪Δ[n])\X(\partial \Delta[n] \hookrightarrow \Delta[n]) \backslash X is the canonical morphism from X nX_n into the nn-matching object.

Let f:X→Yf : X \to Y be a morphism in [Δ op,sSet][\Delta^{op}, sSet]. Then

  • the relative matching morphism of ff at stage nn is

    (∂Δ[n]↪Δ[n])\¯f; (\partial \Delta[n] \hookrightarrow \Delta[n]) \bar \backslash f \,;

  • the object (∂Δ c)⊗X(\partial \Delta^c) \otimes X is the latching object at stage nn;

  • the morphism (∂Δ c→Δ)⊗X(\partial \Delta^c \to \Delta)\otimes X is the canonical morphism out of the latching object into X nX_n;

  • the morphism (∂Δ c→Δ)⊗¯f(\partial \Delta^c \to \Delta) \bar \otimes f is the relative latching morphism of ff.

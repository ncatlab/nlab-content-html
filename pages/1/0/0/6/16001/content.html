Ideas and objectives

The motivation of generalized global analytic geometry is to define analytic spaces associated to rig categories, and to use their relations with classical global analytic spaces in order to properly define Arakelov models of arithmetic varieties in an analytic setting.

Since rig categories are categorical analogs of associative and commutative semi-rings, a proper theory for them can only be given in the setting of âˆ\infty-categories.

One will thus need to define overconvergent global analytic spaces over a given rig âˆ\infty-category, rational domains in them, and compare the associated GG-topology with the usual GG-topology of overconvergent analytic geometry.

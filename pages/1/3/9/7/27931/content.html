This page contains a list of textbooks on category theory, sorted in chronological order.

Introductory texts

Textbooks

Basic category theory

  • Peter Freyd, Abelian Categories – An Introduction to the theory of functors, Harper and Row (1964), Reprints in Theory and Applications of Categories, 3 (2003) &lbrack;tac:tr3, pdf&rbrack;

  • Peter Freyd, The theories of functors and models, in: Proceedings of Symposium on the Theory of Models, North Holland (1965) &lbrack;doi:10.1016/C2013-0-11897-1&rbrack;

  • Barry Mitchell, Theory of categories, Pure and Applied Mathematics 17, Academic Press (1965) &lbrack;ISBN:978-0-12-499250-4&rbrack;

  • Saunders MacLane (notes by Ellis Cooper), Lectures on category theory, Bowdoin Summer School (1969)

  • Peter Hilton (ed.) Category Theory, Homology Theory and Their Applications,

    vol 1: Lecture Notes in Mathematics 86, Springer (1969) &lbrack;doi:10.1007/BFb0079380&rbrack;

    vol 2: Lecture Notes in Mathematics 92, Springer (1969) &lbrack;doi:10.1007/BFb0080761&rbrack;

    vol 3: Lecture Notes in Mathematics 99, Springer (1969) &lbrack;doi:10.1007/BFb0081959&rbrack;

  • Bodo Pareigis, Categories and Functors, Pure and Applied Mathematics 39, Academic Press (1970) &lbrack;doi:10.5282/ubm/epub.7244, pdf&rbrack;

  • Saunders MacLane, Categories for the Working Mathematician, Graduate Texts in Mathematics 5, Springer (1971, second ed. 1997) &lbrack;doi:10.1007/978-1-4757-4721-8&rbrack;

  • John Gray, Formal category theory: adjointness for 2 2 -categories, Lecture Notes in Mathematics, 391, Springer 1974 (doi:10.1007/BFb0061280)

    (formal category theory in the 2-category Cat)

  • Horst Schubert, Categories, Springer (1972) &lbrack;doi:10.1007/978-3-642-65364-3&rbrack;

  • Nicolae Popescu, Liliana Popescu: Theory of categories, Sijthoff & Noordhoff International Publishers (1979) &lbrack;doi:10.1007/978-94-009-9550-5&rbrack;

  • Robert Geroch, Mathematical Physics, University of Chicago Press (1985) &lbrack;ISBN:9780226223063, ark:/13960/t10p8v264&rbrack;

    (introduces categories by examples arising in mathematical physics)

  • Jiří Adámek, Horst Herrlich, George Strecker: Abstract and Concrete Categories – The Joy of Cats, Wiley (1990), reprinted as: Reprints in Theory and Applications of Categories 17 (2006) 1-507 &lbrack;tac:tr17, book webpage, pdf&rbrack;

  • Peter Freyd, Andre Scedrov, Categories, Allegories, Mathematical Library Vol 39, North-Holland (1990) (ISBN 978-0-444-70368-2)

  • Benjamin Pierce, Basic category theory for computer scientists, 1991 20 (publisher)

  • Francis Borceux, Handbook of Categorical Algebra, Encyclopedia of Mathematics and its Applications 50, Cambridge University Press (1994)

  • Roy L. Crole, Categories for types, Cambridge University Press (1994) &lbrack;doi:10.1017/CBO9781139172707&rbrack;

    (categorical semantics of type theory)

  • Michael Barr, Charles Wells, Category theory for computing science, Prentice-Hall International Series in Computer Science (1995); reprinted in: Reprints in Theory and Applications of Categories 22 (2012) 1-538 &lbrack;pdf, tac:tr22&rbrack;

    (aimed at computer science, see computational trilogy)

  • Steve Awodey, Category theory, Oxford University Press (2006, 2010) &lbrack;doi:10.1093/acprof:oso/9780198568612.001.0001, ISBN:9780199237180, pdf&rbrack;

  • Masaki Kashiwara, Pierre Schapira, Categories and Sheaves, 2006

  • F. William Lawvere and Stephen Schanuel, Conceptual Mathematics: A first introduction to categories, 2 nd2^{nd} Edition, Cambridge University Press 2009 (pdf)

  • David Spivak, Category theory for scientists (arXiv:1302.6946)

  • Tom Leinster, Basic Category Theory, 2014

  • Emily Riehl, Category Theory in Context, Dover Publications (2017) &lbrack;ISBN:9780486809038, pdf, webpage&rbrack;

  • Martin Brandenburg, Einführung in die Kategorientheorie, Springer 2017

    (doi:10.1007/978-3-662-53521-9)

  • Brendan Fong, David Spivak, An invitation to applied category theory, 2018 (web, pdf)

  • Chris Heunen, Jamie Vicary, Categories for Quantum Theory, Oxford University Press 2019 (ISBN:9780198739616)

    (emphasis on monoidal category-theory with an eye towards quantum information via dagger-compact categories)

  • Marco Grandis, Category Theory and Applications: A Textbook for Beginners, World Scientific, 2021 (doi:10.1142/12253)

  • Paolo Perrone: Starting Category Theory, World Scientific (2024) [doi:10.1142/13670, arXiv:1912.10642]

On category theory in computer science/programming languages (such as for monads in computer science):

  • Bartosz Milewski (compiled by Igal Tabachnik), Category Theory for Programmers, Blurb (2019) &lbrack;pdf, github, webpage, ISBN:9780464243878&rbrack;

Topos theory

Monographs with focus on topos theory:

  • Peter Johnstone, Topos theory, 1977.

  • Michael Barr, Charles Wells, Toposes, Triples, and Theories, Grundlehren der math. Wissenschaften 278 Springer 1983 (TAC reprints)

    (Here “triple” means“ monad”).

  • Robert Goldblatt, Topoi, the categorial analysis of logic, 1984 (gbnooks)

  • Colin McLarty, Elementary Categories, Elementary Toposes, Oxford University Press 1992 (ISBN:9780198514732)

  • Ieke Moerdijk, Saunders Mac Lane, Sheaves in Geometry and Logic Springer 1992

  • Peter Johnstone, Sketches of an Elephant, vol 1-2, Oxford University Press 2002

Higher category theory

  • Carlos Simpson, Homotopy Theory of Higher Categories (pdf)

  • Project description: higher categorical structures and their applications (pdf)

  • Jacob Lurie, Higher Topos Theory (pdf)

  • Tom Leinster, Higher operads, higher categories, math.CT/0305049

  • Eugenia Cheng, Aaron Lauda, Higher-dimensional categories: an illustrated guide book free online

Towards homotopy theory:

  • Birgit Richter, From categories to homotopy theory, Cambridge Studies in Advanced Mathematics 188, Cambridge University Press 2020 (doi:10.1017/9781108855891, book webpage, pdf)

Foundations

The foundation of category theory in homotopy type theory (see at internal category in homotopy type theory) is discussed in

  • Benedikt Ahrens, Chris Kapulkin, Michael Shulman, Univalent categories and the Rezk completion (arXiv:1303.0584)

  • Thomas Streicher, Introduction to Category Theory and Categorical Logic (2003) &lbrack;pdf, pdf&rbrack;

  • Bodo Pareigis, Category theory (2004) &lbrack;dvi, ps, pdf&rbrack;

  • Tom Leinster, Notes on basic category theory (2007) &lbrack;web&rbrack;

  • David Spivak, Category theory for scientists &lbrack;arXiv:1302.6946&rbrack;

  • Peter Johnstone, Category Theory , Lecture notes taken by David Mehrle, University of Cambridge (2015) &lbrack;pdf&rbrack;

  • André Joyal, Categories

  • Benedikt Ahrens and Kobe Wullaert, Category Theory for Programming (2022) &lbrack;arXiv:2209.01259&rbrack;

Videos

  • The Catsters, Videos on various topics in category theory. (YouTube link)

Videos at an introductory level that cover basic concepts and constructions of category theory. The Catsters are Eugenia Cheng and Simon Willerton (anyone else?).

  • Tom LaGatta, Category theory (video)

Enthusiastic, mostly nontechnical talk given by a probability theorist, made for an audience innocent of any exposure to category theory.

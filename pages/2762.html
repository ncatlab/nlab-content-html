Motivation and Statement

In the context of elliptic cohomology one assigns to every elliptic curve œï\phi over a ring RR a cohomology theory represented by an E-‚àû ring spectrum E œïE_\phi.

Since, by definition, we may identify the elliptic curve œï\phi over RR with a patch œï:SpecR‚Üí‚Ñ≥ 1,1\phi : Spec R \to \mathcal{M}_{1,1} of the moduli stack ‚Ñ≥ 1,1\mathcal{M}_{1,1} of elliptic curves, this assignment

ùí™ Der:œï‚Ü¶E œï \mathcal{O}^{Der} : \phi \mapsto E_\phi

looks like an E-‚àû ring valued structure sheaf on ‚Ñ≥ 1,1\mathcal{M}_{1,1}.

There is a very general theory of higher geometry for generalized spaces with generalized structure sheaves. Using this one may regard the pair

(‚Ñ≥ 1,1,ùí™ Der) (\mathcal{M}_{1,1}, \mathcal{O}^{Der})

as a structured space that is a ‚Äúderived‚Äù Deligne-Mumford stack.

The central theorem about elliptic cohomology of Jacob Lurie, refining the Goerss-Hopkins-Miller theorem says that

the central theorem, first version

The moduli stack ‚Ñ≥ 1,1\mathcal{M}_{1,1} of elliptic curves equipped with the E-‚àû ring-valued structure sheaf ùí™ Der\mathcal{O}^{Der} may be regarded as the derived moduli stack of derived elliptic curves in that for any E-‚àû ring RR the space of derived stack morphisms

(SpecR,R)‚Üí(‚Ñ≥ 1,1,ùí™ Der) (Spec R, R) \to (\mathcal{M}_{1,1}, \mathcal{O}^{Der})

is equivalent to the space of derived elliptic curves over RR.

After we have looked at some concepts in higher geometry a bit more closely below, we will restate this in slightly nicer fashion.

concrete spaces co-probeable by model spaces: structured (‚àû,1)(\infty,1)-toposes

Spaces probeable by ùí¢\mathcal{G} in the above sense can be very general. They need not even have a concrete underlying space , even for general definitions of what that might mean.

(Counter-)Example For ùí¢=\mathcal{G} = Diff, for every n‚àà‚Ñïn \in \mathbb{N} we have the ‚àû-stack Œ© cl n(‚àí)\Omega_{cl}^n(-) (which happens to be an ordinary sheaf) that assigns to each manifold UU the set of closed n-forms on UU. This is important as a generalized space: it is something like the rational version of the Eilenberg-MacLane space K(‚Ñ§,n)K(\mathbb{Z}, n). But at the same time this is a ‚Äúwild‚Äù space that has exotic properties: for instance for n=3n=3 this space has just a single point, just a single curve in it, just a single surface in it, but has many nontrivial probes by 3-dimensional manifolds.

In the classical theory for instance of ringed spaces or diffeological spaces a concrete underlying space is taken to be a topological space. But this in turn is a bit too restrictive for general purposes: a topological space is the same as a localic topos: a category of sheaves on a category of open subsets of a topological space. The obvious generalization of this to higher geometry is: an n-localic (‚àû,1)-topos ùí≥\mathcal{X}.

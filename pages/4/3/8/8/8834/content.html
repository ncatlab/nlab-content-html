Definitions

A sequence of maps A 0‚ÜíA 1‚Üí‚ãØ‚ÜíA nA_0 \to A_1 \to \cdots \to A_n will be called a bracket sequence (a novel phrase for the purposes of this entry) in either of two cases:

  • n=3n = 3 and the composites A 0‚ÜíA 2A_0 \to A_2 and A 1‚ÜíA 3A_1 \to A_3 are nulhomotopic; OR
  • n>3n \gt 3, and (using the preceding notations), there are choices of factor C‚ÜíA 2C\to A_2 and D‚ÜíA 3 D \to A_3 such that the induced sequence Œ£A 0‚ÜíD‚ÜíA 3‚Üí‚ãØ‚ÜíA n \Sigma A_0 \to D \to A_3 \to \cdots \to A_n is a bracket sequence.

In all cases, a bracket sequence leads to a three-map sequence

The collection of all such maps, taking all compatible variations, is the Toda Bracket of the bracket sequence.

  • n>2n \gt 2, and there is a choice of factorization A 1‚ÜíC‚ÜíA 2 A_1 \to C \to A_2 such that the sequence C‚ÜíA 2‚Üí‚ãØ‚ÜíA n C \to A_2 \to \cdots \to A_n is null-bracket.

If the Toda bracket for a bracket sequence includes the trivial map Œ£ m+1A 0‚ÜíA m+3\Sigma^{m+1} A_0 \to A_{m+3} then the sequence is null-bracket.

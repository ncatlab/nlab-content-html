Idea

The category of couplings, sometimes denoted Krn or ProbStoch, is a category of probability spaces and transport plans between them.

This category is naturally a dagger category, as transport plans can be interpreted as going in either direction.

It is used in categorical probability to model, category theoretically, properties which only hold almost surely, i.e. only up to an event of zero probability (zero measure).

Construction

There are two equivalent definitions of the category of couplings, either in terms of couplings, or in terms of equivalence classes of Markov kernels.

In terms of couplings

The category Krn has

  • As objects, standard Borel probability spaces, i.e. triples (X,𝒜,p)(X,\mathcal{A},p) where XX is a set 𝒜\mathcal{A} is a sigma-algebra on XX making (X,𝒜)(X,\mathcal{A}) a standard Borel space, and pp is a probability measure on (X,𝒜)(X,\mathcal{A});

  • as morphisms, couplings between probability spaces.

The identity and composition couplings are described here.

In terms of Markov kernels

The category Krn has

  • As objects, standard Borel probability spaces (see above);

  • as morphisms, equivalence classes of measure-preserving Markov kernels under almost sure equality.

The identity and composition are constructed as in Stoch.

Equivalence of the definitions

Given a measure-preserving Markov kernel k:(X,𝒜,p)→(Y,ℬ,q)k:(X,\mathcal{A},p)\to(Y,\mathcal{B},q), one can define a coupling canonically as follows,

r k(A×B)=∫ Ak(B|x)p(dx) r_k(A\times B) = \int_A k(B|x)\,p(dx)

for all A∈𝒜A\in\mathcal{A} and B∈ℬB\in\mathcal{B}. (See also here.)

Conversely, whenever (X,𝒜,p)(X,\mathcal{A},p) and (Y,𝒜,q)(Y,\mathcal{A},q) are standard Borel, given a coupling rr on (X×Y,𝒜⊗ℬ)(X\times Y,\mathcal{A}\otimes\mathcal{B}) one can form the regular conditional distribution r′:(X,𝒜,p)→(Y,𝒜,q)r':(X,\mathcal{A},p)\to(Y,\mathcal{A},q), which is a measure-preserving kernel, defined up to almost sure equality.

As one can check, these two assignment are mutually inverse, so that the two definitions of Krn give isomorphic categories.

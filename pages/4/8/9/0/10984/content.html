Definition

An adjoint triple F‚ä£G‚ä£HF \dashv G \dashv H is called an ambidextrous adjunction (or sometimes ambijunction, for short) if the left adjoint FF and the right adjoint HH of GG are equivalent F‚âÉHF \simeq H, or more precisely: equipped with a specified equivalence. Sometimes FF is said to be biadjoint to GG (not to be confused with biadjoint in the sense of biadjunction).

In the special case that GG is a fully faithful functor with an ambidextrous adjoint one also speaks of an essential localization (cf. bireflective subcategory).

Properties

Frobenius algebra structure

The monad induced by an ambidextrous adjunction is a Frobenius monoid object in endofunctors. (e.g. Lauda 05, theorem 17), hence a Frobenius monad.

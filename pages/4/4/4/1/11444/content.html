Idea

What is called MRMR cohomology theory or Real cobordism (Landweber 68, Landweber 69) is the ℤ 2\mathbb{Z}_2-equivariant cohomology theory version of complex cobordism MUMU.

There is an evident action of ℤ 2\mathbb{Z}_2 on formal group laws given by negation in the formal group (the inversion involution), and this lifts to an involutive automorphism MU→≃MUMU \stackrel{\simeq}{\to} MU of the spectrum MU. This induces an ℤ 2\mathbb{Z}_2-equivariant spectrum MℝM\mathbb{R}, and real cobordism is the cohomology theory that it represents. This is directly analogous to how complex K-theory KU gives ℤ 2\mathbb{Z}_2-equivariant KR-theory, both are examples of real-oriented cohomology theories.

A modern review in in (Kriz 01, section 2).

Properties

Relation to real manifolds

While π •(Mℝ)\pi_\bullet(M \mathbb{R}) is not the cobordism ring of real manifolds, still every real manifold does give a class in MℝM \mathbb{R} (Hu 99, Kriz 01, p. 13).

E ∞E_\infty-structure

MℝM \mathbb{R} is naturally an E-∞ ring spectrum. (reviewed as Kriz 01, prop. 3.1)

Universal real orientation

In direct analogy with the situation for complex cobordism theory in complex oriented cohomology theory, MℝM \mathbb{R} is the univeral real oriented cohomology theory:

Equivalence classes of real orientations of a ℤ/2ℤ\mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}-equivariant E-∞ ring EE are in bijection to equivalence classes of E-∞ ring homomorphisms

Mℝ⟶E. M \mathbb{R}\longrightarrow E \,.

(Kriz 01, theorem 2.25)

Idea

What is called picture number in string theory is a certain grading originally seen in the quantization of the NSR superstring (Friedan-Martinec-Shenker 86).

The supergeometric meaning was clarified in Belopolsky 97b in terms of an extra grading on differential forms on supermanifolds induced from a choice of integral top-form needed for a notion of integration over supermanifolds, see Catenacci-Grassi-Noja 18

References

The appearance in the quantization of the NSR superstring is due to

  • Daniel Friedan, Emil Martinec, Stephen Shenker, Conformal invariance, supersymmetry and string theory, Nucl. Phys., B271 (1986) 93

A supergeometric interpretation of picture changing operators as part of the geometry of super Riemann surfaces is given in

  • Alexander Belopolsky, New Geometrical Approach to Superstrings (arXiv:hep-th/9703183)

  • Alexander Belopolsky, Picture changing operators in supergeometry and superstring theory (arXiv:hep-th/9706033)

further amplified in

  • Edward Witten, appendix D of Notes On Super Riemann Surfaces And Their Moduli (arXiv:1209.2459)

In this perspective picture number is an extra grading on differential forms on supermanifolds induced from a choice of integral top-form needed to define integration over supermanifolds:

  • Roberto Catenacci, Pietro Grassi, S. Noja, Superstring Field Theory, Superforms and Supergeometry, Journal of Geometry and Physics Volume 148, February 2020, 103559 (arXiv:1807.09563)

    (with an eye towards superstring field theory)

  • Carlo A. Cremonini, Pietro Grassi, Pictures from Super Chern-Simons Theory (arXiv:1907.07152)

    (with an eye towards super Chern-Simons theory)

  • Carlo A. Cremonini, The Geometry of Picture Changing Operators [arXiv:2305.02828]

Review:

  • Pietro Grassi, Integral Forms and Applications, Sestri Levante 2015 (pdf, pdf)

Via pure spinors:

  • Andrei Mikhailov, Dennis Zavaleta, Geometrical framework for picture changing operators in the pure spinor formalism (arXiv:2003.13995)

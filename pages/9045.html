Idea

Pure Hodge structure

A Hodge structure (or pure Hodge structure, for emphasis) is a (bi-)grading structure on cohomology groups ‚Äì called a Hodge decomposition ‚Äì of the kind that is exhibited by the de Rham cohomology/complex-ordinary cohomology of compact K√§hler manifolds, according to the Hodge theorem. A Hodge structure is said to be of weight dd if it behaves like the cohomology of a K√§hler manifold of dimension dd.

If instead of considering a single cohomology group one considers the cohomology groups of a parameterized collection of spaces ‚Äì hence the cohomology sheaves/stacks ‚Äì then one speaks of variation of Hodge structure (of a given weight).

By a central theorem of Hodge theory (recalled as theorem 1 below) the traditional (and original) filtration on the complex cohomology of a K√§hler manifold induced by the harmonic differential forms generalizes to a filtration of the complex-valued ordinary cohomology of any complex analytic space which is simply given by the canonical degree-filtration of the holomorphic de Rham complex.

This means that ordinary differential cohomology in the guise of Deligne cohomology is nothing but the homotopy pullback of a stage of the Hodge filtration along the ‚Äú Chern character ‚Äù map from integral to complex cohomology. (A point of view highlighted for instance in Peters-Steenbrink 08, section 7.2). Viewed this way Hodge structures are filtrations of stages of differential form cycle refinements of Chern characters that appear in the general definition/characterization of differential cohomology, as discussed at differential cohomology hexagon starting around the section de Rham coefficients

This modern point of view is also crucial for instance in the characterization of an intermediate Jacobian (see there) as the subgroup of Deligne cohomology that is in the kernel of the map to Hodge-filtering stage of ordinary cohomology. See at intermediate Jacobian ‚Äì characterization as Hodge-trivial Deligne cohomology.

Mixed Hodge structure

A mixed Hodge structure is a filtration on cohomology groups ‚Äì called a Hodge filtration ‚Äì such that the associated graded object has pure Hodge structure of weight kk in each degree kk. The archetypical example exhibiting this is the cohomology of complex varieties that have singularities (Deligne 71 Deligne 74).

Definition

Historically, Hodge structures originate in the special structure induced on the de Rham cohomology groups of a compact K√§hler manifold by the existence of harmonic differential forms. Below we first discuss this canonical Hodge structure

  • on the cohomology of a K√§hler manifold

But it turns out that this Hodge structure only depends on the natural degree-filtration on the holomorphic de Rham complex and hence more generally there is canonical Hodge structure

  • on the cohomology of a complex analytic space.

Abstracting from here one defines Hodges structures

  • generally on abelian groups.

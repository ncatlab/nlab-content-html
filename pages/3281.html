Idea

A stable model category is a 1-category structure used to present a stable (‚àû,1)-category in analogy to how a general model category encodes a (generally non-stable) (‚àû,1)-category.

A stable model category ùíû\mathcal{C} is

  • such that the loop space object functor Œ©\Omega and the suspension object functor Œ£\Sigma, are inverse equivalences on the homotopy category Ho(C)Ho(C):

Properties

Triangulated homotopy category

The homotopy category Ho(ùíû)Ho(\mathcal{C}) of a stable model category, equipped with the reduced suspension functor Œ£:Ho(ùíû)‚Üí‚âÉHo(ùíû)\Sigma \colon Ho(\mathcal{C})\overset{\simeq}{\to} Ho(\mathcal{C}) is a triangulated category (Hovey 99, section 7).

Relation to stable ‚àû\infty-categories

Stabilization of model categories is a model for the abstractly defined stabilization in (infinity,1)-category theory (Robalo 12, prop. 4.15).

Idea

A nodal singularity of an algebraic curve is one of the form parameterized by the equation xy=0x y = 0. A nodal curve is a curve with a nodal singularity.

(e.g.Hain 08, p. 45)

The nodal cubic

For nodal cubic curve (over some base) is (see at elliptic curve ‚Äì Nodal curves and cuspidal curves for notation and background) the solution to the Weierstrass equation for which the discriminant vanishes, but the modular invariant c 4c_4 does not.

Formal group and height

The formal group associated with a nodal cubic curve is of height 1. Indeed, passing to the point of the nodal curve in ‚Ñ≥ ell¬Ø\mathcal{M}_{\overline{ell}} connects elliptic cohomology (of chromatic level 2) to topological K-theory (of chromatic level 1).

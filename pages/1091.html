Idea

For f:X‚ÜíYf : X \to Y a morphism in a category CC with pullbacks, there is an induced functor

f *:C/Y‚ÜíC/X f^* : C/Y \to C/X

of over-categories. This is the base change morphism. If CC is a topos, then this refines to an essential geometric morphism

(f !‚ä£f *‚ä£f *):C/X‚ÜíC/Y. (f_! \dashv f^* \dashv f_*) : C/X \to C/Y \,.

The dual concept is cobase change.

The concept of base change generalises from this case to other fibred categories.

That we have adjoint functors/adjoint (‚àû,1)-functors (f !‚ä£f *)(f_! \dashv f^*) follows directly from the universal property of the pullback. Proposition

f *f^* is a logical functor. Hence (f *‚ä£f *)(f^* \dashv f_*) is also an atomic geometric morphism.

Notice that the subobject classifier of an over topos H/X\mathbf{H}/X is (p 2:Œ© H√óX‚ÜíX)(p_2 : \Omega_{\mathbf{H}} \times X \to X). This product is preserved by the pullback by which f *f^* acts, hence f *f^* preserves the subobject classifier.

Definition

A (necessarily essential and atomic) geometric morphism of the form (f *‚ä£‚àè f)(f^* \dashv \prod_f) is called the base change geometric morphism along ff.

The right adjoint f *=‚àè ff_* = \prod_f is also called the dependent product relative to ff.

The left adjoint f !=‚àë ff_! = \sum_f is also called the dependent sum relative to ff.

In the case Y=*Y = * is the terminal object, the base change geometric morphism is also called an etale geometric morphism. See there for more details
